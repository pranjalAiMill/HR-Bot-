POLICY 12: EMPLOYEE HELPDESK & POLICY Q&A POLICY

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes the governance framework, service standards, escalation pathways, and operational guidelines for the organization's Employee Helpdesk function. The Employee Helpdesk serves as the primary point of contact for all employee queries relating to HR policies, procedures, benefits, entitlements, workplace concerns, and general employment matters. The goal of the Helpdesk is to deliver accurate, timely, empathetic, and consistent support to every employee across the organization — regardless of their location, seniority, or employment type.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to:
- All employees seeking guidance or support on any HR-related matter.
- All HR personnel operating the Helpdesk function, including HR Business Partners, HR Coordinators, and HR Shared Services staff.
- The AI-powered HR Assistant Bot and any automated response system deployed by the organization to handle first-line employee queries.
- Third-party HR service providers engaged to deliver Helpdesk support under a service-level agreement.

3. POLICY STATEMENT
The organization is committed to ensuring that every employee has straightforward, confidential, and effective access to accurate information about their rights, entitlements, and the organization's policies at all times. No employee should feel uninformed, unsupported, or unable to access help. The Employee Helpdesk exists to make policy accessible, reduce barriers to understanding, and ensure consistent application of HR frameworks across the organization.

4. HELPDESK CHANNELS & ACCESS
Employees may access the Employee Helpdesk through the following channels:
4.1 HR Bot / AI Policy Assistant (First-Line, 24/7)
The organization's AI-powered HR Assistant Bot is available twenty-four (24) hours a day, seven (7) days a week through the intranet portal and mobile HR application. The Bot is trained on all current HR policies and is capable of answering the majority of policy-related queries instantly, including but not limited to:
- Annual leave balances and entitlements.
- Sick leave rules and documentation requirements.
- Maternity, paternity, and parental leave queries.
- Performance management timelines and processes.
- Expense claim guidance.
- Public holiday schedules.
- Payroll queries (basic).
- Onboarding checklists and progress.
- Learning and development program information.
- Remote working eligibility and application process.
- Code of conduct questions.
- Grievance and whistleblowing signposting.
The Bot does not have access to individual employee personal data beyond what is necessary to answer the specific query. All Bot interactions are logged for quality assurance and compliance purposes. Where a query cannot be resolved by the Bot, the employee is automatically prompted to escalate to a human HR representative.

4.2 HR Email Helpdesk
Employees may submit detailed, non-urgent queries via the dedicated HR Helpdesk email address. This channel is monitored during business hours, Monday to Friday. Response time target: within one (1) business day for standard queries, and within four (4) hours for urgent matters flagged as such in the subject line.
4.3 HR Live Chat (Business Hours)
A live chat facility is available through the intranet HR portal during business hours (9:00 AM to 5:30 PM, Monday to Friday). Live chat is staffed by HR Coordinators and is appropriate for short, specific queries that require a human response but are not sensitive enough to warrant a phone call or in-person meeting.
4.4 HR Direct Line (Phone)
A dedicated HR Helpdesk phone line is available for employees who require verbal assistance or whose query is of a sensitive or urgent nature. The HR phone line is staffed during business hours. Voicemail is available outside business hours, and all messages are returned by 12:00 PM on the next business day.
4.5 In-Person HR Surgeries
HR Business Partners host scheduled in-person (or virtual) HR Surgery sessions twice per month in each office location. These sessions provide employees with confidential, face-to-face access to an experienced HR professional for more complex or sensitive discussions. Surgery schedules are published monthly on the intranet.

5. SERVICE LEVEL STANDARDS (SLA)
P1 — Critical:
This includes safety threats, legal deadlines, or payroll emergencies. The response target is within 2 hours, and the resolution target is within 24 hours.
P2 — Urgent:
This includes disciplinary or grievance matters, imminent leave requests, and compliance-related issues. The response target is within 4 hours, and the resolution target is within 3 business days.
P3 — Standard:
This includes policy-related questions, employee benefit queries, and documentation requests. The response target is within 1 business day, and the resolution target is within 5 business days.
P4 — Low:
This includes general information requests, feedback submissions, and clarification queries. The response target is within 2 business days, and the resolution target is within 10 business days.

6. ESCALATION FRAMEWORK
The Helpdesk operates on a four-tier escalation model:
Tier 1 — Self-Service & Automated: The AI HR Bot, intranet FAQs, and the Policy Library handle routine informational queries without human intervention. The Bot resolves an estimated 70–80% of all employee queries at this level.
Tier 2 — HR Coordinator: Human HR Coordinators handle queries that require judgment, access to employee records, or personalization that the Bot cannot provide. Examples include leave balance adjustments, document requests, and joining/exit formalities.
Tier 3 — HR Business Partner: Complex matters involving performance management, disciplinary action, grievances, long-term absence, or legal compliance are escalated to the relevant HR Business Partner assigned to the employee's business unit.
Tier 4 — HR Leadership / Legal Counsel: Matters involving serious misconduct allegations, whistleblowing disclosures, potential litigation, regulatory investigations, or board-level concerns are escalated immediately to the VP of HR and the organization's Legal Counsel.

7. CONFIDENTIALITY
All Helpdesk interactions are treated as strictly confidential. HR staff are bound by professional confidentiality obligations and must not disclose the content of any employee query to colleagues, line managers, or third parties without the express consent of the employee, except where:
- Disclosure is required to investigate a serious concern or formal allegation.
- Disclosure is required by law or a regulatory authority.
- There is an imminent risk to the health or safety of the employee or others.
Employees are encouraged to raise any concern freely, knowing the Helpdesk is a safe, judgment-free, and confidential environment.

8. POLICY Q&A GOVERNANCE
8.1 Policy Library Maintenance
The HR Department maintains a current, accurate, and accessible Policy Library on the intranet. All policies are version-controlled and accompanied by a plain-language FAQ summary that translates complex policy language into everyday terms that all employees can understand.
8.2 Policy Update Process
When any policy is revised, the following process is followed:
Step 1 — The draft revision is reviewed and approved by the relevant HR Policy Owner, Legal Counsel, and senior leadership.
Step 2 — If the revision materially affects employee terms or working conditions, a consultation period of a minimum of fourteen (14) days is provided before the policy takes effect.
Step 3 — The updated policy is published to the intranet Policy Library with a clear version change log and a new effective date.
Step 4 — All employees are notified via internal communications (email, intranet announcement, and manager briefing). The AI Bot knowledge base is updated within forty-eight (48) hours of the effective date.
Step 5 — Where required, employees must re-acknowledge the updated policy through the HRMS.
8.3 Policy Interpretation
Where there is ambiguity in the interpretation of any policy provision, the definitive interpretation rests with the VP of Human Resources, in consultation with Legal Counsel where appropriate. Interpretations that set a new precedent are documented and shared across the HR team to ensure consistency of application.

9. QUALITY ASSURANCE & CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT
A post-interaction satisfaction survey is automatically triggered following every Helpdesk interaction. Employees are asked to rate the quality, accuracy, and helpfulness of the response on a scale of 1 to 5. Survey results are reviewed monthly by the HR Director. Where average satisfaction scores fall below 4.0, a root cause review is conducted and corrective actions are implemented within thirty (30) days.
The HR Bot is reviewed and updated quarterly to ensure accuracy against the current policy library. Any error or inaccuracy identified in a Bot response must be reported to hrhelp@[company].com and will be corrected within forty-eight (48) hours

10. ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES
- Employees: Access the Helpdesk through the appropriate channel, provide accurate and complete information when submitting queries, and treat HR staff with courtesy and respect.
- HR Coordinators: Respond to Tier 2 queries within SLA, accurately record all interactions in the HRMS, and escalate to HR Business Partners when required.
- HR Business Partners: Manage complex, sensitive, and escalated matters, provide consistent policy interpretation, and maintain confidentiality.
- HR Technology Team: Maintain the AI Bot, ensure the knowledge base is current, and resolve technical issues within agreed SLAs.
- VP – Human Resources: Provides final interpretation authority on all policy matters and owns the overall quality and performance of the Helpdesk function.
