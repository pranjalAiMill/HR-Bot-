Employee Helpdesk & HR Support Policy


3.1 Purpose and Organizational Philosophy
3.1.1 Why This Policy Exists
— The Foundation of Effective Employee Support. The Employee Helpdesk Policy establishes the framework through which the Company delivers consistent, timely, and high-quality support to every employee across all functions, locations, and levels of the organization. Where the Code of Conduct governs how employees behave, and the Attendance Policy governs how employees manage their time, this policy governs how the Company responds when employees need assistance — with HR matters, IT issues, administrative queries, facilities concerns, payroll questions, or any other operational need that arises in the normal course of employment. A helpdesk that functions well is not a bureaucratic formality. It is a foundational organizational commitment that directly reflects the Company's values and its genuine care for the people who make the organization work.
— The Organizational Imperative. The Company's ability to operate efficiently, meet its obligations to clients, and retain talented people depends in meaningful part on the ability of its employees to resolve workplace issues quickly, accurately, and without unnecessary disruption to their professional responsibilities. An employee who cannot get a timely answer to a payroll query, who cannot access a critical system, who does not know where to turn with a workplace concern, or who submits a request and hears nothing back is an employee whose trust in the organization is quietly eroded. The Helpdesk exists to prevent that erosion — to be the reliable, responsive, and competent point of support that every employee deserves and that the Company is fully committed to providing.
— The Human Imperative. This policy has been written with a clear recognition that employees who contact the Helpdesk are often doing so at a moment of genuine need — when something has gone wrong, when they are confused about a process, when they are navigating an unfamiliar system, or when they are dealing with a situation that is causing them professional stress or disruption. The Helpdesk is not a gatekeeping function or a bureaucratic obstacle. It is a service function, and every employee who contacts it deserves to be received with patience, respect, and a sincere commitment to helping them find a resolution as efficiently and effectively as possible.
— The Philosophy of Service Excellence. The Employee Helpdesk operates on the philosophy that every interaction between an employee and the Helpdesk is an opportunity to demonstrate the Company's genuine commitment to its people. A Helpdesk interaction handled well — promptly, accurately, empathetically, and with clear follow-through — reinforces the employee's trust in the organization and affirms their sense of being genuinely valued. A Helpdesk interaction handled poorly — with delays, dismissiveness, inaccurate information, or unexplained silence — erodes that trust in ways that compound over time and are difficult to repair. This policy sets the standard for service excellence and holds both the Helpdesk function and everyone who interacts with it to that standard without exception.
— A Living and Evolving Document. Like all enterprise policies, the Employee Helpdesk Policy is a living document that will be reviewed, updated, and refined as the organization grows and changes, as the technology and tools used to support employees evolve, and as practical experience and employee feedback identify areas where the service can be strengthened. Every employee is encouraged to engage with this policy not as a formality but as a genuine and useful guide to understanding what support is available to them, how to access it, and what they can reasonably expect when they do.

3.2 Scope and Applicability
3.2.1 Who This Policy Applies To
— Universal Application Without Exception. This policy applies universally and without exception to every individual engaged with the Company in any employment or engagement capacity, and equally to every member of the Helpdesk team who provides support under this framework. There are no exemptions based on seniority, tenure, professional standing, department, business unit, or geographic location. Every employee is entitled to access the Helpdesk and receive a professional, timely, and respectful response, and every Helpdesk agent is held to the full conduct and service standards described in this policy regardless of the nature or volume of the request they are handling.
— All Categories of Engagement Covered. This policy covers permanent full-time employees, part-time employees, employees on fixed-term or rolling contracts, interns and trainees engaged in structured programs, external consultants working on Company premises or accessing Company systems on a sustained basis, and any other individual operating within the Company's professional environment who has a legitimate workplace query or concern that falls within the Helpdesk's scope of service. The nature or duration of an individual's engagement does not reduce their entitlement to professional and respectful Helpdesk support.
— Seniority Does Not Alter the Service Standard. Every employee is entitled to the same quality of Helpdesk support regardless of their seniority or position within the organization. A junior team member's request is not less important than a senior leader's request, and Helpdesk agents must not prioritize, de-prioritize, or differentiate in the quality of their response based on the organizational level of the employee submitting the request. Requests are prioritized according to urgency and impact as described in Section 3.5, not according to hierarchy.
3.2.2 Where This Policy Applies
— All Physical and Digital Environments. This policy applies in all settings in which employees access Helpdesk support, whether they are working on Company premises, at client sites, at home in a remote or hybrid arrangement, while traveling on Company business, or in any other location from which they may legitimately need to raise a Helpdesk request. The Helpdesk's obligation to provide consistent, professional, and timely support does not vary based on the employee's physical location at the time of the request.
— Remote and Hybrid Work Environments. Given the significant and growing proportion of the workforce operating in remote or hybrid arrangements, this policy places particular emphasis on ensuring that remote employees have equal and unimpeded access to every category of Helpdesk support. The fact that a remote employee cannot walk to a physical support desk does not mean they are less well-served. The Company is committed to ensuring that digital access channels for Helpdesk support are as effective, responsive, and accessible as any in-person equivalent.
3.2.3 Relationship With Other Policies
— Supporting Framework for the Broader Policy Library. The Employee Helpdesk Policy operates as a practical support mechanism for the broader policy framework within which all employees work. Employees who have questions about the Code of Conduct referenced as HR-COC-001, the Attendance Policy referenced as HR-ATT-001, the Work From Home Policy referenced as HR-WFH-001, the Information Security Policy referenced as HR-ISP-001, the Data Protection and Privacy Policy referenced as HR-DPP-001, or any other Company policy, are encouraged to raise those questions through the Helpdesk as their first point of contact. The Helpdesk will either resolve the query directly or direct the employee to the appropriate function for specialist guidance.
— Conflict Resolution. In the event of any apparent conflict between the standards or commitments established in this policy and those established in any other Company policy, HR will determine the applicable provision based on the specific circumstances. Employees should raise apparent conflicts through the Helpdesk itself and should not act unilaterally on their own interpretation of any such conflict.

3.3 The Employee Helpdesk — Structure and Purpose
3.3.1 What the Employee Helpdesk Is
— The Central Point of Employee Support. The Employee Helpdesk is the Company's designated, centralized, and structured mechanism for receiving, processing, and resolving the support needs of its employees. It is not a single team or a single channel — it is an integrated service function that brings together HR support, IT support, and facilities and administrative support under a unified framework of standards, accountability, and service quality. The Helpdesk is staffed by trained agents who are equipped to handle a wide range of employee queries and who are supported by clear escalation pathways for matters requiring specialist input.
— Purpose, Not Just Process. The Employee Helpdesk exists for a clear and specific purpose — to remove the friction that employees experience when they encounter problems, questions, or uncertainties in the course of their work, and to do so in a way that is fast, accurate, respectful, and genuinely helpful. It is not a process for deflecting queries or generating ticket numbers. It is a service designed to produce real resolutions for real people, and every agent and every interaction within the Helpdesk must be oriented toward that purpose at all times.
3.3.2 Categories of Helpdesk Support
— Three Core Service Pillars. The Helpdesk provides support across three core service pillars. The first is HR support, covering matters such as payroll queries, leave requests, policy clarifications, onboarding and offboarding processes, employment documentation, benefits questions, and guidance on HR processes and procedures. The second is IT support, covering matters such as system access and login issues, hardware and software problems, connectivity and network concerns, email and communication platform issues, software installation, and IT security concerns. The third is facilities and administrative support, covering matters such as workspace concerns, office equipment, travel and expense administration, building access, and other operational or administrative queries that do not fall within the HR or IT categories. Each pillar operates within the same overarching framework of service standards, prioritization, escalation, and accountability described in this policy.

3.4 How to Access the Helpdesk
3.4.1 Available Contact Channels
— Multiple Channels for Maximum Accessibility. The Company provides multiple channels through which employees may contact the Helpdesk, recognizing that different employees in different situations will have different preferences and needs. These channels include the online self-service portal, which is the primary and preferred channel for non-urgent requests and allows employees to submit, track, and manage their own tickets at any time; the dedicated Helpdesk email address, which is appropriate for written queries that require documentation or detailed explanation; the Helpdesk telephone line, which is available during standard business hours for employees who prefer to speak with an agent directly or whose issue requires verbal clarification; the live chat function on the self-service portal, which is available during standard business hours for employees who need quick guidance or an initial assessment of their query; and walk-in support at designated physical Helpdesk locations, which is available at the Company's primary office locations during standard business hours. The specific contact details for each channel are published in the HRMS and on the Company intranet and will be kept current by HR.
— Channel Availability. The self-service portal and email channel are available at all times. The telephone, live chat, and walk-in channels operate during standard business hours, which are published on the Company intranet. Out-of-hours support for critical and emergency issues is described in Section 3.14.
3.4.2 Choosing the Right Channel
— Matching the Channel to the Need. Employees are encouraged to use the channel that is most appropriate to the nature and urgency of their request. The self-service portal is the recommended channel for the majority of non-urgent requests because it generates an automatic ticket reference, enables employees to track the progress of their request, and creates a clear record of the interaction for both the employee and the Helpdesk. The telephone and live chat channels are best suited to queries that require immediate dialogue or initial triage before a formal ticket is raised. Email is appropriate for requests that require the submission of documents or detailed written explanation. Walk-in support is best suited to employees who need hands-on assistance with a physical device, system, or workspace issue. Regardless of the channel used, every interaction will be logged in the Helpdesk system and managed in accordance with the standards described in this policy.

3.5 Ticket Management and the Request Lifecycle
3.5.1 Ticket Creation and Acknowledgment
— Every Request Becomes a Ticket. Every request submitted to the Helpdesk — regardless of the channel through which it is received — will be converted into a formal ticket in the Helpdesk management system. This ticket is the primary mechanism through which the request is tracked, managed, communicated, and ultimately resolved. Every ticket will be assigned a unique reference number that the employee can use to track the status of their request, communicate with the Helpdesk about progress, and reference in any subsequent discussion about the matter. The creation of a ticket is not optional or discretionary. It is a mandatory step in the request lifecycle that applies to every interaction without exception.
— Acknowledgment Commitment. Every ticket raised through the Helpdesk will receive an acknowledgment confirming receipt and providing the employee with their ticket reference number. For tickets raised through the self-service portal or by email, the acknowledgment will be automated and will be delivered within thirty minutes of the ticket being submitted. For tickets raised by telephone or live chat, acknowledgment will be provided verbally during the interaction, followed by a written confirmation sent to the employee's Company email address. An acknowledgment is not a resolution. It is a confirmation that the request has been received and is being managed. The time taken to reach a resolution will depend on the priority level assigned to the ticket as described in Section 3.5.2.
3.5.2 Ticket Prioritization
— A Clear and Consistent Prioritization Framework. All tickets will be assigned a priority level upon receipt based on an assessment of the urgency of the issue and its impact on the employee's ability to perform their work. The Company uses four priority levels. Critical priority applies to issues that entirely prevent an employee or group of employees from working — such as a complete system outage, a total loss of network access, or a payroll error that affects an employee's financial wellbeing — and requires an immediate response with resolution or workaround within four business hours. High priority applies to issues that significantly impair an employee's ability to work but do not prevent them from working entirely, and requires a response within one business day. Medium priority applies to issues that cause inconvenience or disruption without significantly impairing core work functions, and requires a response within two business days. Low priority applies to general queries, informational requests, and non-urgent administrative matters, and requires a response within five business days. The specific response time commitments associated with each priority level are detailed in Section 3.6.
— Transparency in Prioritization. The priority level assigned to a ticket will be communicated to the employee at the time of acknowledgment. Where an employee believes their ticket has been assigned an incorrect priority level, they may request a review through the escalation process described in Section 3.11.
3.5.3 Ticket Resolution and Closure
— Resolution With Confirmation. A ticket will be considered resolved only when the employee's underlying issue or query has been fully and verifiably addressed. Helpdesk agents must not close a ticket based on the assumption that a resolution has been achieved without confirming this with the employee. Before closing any ticket, the agent must communicate the proposed resolution to the employee, confirm that the employee is satisfied that the issue has been addressed, and provide any relevant follow-up information or guidance that the employee may need to prevent recurrence. Where a ticket has been resolved by referral to another function — such as Payroll, Legal, or a specialist HR team — the Helpdesk agent must confirm with the employee that the referral has been received and that the employee knows who is now handling their matter before closing the original ticket.
— Reopening Closed Tickets. Where an employee believes that a ticket has been closed without their issue being fully resolved, they have the right to request that the ticket be reopened within five business days of the closure notification. A request to reopen a ticket will be treated with the same seriousness as the original submission and will be reviewed by the Helpdesk Team Lead. Where reopened tickets reveal a pattern of premature closure by a particular agent, this will be addressed through the performance management and quality assurance processes described in Section 3.20.

3.6 Service Level Standards and Response Commitments
3.6.1 Response Time Commitments by Priority Level
— Published and Accountable Commitments. The Company's response time commitments for each ticket priority level are as follows. Critical priority tickets will receive an initial substantive response — meaning a response that actively addresses the issue rather than simply restating the acknowledgment — within two business hours of receipt, and the Helpdesk will provide a workaround or resolution within four business hours. High priority tickets will receive an initial substantive response within four business hours and a full resolution within one business day. Medium priority tickets will receive an initial substantive response within one business day and a full resolution within three business days. Low priority tickets will receive an initial substantive response within two business days and a full resolution within five business days. These commitments represent the minimum standard the Company holds itself to, not a ceiling on the speed of service the Helpdesk aims to deliver. Where resolution within the stated timeframe proves impossible due to complexity or dependency on a third party, the Helpdesk agent must proactively communicate this to the employee, provide a realistic revised estimate, and update the employee at regular intervals until resolution is achieved.
3.6.2 The Company's Service Quality Commitment
— More Than Speed. The Company's commitment to Helpdesk service quality goes beyond response and resolution times. The Company is committed to ensuring that every Helpdesk interaction is conducted with professionalism, respect, and genuine care for the employee's experience. It is committed to ensuring that the information and guidance provided through the Helpdesk is accurate, complete, and genuinely useful. It is committed to ensuring that employees are kept informed throughout the lifecycle of their ticket and never left wondering what is happening with their request. And it is committed to treating every employee who contacts the Helpdesk as an individual with a real need that deserves a real and thoughtful response, not as a ticket number to be processed and closed. These commitments are not aspirational statements. They are operational standards against which the Helpdesk's performance will be monitored, measured, and continuously improved as described in Section 3.20.

3.7 HR Helpdesk Services
3.7.1 Scope of HR Support
— What the HR Helpdesk Covers. The HR Helpdesk provides employees with support across all routine HR matters that can be addressed through a centralized service function. This includes queries about payroll — including salary calculations, deductions, payment dates, and discrepancies — leave and absence management, including the submission of leave requests and the interpretation of leave entitlements under the applicable policy, employment documentation requests such as employment confirmation letters, salary certificates, and reference letters, queries about employee benefits and their administration, guidance on HR policies and procedures including the Code of Conduct, Attendance Policy, Work From Home Policy, and all other policies in the Company's policy library, support with HRMS navigation and self-service functionality, and any other routine HR administrative matter that an employee requires assistance with in the course of their employment. The HR Helpdesk does not handle matters that involve active disciplinary processes, formal grievances, formal performance management procedures, or complex employment relations concerns — these matters are handled directly by the HR Business Partner team, and the Helpdesk will refer and warm-transfer employees accordingly where needed.
3.7.2 Sensitive HR Queries and Confidentiality
— Handling Sensitive Matters With Care. Some queries directed to the HR Helpdesk will touch on matters that are sensitive in nature — personal circumstances, health conditions, financial concerns, or issues relating to workplace relationships or conduct. Helpdesk agents handling such queries must treat them with a heightened standard of discretion, must ensure that the interaction is not conducted in a manner that could be overheard by unauthorized individuals, must not retain or share sensitive personal information beyond what is strictly necessary to address the query, and must escalate to the HR Business Partner team where the nature of the matter exceeds the scope of standard Helpdesk support. Employees who raise sensitive matters through the Helpdesk can be confident that those matters will be handled in full compliance with the Company's Data Protection and Privacy Policy referenced as HR-DPP-001 and with the confidentiality standards described in Section 3.12.

3.8 IT Helpdesk Services
3.8.1 Scope of IT Support
— What the IT Helpdesk Covers. The IT Helpdesk provides employees with support across all technology-related issues that affect their ability to perform their work. This includes assistance with system access and authentication — including password resets, multi-factor authentication setup, and access permissions — hardware issues such as laptop faults, peripheral device failures, and equipment replacement requests, software issues including application errors, installation requests, and licensing queries, connectivity and network issues including VPN access, Wi-Fi connectivity, and remote access problems, email and calendar issues including configuration, access, and functionality concerns, communication platform support covering tools such as instant messaging, video conferencing, and collaboration platforms, and IT security incidents including potential phishing attempts, suspicious system behavior, or suspected data breaches. Employees who identify an IT security incident of any kind — regardless of severity — must report it through the Helpdesk immediately as their primary point of contact, in addition to following the procedures set out in the Information Security Policy referenced as HR-ISP-001.
3.8.2 IT Security Incidents Reported Through the Helpdesk
— Prioritizing Security Incidents. IT security incidents reported through the Helpdesk will be automatically escalated to Critical priority and referred immediately to the IT Security team. The Helpdesk will not attempt to resolve security incidents through standard Tier 1 support processes. The role of the Helpdesk in the context of a security incident is to receive the report promptly, acknowledge receipt to the employee, escalate to the IT Security team without delay, and provide the employee with clear guidance on any immediate steps they should take while the incident is being investigated — such as disconnecting from the network, changing credentials, or ceasing use of a potentially compromised device. The Helpdesk will maintain communication with the employee throughout the security incident process and will not close the related ticket until the IT Security team has confirmed that the incident has been fully addressed and the employee has been given a clear and final update.

3.9 Facilities and Administrative Helpdesk Services
3.9.1 Scope of Facilities and Administrative Support
— What Facilities and Administrative Support Covers. The Helpdesk provides employees with support across a range of facilities and administrative matters that affect their physical working environment and their operational needs. This includes workspace concerns such as desk or office allocation queries, maintenance requests, lighting, heating, air conditioning, or other physical environment issues that affect the employee's comfort or ability to work, office equipment support covering printers, scanners, telephone handsets, and other shared office equipment, building access issues including access card registration, replacement, and deactivation, parking and transport queries where applicable, travel and accommodation support in connection with business travel, expense reporting guidance and process clarification, and general administrative queries that do not fall within the HR or IT service pillars. Where a facilities or administrative matter requires involvement from a third-party service provider — such as a building management company, a travel agent, or an equipment supplier — the Helpdesk will act as the primary point of coordination and will keep the employee informed of progress until the matter is fully resolved.

3.10 Roles and Responsibilities
3.10.1 Employee Responsibilities
— The Employee's Role in Effective Helpdesk Support. Every employee has a responsibility to engage with the Helpdesk in a manner that enables the function to serve them effectively. This means raising requests through the appropriate channel for the nature of the issue, providing clear, accurate, and complete information about the issue when submitting a ticket, including relevant context such as error messages, system names, affected dates, or prior attempts to resolve the issue independently, responding promptly to communications from Helpdesk agents who require additional information to progress the resolution, treating all Helpdesk agents with the same respect and professionalism expected in every other professional interaction within the Company, providing honest feedback on the Helpdesk experience through the mechanisms described in Section 3.20, and not misusing the Helpdesk in the ways described in Section 3.23. An employee who fails to engage constructively with the Helpdesk process — by providing incomplete information, failing to respond to agent communications, or treating agents disrespectfully — is making it materially more difficult for the Company to provide them with the quality of support they deserve, and this failure is taken seriously under this policy.
3.10.2 Helpdesk Agent Responsibilities
— The Agent's Role in Service Delivery. Helpdesk agents are the human face of the Company's commitment to employee support, and they carry a significant responsibility to uphold the standards described in this policy in every interaction. Their responsibilities include receiving every request courteously, patiently, and professionally regardless of the nature of the query or the manner in which it is raised, creating accurate and complete ticket records for every interaction, assessing and assigning priority levels fairly and consistently in accordance with the framework set out in Section 3.5.2, providing timely, accurate, and genuinely useful responses that address the employee's actual issue rather than offering generic deflections, keeping employees informed of progress at regular intervals throughout the ticket lifecycle without waiting to be asked for an update, escalating requests that exceed their expertise or authority without delay and in a way that maintains continuity of service for the employee, maintaining the confidentiality of all information shared by employees in the course of Helpdesk interactions, and completing every interaction in a manner that leaves the employee feeling genuinely supported and valued.
3.10.3 Manager Responsibilities
— The Manager's Supporting Role. Managers within the organization bear a specific and important responsibility in relation to the Employee Helpdesk, both as users of the service themselves and as leaders of teams whose members will from time to time require Helpdesk support. Managers must familiarize themselves with the Helpdesk channels, scope, and service standards described in this policy so that they can direct their team members to the appropriate support quickly and accurately. They must not attempt to resolve matters that fall within the Helpdesk's scope through informal workarounds that bypass the Helpdesk process, as this undermines the integrity of the tracking and resolution system. They must support team members who need to take time to engage with the Helpdesk in resolving issues that affect their ability to work, and must not treat the raising of a Helpdesk request as an indication of poor performance or lack of capability. Where a manager identifies a pattern of Helpdesk queries within their team that may indicate a broader process, training, or systems issue, they must escalate this observation to HR so that the root cause can be addressed.
3.10.4 HR and IT Leadership Responsibilities
— Ownership and Accountability at the Leadership Level. The Head of HR and the Head of IT share joint responsibility for the overall performance, quality, and continuous improvement of the Employee Helpdesk. They are responsible for ensuring that the Helpdesk is adequately staffed, trained, and resourced to meet the service level commitments described in this policy, for overseeing the quality assurance and performance monitoring processes described in Section 3.20, for reviewing the Helpdesk performance data described in Section 3.24 and taking corrective action where performance falls below the committed standards, for keeping this policy current, relevant, and fit for purpose through the annual review process described in Section 3.25, and for creating an organizational environment in which the Helpdesk is genuinely valued and supported as a critical employee service function rather than treated as a secondary administrative overhead.

3.11 Escalation Framework
3.11.1 When and How to Escalate
— The Purpose of Escalation. The escalation framework exists to ensure that issues which cannot be fully resolved at the initial point of contact are referred promptly and effectively to the level of expertise, authority, or resource required to achieve a resolution. Escalation is not an admission of failure. It is a structured and professional mechanism for ensuring that complex, sensitive, or urgent matters are handled by the most appropriate person or team within the timeframe that the employee's situation requires. Every Helpdesk agent is expected to recognize when a matter exceeds their own capability or authority and to escalate without hesitation and without delay. Holding onto a ticket that requires escalation in the hope that a solution will emerge is not acceptable. It delays resolution and undermines the employee's trust in the service.
— How Employees Can Request Escalation. Employees who are not satisfied with the progress of their ticket — because they believe it has been incorrectly prioritized, because the response time commitment has been breached without explanation, or because the proposed resolution does not address their actual issue — may request escalation directly. An escalation request can be submitted through the self-service portal by selecting the escalation option on the relevant ticket, or by contacting the Helpdesk by telephone and asking to speak with the Helpdesk Team Lead. Escalation requests will be acknowledged within two business hours and reviewed by the Helpdesk Team Lead within the same business day.
3.11.2 Escalation Tiers
— A Three-Tier Escalation Structure. The Helpdesk operates on a three-tier escalation structure. Tier 1 represents the initial Helpdesk agent who receives and attempts to resolve the request through standard support processes. The majority of Helpdesk interactions will be fully resolved at Tier 1. Tier 2 represents the Helpdesk Team Lead and specialist support agents who handle requests that require deeper expertise, more complex diagnosis, or coordination across multiple functions. Tier 2 will handle escalations from Tier 1 and will take ownership of tickets that have been escalated by employees under the process described in Section 3.11.1. Tier 3 represents senior HR, IT, or Facilities leadership and, where appropriate, external specialist support. Tier 3 is reserved for the most complex, sensitive, or critical matters that cannot be resolved within the Helpdesk function and require senior organizational involvement or external resource. At every tier, the escalating agent retains responsibility for communicating the escalation to the employee and ensuring that the employee knows who is now handling their matter.

3.12 Confidentiality and Data Protection in Helpdesk Interactions
3.12.1 Employee Data and Privacy
— All Helpdesk Interactions Are Confidential. The information that employees share with the Helpdesk in the course of raising a request — whether relating to their own circumstances, their work, their colleagues, or the Company's systems and processes — is treated as strictly confidential and is handled in full compliance with the Company's Data Protection and Privacy Policy referenced as HR-DPP-001. Ticket records are maintained securely within the Helpdesk management system and are accessible only to authorized Helpdesk personnel and, where escalation requires it, to the relevant specialist function. No information shared through a Helpdesk interaction will be disclosed to any person who does not have a direct and legitimate role in resolving the employee's request, and no such information will be retained beyond the period necessary for the purposes of resolution, quality assurance, and compliance with applicable data retention requirements.
3.12.2 Helpdesk Agent Confidentiality Obligations
— A Personal Obligation on Every Agent. Every Helpdesk agent has a personal and professional obligation to maintain the confidentiality of every interaction they handle. This means not discussing the content of any employee's ticket with colleagues who are not involved in the resolution, not referencing details from one employee's interaction in the context of another employee's request, not retaining records of sensitive interactions in personal notes or informal communications, and handling all information relating to employees' personal circumstances, health conditions, performance, or employment status with the highest level of discretion. A breach of confidentiality by a Helpdesk agent will be treated as a serious conduct violation under the Code of Conduct and will be addressed through the disciplinary framework.

3.13 Standards of Conduct in Helpdesk Interactions
3.13.1 Employee Conduct When Engaging the Helpdesk
— Respectful Engagement Is Required. The same standards of respectful and professional conduct that apply in every other professional interaction within the Company apply equally in all interactions with the Helpdesk. Employees must communicate with Helpdesk agents courteously, providing the information required to address their query clearly and completely, responding to requests for clarification promptly and cooperatively, and treating agents as professional colleagues deserving of the same respect as any other member of the workforce. The Company recognizes that employees who contact the Helpdesk may sometimes be frustrated or stressed by the issue they are facing. That frustration is understandable and the Helpdesk will respond to it with patience and empathy. What is not acceptable, however, is directing that frustration at Helpdesk agents through aggressive, abusive, dismissive, or demeaning communication. Any such conduct will be noted in the ticket record and addressed in accordance with the Code of Conduct.
3.13.2 Helpdesk Agent Conduct Standards
— Professionalism in Every Interaction. Helpdesk agents are expected to conduct every interaction to the highest standard of professional service. This means greeting every employee warmly and professionally regardless of the nature or complexity of the request, listening to the employee's issue with genuine attention before beginning to formulate a response, communicating clearly, accurately, and in plain language that is accessible to employees of all technical backgrounds, managing the employee's expectations honestly — including communicating clearly when a resolution will take time and why — following up proactively without waiting for the employee to chase progress, and closing every interaction in a way that confirms the employee's satisfaction and leaves them with a clear understanding of any next steps. Agents who fall short of these standards will be supported through coaching, training, and performance management processes as described in Section 3.22.

3.14 Out-of-Hours and Emergency Support
3.14.1 Support Outside Standard Business Hours
— Provision of Out-of-Hours Support. The Company recognizes that operational needs do not always align with standard business hours and that some employees — particularly those in remote locations, those working non-standard shifts, or those traveling across time zones on Company business — may require Helpdesk support outside of standard operating hours. The Company maintains an out-of-hours support provision for critical priority issues that cannot wait until the next business day. Details of the out-of-hours support channel and its availability are published on the Company intranet and in the HRMS. Out-of-hours support is scoped to critical and emergency issues only and does not extend to medium or low priority queries, which will be addressed on the next business day.
3.14.2 Emergency and Critical Issue Handling
— An Urgent Response for Critical Situations. Where an employee experiences a critical issue outside standard business hours — such as a complete inability to access systems required for imminent work commitments, a suspected data breach or security incident, or a payroll emergency that requires immediate escalation — the out-of-hours channel will connect them with an on-call Helpdesk agent who will triage the issue, take any immediate protective or remedial action within their authority, escalate to senior IT or HR leadership where required, and ensure that the employee has a clear point of contact and a realistic timeline for resolution. Critical issues reported through the out-of-hours channel will be fully documented and transferred to the standard Helpdesk system at the commencement of the next business day to ensure continuity of management and resolution.

3.15 Support for Remote and Hybrid Employees
3.15.1 Equal Access to Helpdesk Services for Remote Employees
— No Disadvantage Based on Work Location. The Company is committed to ensuring that employees working remotely or in hybrid arrangements have equal access to the full range of Helpdesk services and receive the same quality and timeliness of support as employees working from Company premises. Remote employees will not be deprioritized, offered a reduced service, or directed to resolve issues independently simply because they are not physically present in a Company office. Every service, channel, and commitment described in this policy applies equally and without modification to remote and hybrid employees.
3.15.2 Remote-Specific Support Considerations
— Tailored Support for Remote Working Challenges. The Helpdesk recognizes that remote and hybrid employees face a specific set of challenges that are distinct from those of on-site employees — including home network and connectivity issues, remote access configuration, the setup and maintenance of Company devices in a home environment, and the absence of informal in-person support from colleagues. Helpdesk agents handling requests from remote employees will be trained to address these specific challenges and will apply a heightened level of patience and practical guidance when supporting employees who may have limited access to on-site technical assistance.

3.16 Helpdesk Access for New Joiners and Exiting Employees
3.16.1 Onboarding Support Through the Helpdesk
— A First Point of Support for New Employees. The Employee Helpdesk plays a critical role in the onboarding experience of new joiners. For many new employees, the Helpdesk will be one of the first organizational touchpoints through which they interact with the Company's support infrastructure, and the quality of that first experience will materially shape their initial impression of the organization's commitment to its people. The Helpdesk will be proactively communicated to all new joiners as part of the onboarding process, with clear information about how to access support, what types of queries it handles, and what they can expect in terms of response. Onboarding-related queries — including system access, equipment setup, HRMS navigation, payroll setup, and policy questions — will be treated as a priority category during the employee's first thirty days of employment, with Helpdesk agents providing a level of guidance and patience that reflects the new joiner's unfamiliarity with the Company's systems and processes.
3.16.2 Offboarding Support Through the Helpdesk
— Support for Employees Leaving the Organization. The Helpdesk also plays an important role in the offboarding process for employees who are leaving the Company. Exiting employees may raise queries through the Helpdesk relating to the return of Company equipment, the processing of final payroll and expense claims, the deactivation of system access, the provision of employment documentation, or any other administrative matter arising in connection with their departure. The Helpdesk will handle all such queries with the same professionalism and efficiency applied to any other request, recognizing that the experience of leaving an organization is often significant for the individual involved and that the quality of support provided during this period reflects on the Company's values and its respect for its people.

3.17 Language and Accessibility of Helpdesk Services
3.17.1 Accessibility Commitments
— Support That Is Accessible to All. The Company is committed to ensuring that the Helpdesk is genuinely accessible to every employee, including employees with disabilities or accessibility needs that may affect their ability to use the standard contact channels. Employees who require an alternative format, an alternative communication method, or any other reasonable adjustment to access Helpdesk support are encouraged to make their needs known through any available channel, and the Helpdesk will work with HR and the relevant specialist function to ensure that an appropriate and effective accommodation is identified and implemented without delay. Accessibility needs will be recorded in the employee's Helpdesk profile with the employee's consent so that future interactions can be handled in a manner that meets those needs without requiring the employee to explain them on each occasion.
3.17.2 Language Support
— Supporting Employees in Their Preferred Language. Where the Company operates across multiple geographies and language groups, the Helpdesk will make reasonable efforts to provide support in the employee's preferred working language or to connect the employee with a bilingual agent or resource where this is practicable. Where language support is not immediately available, the Helpdesk will acknowledge this transparently, provide a realistic timeline for obtaining assistance, and take all reasonable steps to ensure the employee is not materially disadvantaged in obtaining the support they need.

3.18 Knowledge Base and Self-Service Resources
3.18.1 The Employee Self-Service Portal
— Empowering Employees to Help Themselves. The Company maintains a comprehensive self-service knowledge base within the Helpdesk portal that contains guidance articles, frequently asked questions, step-by-step process guides, and policy summaries covering the most commonly raised categories of HR, IT, and facilities queries. Employees are encouraged to consult the knowledge base as a first step when they have a query, as many routine questions can be answered immediately through self-service without the need to raise a ticket. The knowledge base is reviewed and updated on a regular basis by the Helpdesk team, in collaboration with HR, IT, and Facilities, to ensure that its content remains accurate, current, and useful.
3.18.2 Encouraging Self-Service Without Discouraging Contact
— Self-Service as a Complement, Not a Barrier. The Company's investment in self-service resources is intended to give employees a faster and more convenient option for resolving routine queries — it is not intended to discourage employees from contacting the Helpdesk when they genuinely need human support. Employees who consult the self-service portal and are unable to find an answer to their query, or who feel that their situation requires a personalized response, should contact the Helpdesk through their preferred channel without hesitation. Helpdesk agents will never suggest that an employee should have used self-service instead of contacting the Helpdesk in a way that makes the employee feel that their contact was unwelcome or burdensome.

3.19 Communication Standards in Helpdesk Interactions
3.19.1 How the Helpdesk Communicates With Employees
— Clear, Consistent, and Professional Communication. All written communication from the Helpdesk — whether delivered through the ticketing system, by email, or through the live chat platform — must be written in clear, professional, and plain language that is accessible to employees of all backgrounds and technical levels. Agents must avoid the use of jargon, acronyms, or technical language that the employee is unlikely to understand without explanation. Every communication from the Helpdesk must clearly identify the agent's name, their role, the ticket reference number, the current status of the request, and any action required from the employee. Communications must be free from errors, must be appropriately formatted, and must reflect the same standard of professional conduct expected in any other form of Company communication.
3.19.2 Keeping Employees Informed
— Proactive Updates Without Prompting. One of the most significant sources of employee frustration with support functions is the experience of submitting a request and then hearing nothing — not knowing whether the request has been received, whether anyone is working on it, or when a resolution can be expected. The Helpdesk is committed to preventing this experience by maintaining a standard of proactive communication throughout the ticket lifecycle. Employees must not have to chase the Helpdesk for an update. Where a ticket is being worked on but a resolution is not yet available, the agent must send a proactive update to the employee within the resolution timeframe for the relevant priority level, confirming the current status, explaining any reason for delay, and providing a revised estimate where applicable. This standard of proactive communication must be maintained throughout the lifecycle of every ticket until closure.

3.20 Feedback, Quality Assurance, and Continuous Improvement
3.20.1 Employee Feedback Mechanisms
— Every Resolved Ticket Invites Feedback. Upon the closure of every Helpdesk ticket, the employee will receive an automated survey inviting them to rate their experience and provide any comments they wish to share about the quality of the support they received. This survey is brief, taking no more than two minutes to complete, and covers the key dimensions of the Helpdesk experience — the speed of the response, the accuracy and usefulness of the resolution, the professionalism and courtesy of the agent, and the employee's overall satisfaction with the interaction. Participation in the survey is voluntary but strongly encouraged. The responses are treated as valuable operational data that directly inform the Helpdesk's continuous improvement agenda and are reviewed by the Helpdesk Team Lead and HR leadership on a monthly basis. Employees who wish to provide more detailed feedback — or who wish to raise a concern about a specific interaction — may do so through the complaints process described in Section 3.21.
3.20.2 Quality Assurance and Performance Monitoring
— A Structured Quality Assurance Framework. The Helpdesk operates within a structured quality assurance framework that includes regular review of ticket records by the Helpdesk Team Lead to assess the quality of agent responses and the consistency of priority assignment, monthly analysis of service level performance data to assess compliance with the response and resolution time commitments described in Section 3.6, regular calibration sessions in which agents and the Team Lead review sample interactions together to align on quality standards and identify development opportunities, and quarterly performance reviews for individual Helpdesk agents that incorporate feedback from the employee satisfaction survey, service level data, and the Team Lead's own quality observations. The outcomes of quality assurance reviews are used constructively — to identify training needs, to recognize excellent performance, and to address quality gaps through coaching and targeted development.
3.20.3 Continuous Improvement Culture
— Improvement Is an Ongoing Commitment. The Company's commitment to Helpdesk service quality is not a static standard to be maintained — it is a continuous improvement journey. The Helpdesk Team Lead will compile a monthly quality and performance report that is shared with HR and IT leadership and that identifies trends in query types, recurring issues that may indicate systemic process or training gaps, areas where service level performance has fallen below the committed standards and the actions being taken to address this, and opportunities to improve the knowledge base, self-service resources, or agent training based on the patterns observed in the month's interactions. This report will feed into the annual policy review process described in Section 3.25 to ensure that the commitments and standards in this policy remain realistic, relevant, and genuinely reflective of the service the Company is able and committed to delivering.

3.21 Handling Complaints About the Helpdesk
3.21.1 The Right to Raise a Complaint
— Every Employee Has the Right to Be Heard. Where an employee is dissatisfied with any aspect of the Helpdesk service they have received — including the quality of an agent's response, a breach of the response or resolution time commitments, a perceived failure of confidentiality, or any other concern about the way in which their request was handled — they have the right to raise a formal complaint and to have that complaint reviewed and addressed in a timely and impartial manner. The right to raise a complaint is unconditional. No employee will be discouraged, penalized, or treated differently as a result of raising a genuine complaint about the Helpdesk service.
3.21.2 How Helpdesk Complaints Are Resolved
— A Fair and Transparent Complaints Process. Complaints about the Helpdesk should be submitted in writing through the designated complaints channel published on the Company intranet. All complaints will be acknowledged within one business day of receipt and will be reviewed by the Helpdesk Team Lead or, where the complaint concerns the Team Lead, by the Head of HR. The reviewer will assess the complaint against the standards set out in this policy, will speak with the relevant agent where appropriate, and will provide the employee with a written response within five business days of the complaint being submitted. The response will clearly set out the reviewer's findings, any corrective action being taken, and any apology or remedy that is appropriate in the circumstances. Where a complaint reveals a serious failure of conduct or service quality, it will be escalated to HR and addressed through the appropriate performance management or disciplinary process.

3.22 Training and Competency of Helpdesk Personnel
3.22.1 Mandatory Training for Helpdesk Agents
— Trained Before Deployed. No Helpdesk agent will be permitted to handle employee queries without first completing the Company's mandatory Helpdesk induction training program. This program covers the scope and purpose of the Helpdesk as described in this policy, the ticket management system and its correct use, the prioritization framework and the response and resolution time commitments, the service quality and conduct standards expected of all agents, the confidentiality and data protection obligations described in Section 3.12, the escalation framework and the circumstances in which escalation is required, and the specific content and processes of the HR, IT, and Facilities service pillars. The induction program must be completed in full and assessed before the agent handles any live interaction. Completion of the program will be recorded in the HRMS and monitored by the Helpdesk Team Lead.
3.22.2 Ongoing Professional Development
— Development Does Not Stop at Induction. The Company is committed to the ongoing professional development of its Helpdesk personnel, recognizing that service quality depends on agents who are continuously growing in their capability, knowledge, and professionalism. Ongoing development is delivered through monthly calibration sessions that sharpen agents' quality standards, quarterly refresher training covering policy updates, process changes, and new systems or tools, targeted coaching for agents who have been identified through quality assurance reviews as having specific development needs, access to the broader Company learning and development program including communication skills, empathy training, and technical knowledge development, and an annual performance review that includes a forward-looking development conversation between the agent and the Team Lead. Helpdesk agents who demonstrate exceptional service quality and professional growth will be recognized through the Company's performance recognition framework and considered for advancement within the Helpdesk function or the broader HR and IT teams.

3.23 Misuse of the Helpdesk — Standards and Consequences
3.23.1 What Constitutes Misuse
— The Helpdesk Must Be Used in Good Faith. The Employee Helpdesk is a shared organizational resource that exists for the genuine support needs of every employee. Its capacity and effectiveness depend on employees using it responsibly and in good faith. Misuse of the Helpdesk includes submitting requests that are knowingly false, fabricated, or designed to harass or inconvenience specific agents or colleagues, submitting repeated requests on the same resolved matter without a genuine basis for believing the resolution was inadequate, using the Helpdesk as a channel to make complaints or raise concerns that properly belong in the grievance or disciplinary process, engaging in abusive, threatening, or disrespectful communication with Helpdesk agents, and using Helpdesk interactions to obtain information about colleagues or Company systems to which the employee is not entitled. This list is illustrative and not exhaustive.
3.23.2 Consequences of Misuse
— Misuse Is a Conduct Concern. Any employee who misuses the Helpdesk in the ways described in Section 3.23.1 will be addressed under the progressive disciplinary framework set out in the Code of Conduct. The Helpdesk Team Lead will document instances of misuse in the ticket record and report them to HR. HR will assess whether the conduct meets the threshold for formal disciplinary action and will proceed accordingly. In cases where an employee's conduct toward a Helpdesk agent constitutes harassment or abusive behavior, this will be treated with the same seriousness as any other instance of workplace harassment under the Company's Anti-Harassment and Anti-Bullying Policy.

3.24 Helpdesk Performance Reporting and Governance
3.24.1 Reporting and Accountability
— Visible Performance, Visible Accountability. The Company is committed to transparency in the performance of the Employee Helpdesk. The Helpdesk Team Lead will produce a monthly performance report that is shared with the Head of HR, the Head of IT, and the Chief Human Resources Officer. This report will include data on total ticket volumes by category and priority level, service level compliance rates against the response and resolution time commitments described in Section 3.6, employee satisfaction survey results and key qualitative themes from the feedback received, escalation volumes and trends, quality assurance findings and outcomes, and any significant incidents or complaints received during the reporting period. This report provides the leadership team with the information needed to hold the Helpdesk function accountable for its performance and to make informed decisions about resourcing, training, and process improvement.
3.24.2 Governance and Oversight
— Clear Governance for a Critical Function. The Employee Helpdesk operates under the joint governance of the Head of HR and the Head of IT, with ultimate accountability resting with the Chief Human Resources Officer. The Helpdesk Team Lead is responsible for day-to-day operational management of the function, including the performance and development of all Helpdesk agents, the quality and consistency of the service delivered, and compliance with the standards established in this policy. The governance framework includes a quarterly Helpdesk review meeting attended by the Head of HR, the Head of IT, and the Helpdesk Team Lead, at which performance data is reviewed, priorities for the forthcoming quarter are agreed, and any policy, resourcing, or process changes required to maintain or improve service quality are discussed and approved.

3.25 Policy Compliance, Governance, and Review
3.25.1 Responsibility for Policy Compliance
— Every Employee's Personal Responsibility. Compliance with this Employee Helpdesk Policy is the personal responsibility of every employee without exception. Employees are responsible for reading and understanding this policy in full, for engaging with the Helpdesk in accordance with its provisions, for treating Helpdesk agents with the professionalism and respect this policy requires, and for providing honest feedback on their experience through the mechanisms described in Section 3.20. Compliance with this policy is not optional and is not dependent on whether a particular employee agrees with or is satisfied by any specific aspect of the service.
— Manager and Leadership Responsibility. Managers and leaders bear a heightened responsibility for ensuring that their teams are aware of and engaging with the Helpdesk in accordance with this policy. They must direct team members to appropriate Helpdesk channels when workplace issues arise, must not create informal workarounds that bypass the Helpdesk, must not discourage team members from raising Helpdesk requests, and must cooperate fully with any quality assurance or complaints process that involves members of their team.
— HR Responsibility. HR is responsible for administering this policy, ensuring it is clearly communicated and accessible to all employees, overseeing the performance and governance of the Helpdesk function, investigating any complaints or conduct concerns arising from Helpdesk interactions, and ensuring that the policy is applied consistently and without bias across all employees and all levels of the organization.
3.25.2 Employee Acknowledgment
— Formal Acknowledgment is Mandatory. All employees are required to formally acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agree to engage with the Helpdesk in accordance with this policy. Acknowledgment will be captured through the HRMS at the time of onboarding for all new employees and upon each substantive update to the policy for all existing employees. The acknowledgment record will be maintained in the employee's personnel file. Failure to complete the acknowledgment within the timeline specified by HR will be treated as a compliance concern and addressed accordingly.
3.25.3 Annual Policy Review
— Regular Review to Maintain Relevance and Currency. This Employee Helpdesk Policy will be formally reviewed on an annual basis by HR, or earlier if required by a material change in the Company's structure, technology, or workforce, a significant development in the operational performance or capacity of the Helpdesk, the identification of a gap in the current policy revealed by a complaint, incident, or quality assurance finding, or feedback from employees, managers, or the governance review process that reveals an area for improvement. All updates will be approved by the Chief Human Resources Officer, communicated to all employees through the HRMS and official Company communication channels, and formally acknowledged by all employees within 15 working days of communication. The Company's commitment to maintaining a current, relevant, and genuinely useful Employee Helpdesk Policy is itself a reflection of its values and its seriousness about serving its people with excellence.
