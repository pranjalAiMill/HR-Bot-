10. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT POLICY

10.1 Purpose and Organizational Philosophy
10.1.1 Why This Policy Exists
— The Foundation of Performance Excellence. The Performance Management Policy is one of the most strategically critical documents in the Company's entire policy framework. It establishes the principles, standards, and comprehensive frameworks through which employee performance is managed, evaluated, developed, and recognized — ensuring that individual performance aligns with organizational objectives, that employees receive the feedback and support they need to succeed, and that performance excellence is recognized and rewarded. Where the Employment Policy referenced as HR-EP-001 governs the terms and conditions of employment, and the Learning and Development Policy referenced as HR-LDP-001 governs ongoing development, this policy governs how performance is managed throughout the employment relationship. Performance management is not merely an annual review process. It is a continuous, collaborative system that drives organizational success through individual excellence, and this policy is the framework through which that system operates.
— The Organizational Imperative. The Company operates in a competitive business environment where organizational success depends fundamentally on the performance of individual employees. Research consistently demonstrates that effective performance management significantly improves employee productivity, engagement, and retention, while poor performance management leads to disengagement, underperformance, and talent loss. A performance management system that is fair, transparent, developmental, and aligned with organizational objectives enables the Company to achieve its strategic goals, to recognize and reward excellence, to address performance concerns constructively, and to build a high-performance culture. This policy exists to ensure that the Company maintains such a system — one that drives performance, supports development, and creates a culture of excellence and accountability.
— The Human Imperative. This policy has been written with a genuine recognition that performance management is fundamentally about people — their growth, their success, their development, and their contribution to organizational objectives. Employees want to know how they are performing, what is expected of them, how they can improve, and how their performance is recognized. Effective performance management provides clarity, feedback, support, and recognition that enable employees to succeed and to find meaning and fulfillment in their work. This policy is not a declaration of the Company's authority to evaluate. It is a recognition of the mutual benefit of performance management — a clear, comprehensive, and accessible framework that enables employees to understand expectations, receive feedback, develop capabilities, and achieve their professional goals while contributing to organizational success.
— The Philosophy of Continuous Development. Effective performance management is not a periodic evaluation that occurs once per year. It is a continuous process of goal setting, feedback, coaching, development, and recognition that occurs throughout the performance cycle. This policy is built on the conviction that performance management must be developmental, not merely evaluative — focused on helping employees succeed, grow, and contribute at their highest potential, rather than simply assessing past performance. Performance management is most effective when it is ongoing, collaborative, and focused on future success, and this policy ensures that continuous development approach is applied consistently to every employee.
— A Living and Evolving Document. Like all enterprise policies, the Performance Management Policy is a living document that will be reviewed, updated, and strengthened as the Company's business needs evolve, as performance management best practices develop, as workforce needs change, and as practical experience provides insights into how the policy can be made more effective. Every employee who participates in performance management, every manager who evaluates performance, and every HR professional who administers the system is expected to contribute to the continuous improvement of performance management, ensuring that it remains relevant, effective, and supportive of both individual and organizational success.

10.2 Scope and Applicability
10.2.1 Who This Policy Applies To
— Universal Application to All Employees. This policy applies universally and without exception to every individual engaged by the Company in any employment capacity — regardless of seniority, role, department, business unit, geographic location, or employment classification. The most senior executive in the organization is subject to the same performance management framework as the most junior team member, recognizing that performance management is essential at every level of the organization. The specific application of certain policy provisions may vary based on role complexity, seniority, and business needs, but the fundamental framework of performance management applies to all employees.
— All Categories of Employment. This policy covers permanent full-time employees, part-time employees, employees on fixed-term contracts, and any other individual engaged in an employment relationship with the Company. Each of these individuals, regardless of the nature or duration of their employment, is subject to performance management appropriate to their role and circumstances. The Company recognizes that performance management is essential for all employees and ensures that the framework is applied consistently across all employment categories.
— Probationary and Confirmed Employees. This policy applies to both probationary and confirmed employees, recognizing that performance management begins from the first day of employment and continues throughout the employment relationship. Probationary employees receive performance management that is focused on evaluation and confirmation decisions, while confirmed employees receive ongoing performance management that supports development and career growth. The Company ensures that performance management is appropriate to the employee's stage in the employment relationship.
10.2.2 Where This Policy Applies
— All Work Locations and Arrangements. This policy applies in every location and arrangement in which Company work is performed — whether on Company premises, at client sites, in remote or hybrid arrangements, or in any other work location. Remote and hybrid employees are subject to the same performance management framework as on-site employees, with accommodations for remote delivery of performance management activities. The Company ensures that performance management is effective regardless of work location.
— All Phases of the Employment Relationship. This policy applies throughout the entire employment relationship, from onboarding through active employment and through all phases of career development. Performance management is not a one-time activity but a continuous process that supports employees at every stage of their career with the Company. The Company recognizes that performance management needs may vary by career stage and ensures that the framework adapts to meet those needs.
10.2.3 Relationship With Other Policies
— An Integrated Performance Framework. This Performance Management Policy operates as one component of the Company's broader human resources and talent management framework. It must be read and applied in conjunction with the Employment Policy referenced as HR-EP-001, the Learning and Development Policy referenced as HR-LDP-001, the Code of Conduct referenced as HR-COC-001, and all other applicable Company policies. Where a specific policy provides more detailed guidance on a particular aspect of performance management — such as the Learning and Development Policy's treatment of development planning — that specific policy governs. Where a performance management concern arises that falls outside the scope of any specific policy, this Performance Management Policy provides the governing framework.
— Conflict Resolution. In the event of any apparent conflict between the standards established in this policy and those established in any other Company policy, employees must seek guidance from HR before taking action. Employees must not act unilaterally on their own interpretation of any apparent conflict between policies and must not use an apparent conflict as justification for departing from performance management standards that would otherwise apply.

10.3 Performance Management Philosophy and Principles
10.3.1 Core Principles of Performance Management
— Continuous and Collaborative Process. Performance management is a continuous, collaborative process between employees and managers, not a periodic evaluation that occurs once per year. Continuous performance management recognizes that feedback, coaching, and development must occur regularly throughout the performance cycle to be effective. Collaboration recognizes that both employees and managers have responsibilities and that performance management is most effective when it is a partnership focused on success. The Company is committed to continuous, collaborative performance management that supports ongoing development and improvement.
— Objective and Evidence-Based Evaluation. Performance evaluations are conducted objectively, based on measurable goals, job responsibilities, demonstrated competencies, and documented evidence. Objective evaluation ensures fairness, consistency, and accuracy, and enables employees to understand how their performance is assessed. The Company ensures that performance evaluations are based on facts and evidence, not on subjective impressions or personal relationships. Evidence-based evaluation protects both employees and the Company and ensures that performance management decisions are fair and defensible.
— Developmental and Supportive Focus. Performance management is developmental and supportive, focused on helping employees succeed, grow, and contribute at their highest potential. The Company recognizes that most employees want to perform well and that performance issues often result from lack of clarity, support, or development rather than lack of effort. Performance management provides the clarity, feedback, support, and development that employees need to succeed. The Company is committed to a developmental approach that helps employees improve and grow.
— Fairness, Consistency, and Non-Discrimination. Performance management is conducted fairly, consistently, and without discrimination, ensuring that all employees are evaluated using the same standards and processes. Fairness requires that evaluations are objective, that processes are transparent, and that employees have opportunities to be heard. Consistency requires that the same standards are applied to all employees in similar roles. Non-discrimination requires that performance management decisions are not influenced by protected characteristics. The Company is committed to fair, consistent, and non-discriminatory performance management.
10.3.2 Alignment with Organizational Objectives
— Strategic Alignment. Performance management aligns individual performance with organizational objectives, ensuring that employee goals and activities support the Company's strategic priorities. Strategic alignment ensures that individual effort contributes to organizational success and that employees understand how their work matters. The Company ensures that performance goals are aligned with business objectives and that performance management drives strategic execution.
— Cascading Goals and Objectives. Organizational goals cascade to departments, teams, and individuals, ensuring that every employee's goals are aligned with organizational priorities. Goal cascading ensures that individual performance contributes to team and organizational success and that employees understand their role in achieving organizational objectives. The Company maintains clear goal alignment processes that connect individual performance to organizational success.

10.4 Performance Cycle and Timeline
10.4.1 Annual Performance Cycle
— Standard Performance Cycle. Performance management is conducted on a defined annual cycle, typically aligned with the Company's fiscal year or calendar year. The annual cycle provides a structured framework for goal setting, ongoing feedback, formal reviews, and performance outcomes. The Company ensures that the performance cycle is clearly communicated, that timelines are established and communicated, and that all stakeholders understand the cycle and their responsibilities.
— Cycle Components. The performance cycle includes goal setting at the beginning of the cycle, ongoing feedback and coaching throughout the cycle, mid-year check-ins and reviews, annual formal performance reviews, development planning, and performance outcomes including compensation decisions, recognition, and career development. The Company ensures that all cycle components are completed and that the cycle provides comprehensive performance management support.
10.4.2 Ongoing Performance Management
— Continuous Feedback and Coaching. While the formal performance cycle is annual, performance management is continuous, with regular feedback, coaching, and development occurring throughout the year. Ongoing performance management recognizes that feedback and support must be timely to be effective and that waiting for annual reviews to provide feedback is insufficient. The Company expects managers to provide regular feedback and employees to seek feedback actively throughout the year.
— Regular Check-Ins. Managers and employees are expected to conduct regular check-ins, typically monthly or quarterly, to discuss progress, provide feedback, address concerns, and ensure that performance is on track. Regular check-ins ensure that performance issues are identified and addressed early, that successes are recognized promptly, and that employees receive ongoing support. The Company ensures that check-ins are structured, documented, and focused on development and improvement.
10.4.3 Mid-Year Reviews
— Mid-Year Performance Assessment. Mid-year reviews are conducted approximately halfway through the performance cycle to assess progress, provide feedback, and make adjustments to goals or development plans as needed. Mid-year reviews are less formal than annual reviews but provide important opportunities for feedback and course correction. The Company ensures that mid-year reviews are conducted consistently and that they provide value to both employees and managers.
— Goal Adjustment and Refinement. Mid-year reviews may result in goal adjustments or refinements to reflect changing business priorities, role changes, or other circumstances. Goal adjustments are made collaboratively, are documented, and ensure that goals remain relevant and achievable. The Company recognizes that business needs change and that performance goals must adapt accordingly.

10.5 Goal Setting and Performance Expectations
10.5.1 Principles of Effective Goal Setting
— SMART Goal Framework. Goals are established using the SMART framework — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. SMART goals provide clarity, enable measurement, ensure achievability, maintain relevance, and establish timelines. The Company ensures that all performance goals meet SMART criteria and that employees understand how to set effective goals. Goal setting is collaborative, with employees and managers working together to establish goals that are challenging but achievable.
— Alignment with Organizational Objectives. Goals are aligned with departmental and organizational objectives, ensuring that individual performance contributes to team and organizational success. Goal alignment ensures that employees understand how their work matters and that individual effort supports organizational priorities. The Company maintains clear alignment processes that connect individual goals to organizational objectives.
10.5.2 Types of Performance Goals
— Outcome Goals. Outcome goals focus on results and achievements, such as sales targets, project completion, quality metrics, or other measurable outcomes. Outcome goals are important for driving results and ensuring that performance contributes to business success. The Company ensures that outcome goals are clear, measurable, and aligned with business priorities.
— Behavioral Goals. Behavioral goals focus on how work is performed, such as collaboration, communication, leadership, or other behavioral competencies. Behavioral goals ensure that employees demonstrate the behaviors and competencies that are valued by the Company and that contribute to organizational culture. The Company ensures that behavioral goals are clear, observable, and aligned with Company values.
— Development Goals. Development goals focus on learning and growth, such as skill development, knowledge acquisition, or capability building. Development goals ensure that employees continue to grow and develop and that the organization maintains and enhances its capabilities. The Company ensures that development goals are included in every employee's performance plan and that development is supported and recognized.
10.5.3 Performance Expectations and Standards
— Role-Based Expectations. Performance expectations are established based on role requirements, job descriptions, and organizational needs. Expectations are clear, specific, and communicated to employees at the beginning of the performance cycle. The Company ensures that expectations are realistic, achievable, and aligned with role requirements and business needs.
— Competency Requirements. In addition to goal-based performance, employees are expected to demonstrate behaviors and competencies consistent with Company values, policies, and standards of conduct. Competency requirements are defined for each role and are evaluated as part of performance management. The Company ensures that competency requirements are clear and that employees understand what competencies are expected.
— Revision of Expectations. Performance expectations may be revised during the cycle to reflect changes in role, business priorities, or organizational strategy, with appropriate communication and documentation. The Company recognizes that business needs change and that performance expectations must adapt accordingly. Expectation revisions are made collaboratively, are documented, and ensure that employees understand updated expectations.

10.6 Ongoing Feedback and Coaching
10.6.1 Principles of Effective Feedback
— Constructive and Developmental. Feedback is constructive and developmental, focused on helping employees improve and succeed rather than on criticism or blame. Constructive feedback identifies strengths and areas for improvement, provides specific examples, and offers guidance on how to improve. The Company ensures that feedback is delivered in a supportive, respectful manner that promotes learning and development.
— Timely and Regular. Feedback is timely and regular, provided as close to the performance event as possible and on an ongoing basis throughout the performance cycle. Timely feedback is more effective than delayed feedback, and regular feedback ensures that employees receive ongoing guidance and support. The Company expects managers to provide feedback promptly and employees to seek feedback actively.
— Specific and Actionable. Feedback is specific and actionable, providing clear information about what was done well, what needs improvement, and how to improve. Specific feedback is more useful than general feedback, and actionable feedback enables employees to make improvements. The Company ensures that feedback is detailed enough to be useful and that it provides clear guidance for improvement.
10.6.2 Feedback Delivery and Receipt
— Manager Responsibility for Feedback. Managers are responsible for providing regular feedback to employees throughout the performance cycle. Manager feedback should address both performance outcomes and behavioral expectations, should be balanced (including both strengths and areas for improvement), and should be delivered in a respectful, supportive manner. The Company ensures that managers are trained in effective feedback delivery and that they provide feedback consistently.
— Employee Responsibility for Seeking Feedback. Employees are encouraged to actively seek feedback and to engage in open discussions regarding their performance and development. Employee initiative in seeking feedback demonstrates engagement and commitment to improvement. The Company expects employees to ask questions, seek clarification, and take ownership of their performance and development.
— 360-Degree Feedback. The Company may use 360-degree feedback processes that gather input from multiple sources, including peers, direct reports, and other stakeholders. 360-degree feedback provides comprehensive perspectives on performance and helps identify blind spots. The Company ensures that 360-degree feedback is used appropriately and that it supports development rather than creating anxiety or defensiveness.
10.6.3 Coaching and Development Support
— Manager as Coach. Managers are expected to serve as coaches, providing guidance, support, and development opportunities that help employees succeed and grow. Coaching includes asking questions that promote learning, providing resources and support, creating development opportunities, and helping employees overcome obstacles. The Company ensures that managers understand their coaching role and that they provide effective coaching support.
— Development Resources and Opportunities. Coaching and support may include on-the-job guidance, mentoring, training opportunities, stretch assignments, or resource allocation to help employees succeed in their roles. The Company provides various development resources and ensures that employees have access to the support they need to succeed. Development support is tailored to individual needs and is aligned with performance goals and career aspirations.

10.7 Formal Performance Reviews
10.7.1 Purpose and Structure of Formal Reviews
— Comprehensive Performance Assessment. Formal performance reviews are conducted at defined intervals, typically annually, to provide comprehensive assessment of employee performance against established goals, competencies, and role responsibilities. Formal reviews are structured, documented, and provide opportunities for reflection, feedback, and planning. The Company ensures that formal reviews are thorough, fair, and developmental.
— Review Components. Formal reviews include self-assessment by the employee, manager assessment and evaluation, documentation of performance evidence, discussion of strengths and areas for improvement, goal achievement assessment, competency evaluation, development planning, and performance outcomes. The Company ensures that reviews are comprehensive and that all relevant aspects of performance are addressed.
10.7.2 Self-Assessment Process
— Employee Self-Evaluation. Employees are required to complete a self-assessment as part of the review process, reflecting on their performance, achievements, challenges, and development needs. Self-assessment promotes self-awareness, engagement, and ownership of performance. The Company ensures that self-assessments are structured, that employees understand how to complete them effectively, and that self-assessments are considered in the review process.
— Reflection and Documentation. Self-assessments provide employees with opportunities to reflect on their performance, to document their achievements, and to identify areas for development. Reflection promotes learning and growth, and documentation ensures that employee perspectives are captured. The Company values employee self-assessment and ensures that it is integrated into the review process.
10.7.3 Manager Evaluation and Assessment
— Comprehensive Manager Evaluation. Managers provide written feedback and ratings, where applicable, based on documented performance evidence, goal achievement, competency demonstration, and overall contribution. Manager evaluations are thorough, objective, and based on facts and evidence. The Company ensures that manager evaluations are fair, accurate, and developmental.
— Performance Evidence and Documentation. Manager evaluations are supported by performance evidence, including examples of achievements, documentation of feedback provided throughout the year, and objective data where available. Performance evidence ensures that evaluations are based on facts rather than impressions. The Company ensures that managers document performance throughout the year and that evaluations are supported by evidence.
10.7.4 Review Meetings and Discussions
— Formal Review Discussions. Managers discuss performance outcomes with employees in formal review meetings, providing opportunities for dialogue, clarification, and mutual understanding. Review meetings are structured, respectful, and focused on development and improvement. The Company ensures that review meetings are conducted professionally and that they provide value to both employees and managers.
— Two-Way Communication. Review meetings are two-way conversations, with employees having opportunities to share their perspectives, ask questions, and discuss their performance and development. Two-way communication ensures that reviews are collaborative and that employees feel heard and understood. The Company values employee input and ensures that review meetings are truly collaborative.

10.8 Performance Ratings and Calibration
10.8.1 Rating Systems and Scales
— Standardized Rating Framework. Where a rating system is used, performance ratings are assigned based on documented performance evidence and objective criteria. Rating systems provide consistency and enable comparison across employees, teams, and departments. The Company ensures that rating systems are clear, fair, and well-understood by all stakeholders.
— Rating Definitions and Criteria. Rating definitions are clear and specific, providing employees and managers with understanding of what each rating means and what performance level is required to achieve each rating. Rating criteria are objective, measurable, and aligned with performance expectations. The Company ensures that rating definitions are communicated clearly and that they are applied consistently.
10.8.2 Calibration and Consistency
— Cross-Team Calibration. The Company reserves the right to calibrate performance ratings across teams or departments to ensure consistency and fairness. Calibration processes help ensure that ratings are applied consistently and that similar performance receives similar ratings regardless of team or department. The Company ensures that calibration is fair, transparent, and focused on consistency rather than forced distributions.
— Fairness and Equity in Ratings. Calibration ensures that ratings are fair and equitable, that bias is minimized, and that performance is evaluated consistently across the organization. The Company is committed to fair, equitable rating processes that recognize performance accurately and consistently.
10.8.3 Use of Ratings in Decision-Making
— Performance-Based Decisions. Ratings are used to support decisions related to compensation adjustments, incentives, promotions, development planning, and corrective action. Performance-based decisions ensure that rewards and opportunities are aligned with performance and that high performers are recognized and rewarded. The Company ensures that ratings are used appropriately and that decisions are based on performance evidence.
— Transparency in Rating Usage. The Company is transparent about how ratings are used in decision-making, ensuring that employees understand the connection between performance ratings and outcomes. Transparency builds trust and ensures that the performance management system is understood and accepted. The Company communicates clearly about rating usage and decision-making processes.

10.9 Performance Improvement and Corrective Action
10.9.1 Supporting Performance Improvement
— Commitment to Employee Success. When an employee's performance does not meet expectations, the Company will make reasonable efforts to support improvement through feedback, coaching, and development initiatives. The Company recognizes that most employees want to perform well and that performance issues often result from lack of clarity, support, or development rather than lack of effort. The Company is committed to helping employees improve and succeed.
— Early Identification and Intervention. Performance issues are identified early, and intervention is provided promptly to address concerns before they become serious. Early intervention is more effective than delayed action and provides employees with opportunities to improve before more serious consequences are considered. The Company ensures that performance concerns are identified and addressed promptly.
10.9.2 Performance Improvement Plans
— When PIPs Are Appropriate. If performance issues persist despite feedback and support, the employee may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). PIPs are formal, structured plans that outline specific performance deficiencies, expected improvements, support resources, timelines, and consequences of non-improvement. PIPs are not disciplinary actions but are tools for providing structured support and clear expectations. The Company uses PIPs to help employees improve and succeed.
— PIP Structure and Components. PIPs include clear identification of performance deficiencies, specific improvement objectives, measurable success criteria, support resources and development opportunities, defined timelines for improvement, regular check-ins and progress reviews, and clear consequences of non-improvement. PIPs are comprehensive, specific, and designed to provide employees with every opportunity to succeed. The Company ensures that PIPs are fair, supportive, and focused on improvement.
— Support During PIPs. Employees on PIPs receive additional support, including more frequent feedback, targeted coaching, development opportunities, and resources to help them improve. The Company is committed to providing comprehensive support during PIPs and ensuring that employees have every opportunity to succeed. Support is tailored to individual needs and is designed to address specific performance concerns.
10.9.3 Consequences of Non-Improvement
— Progressive Consequences. Failure to meet the requirements of a PIP may result in further disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Consequences are progressive, with less serious consequences considered before more serious actions. The Company ensures that consequences are fair, are based on performance evidence, and are applied consistently.
— Fair Process and Documentation. Consequences are applied following fair process, with clear communication of concerns, opportunities for improvement, and documentation of performance evidence. The Company ensures that consequences are based on objective performance assessment and that employees understand the basis for decisions. Fair process protects both employees and the Company and ensures that decisions are defensible.

10.10.1 Development Planning
— Integrated Development and Performance. Performance management supports employee development by identifying strengths, development areas, and career aspirations. Development plans are integrated with performance management, ensuring that development supports performance improvement and career growth. The Company ensures that every employee has a development plan that is aligned with performance goals and career objectives.
— Development Plan Components. Development plans include identification of strengths and development areas, specific development objectives, development activities and resources, timelines and milestones, and success criteria. Development plans are comprehensive, specific, and actionable. The Company ensures that development plans are created collaboratively and that they support both performance improvement and career growth.
10.10.2 Development Opportunities and Resources
— Diverse Development Options. Development plans may include training, skill development, stretch assignments, mentoring, role progression opportunities, or other development activities. The Company provides various development opportunities and ensures that employees have access to resources that support their development. Development opportunities are tailored to individual needs and are aligned with performance goals and career aspirations.
— Support for Development. The Company encourages employees to take ownership of their professional growth, with support from managers and HR. Development is a shared responsibility, with employees taking initiative for their own growth and managers and the Company providing resources and opportunities. The Company ensures that development is supported and that employees have access to the resources they need to grow.
10.10.3 Career Growth and Advancement
— Performance-Based Advancement. Strong performance may be recognized through career development opportunities, promotions, role expansions, or other advancement opportunities. Performance-based advancement ensures that opportunities are aligned with performance and that high performers are recognized and rewarded. The Company ensures that advancement opportunities are available and that they are based on performance and potential.
— Career Planning and Support. Performance management includes career planning discussions that help employees understand their career options, identify development needs, and plan for advancement. Career planning is integrated with performance management, ensuring that development supports career goals. The Company provides career planning support and ensures that employees understand their career options and how to advance.

10.11 Roles and Responsibilities
10.11.1 Manager Responsibilities
— Setting Expectations and Goals. Managers are responsible for setting clear performance expectations and goals that are aligned with organizational objectives and role requirements. Goal setting is collaborative, with managers and employees working together to establish goals that are challenging but achievable. The Company ensures that managers are trained in goal setting and that they establish clear, aligned goals.
— Providing Feedback and Coaching. Managers are responsible for providing regular, constructive feedback and coaching throughout the performance cycle. Feedback should be timely, specific, and developmental, and coaching should support employee success and growth. The Company ensures that managers are trained in feedback and coaching and that they provide effective support.
— Conducting Fair Evaluations. Managers are responsible for conducting fair, objective, and evidence-based performance evaluations. Evaluations should be thorough, accurate, and based on documented performance evidence. The Company ensures that managers are trained in performance evaluation and that evaluations are fair and consistent.
— Supporting Employee Development. Managers are responsible for supporting employee development by identifying development needs, providing development opportunities, and supporting career growth. Development support is integrated with performance management, ensuring that development addresses performance concerns and supports career goals. The Company ensures that managers understand their development responsibilities and that they provide effective development support.
10.11.2 Employee Responsibilities
— Understanding Performance Expectations. Employees are responsible for understanding performance expectations, goals, and standards, and for seeking clarification when needed. Understanding expectations is essential to performance success, and employees are expected to take initiative in understanding what is expected. The Company ensures that expectations are communicated clearly and that employees have opportunities to ask questions and seek clarification.
— Active Participation. Employees are responsible for actively participating in the performance management process, including goal setting, self-assessment, review discussions, and development planning. Active participation demonstrates engagement and commitment to performance and development. The Company expects employees to participate fully and to take ownership of their performance and development.
— Striving for Excellence. Employees are responsible for striving to meet or exceed agreed-upon goals and for demonstrating the behaviors and competencies expected by the Company. Performance excellence is the goal, and employees are expected to commit to achieving their goals and demonstrating expected behaviors. The Company expects employees to perform at their highest potential and to contribute to organizational success.
10.11.3 HR Responsibilities
— Policy Administration and Support. HR is responsible for administering the performance management policy, providing support and guidance to managers and employees, and ensuring that the system operates effectively and fairly. HR serves as a resource for performance management questions and concerns and ensures that the system is understood and used effectively. The Company relies on HR to ensure that performance management is administered consistently and effectively.
— Training and Development. HR is responsible for providing training and development to managers and employees on performance management processes, best practices, and effective techniques. Training ensures that all stakeholders understand the system and that they have the skills needed to participate effectively. The Company ensures that performance management training is comprehensive and that it supports effective performance management.
— Monitoring and Compliance. HR is responsible for monitoring performance management processes, ensuring compliance with policy and legal requirements, and identifying areas for improvement. Monitoring ensures that the system operates fairly and effectively and that issues are identified and addressed. The Company relies on HR to monitor performance management and to ensure compliance and effectiveness.

10.12 Fairness, Non-Discrimination, and Bias Prevention
10.12.1 Fairness and Consistency
— Fair Evaluation Standards. Performance evaluations and related decisions are conducted in a fair, consistent, and non-discriminatory manner. Fairness requires that evaluations are objective, that processes are transparent, and that employees have opportunities to be heard. Consistency requires that the same standards are applied to all employees in similar roles. The Company is committed to fair, consistent performance management that treats all employees equitably.
— Transparent Processes. Performance management processes are transparent, with clear communication of expectations, standards, and procedures. Transparency builds trust and ensures that the system is understood and accepted. The Company ensures that performance management processes are communicated clearly and that employees understand how the system works.
10.12.2 Non-Discrimination and Bias Prevention
— Prohibition of Discrimination. The Company does not tolerate bias, favoritism, or retaliation in the performance management process. Performance management decisions must not be influenced by protected characteristics such as race, gender, age, religion, disability, or other protected categories. The Company is committed to non-discriminatory performance management that evaluates performance based on merit and evidence.
— Bias Awareness and Mitigation. The Company provides training on bias awareness and mitigation, helping managers and employees recognize and address unconscious bias in performance management. Bias training helps ensure that evaluations are objective and that bias does not influence performance management decisions. The Company is committed to bias-free performance management and provides training and support to achieve this goal.
10.12.3 Addressing Concerns and Complaints
— Grievance Procedures. Employees who believe their performance evaluation was unfair may raise concerns through the appropriate internal channels, including HR. Grievance procedures provide employees with opportunities to be heard and to have concerns addressed. The Company ensures that grievance procedures are fair, accessible, and effective.
— Investigation and Resolution. Performance management concerns are investigated thoroughly and resolved fairly, with appropriate action taken when concerns are substantiated. The Company is committed to addressing concerns promptly and fairly and to taking corrective action when necessary. The Company ensures that investigations are thorough and that resolutions are fair and appropriate.

10.13 Documentation and Record Keeping
10.13.1 Performance Documentation Requirements
— Comprehensive Documentation. All performance-related discussions, evaluations, and documentation are maintained securely by HR. Documentation includes goal setting documents, feedback records, review summaries, development plans, performance improvement plans, and other performance-related materials. The Company ensures that performance documentation is comprehensive, accurate, and maintained securely.
— Confidentiality and Privacy. Performance documentation is confidential and is maintained in accordance with privacy and data protection requirements. Access to performance documentation is restricted to authorized personnel, and documentation is used only for legitimate business purposes. The Company ensures that performance documentation is handled confidentially and that employee privacy is protected.
10.13.2 Use of Performance Records
— Employment-Related Decisions. Performance records may be used for employment-related decisions, including compensation reviews, promotions, development planning, and corrective action. Performance records provide evidence for decision-making and ensure that decisions are based on documented performance. The Company ensures that performance records are used appropriately and that decisions are based on accurate, documented information.
— Compliance and Legal Purposes. Performance records may be used for compliance and legal purposes, including responding to legal inquiries, defending against claims, and meeting regulatory requirements. The Company ensures that performance records are maintained in accordance with legal requirements and that they are available for legitimate compliance and legal purposes.

10.15 Performance Management Technology and Systems
10.15.1 Performance Management Platforms
