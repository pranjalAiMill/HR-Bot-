Performance Management Policy

- PURPOSE
The purpose of this Performance Management Policy is to establish a structured and transparent framework for setting performance expectations, evaluating employee performance, providing continuous feedback, and supporting employee development. The Company aims to align individual performance with organizational goals, promote accountability, recognize achievements, and address performance gaps in a fair and consistent manner.

- SCOPE
This policy applies to all employees of the Company, including full-time and part-time employees. Performance management processes may vary by role, level, or department but will be governed by the principles outlined in this policy.

- POLICY STATEMENT
The Company believes that effective performance management is a continuous, collaborative process between employees and managers. Performance evaluations are conducted objectively, based on measurable goals, job responsibilities, and demonstrated competencies. Performance management decisions are free from bias and comply with applicable employment laws.

Failure to meet performance expectations may result in corrective action, while strong performance may be recognized through rewards, career development opportunities, or advancement.

- PERFORMANCE CYCLE
Performance management is conducted on a defined cycle, typically annually, with periodic reviews conducted throughout the year. The cycle includes goal setting, ongoing feedback, formal reviews, development planning, and performance outcomes.

- GOAL SETTING AND EXPECTATIONS
At the beginning of each performance cycle, employees and their managers will establish clear performance goals aligned with departmental and organizational objectives. Goals should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound. In addition to goal-based performance, employees are expected to demonstrate behaviors and competencies consistent with Company values, policies, and standards of conduct.

Performance expectations may be revised during the cycle to reflect changes in role, business priorities, or organizational strategy, with appropriate communication and documentation.

- ONGOING FEEDBACK AND COACHING
Managers are responsible for providing regular feedback to employees throughout the performance cycle. Feedback should be constructive, timely, and focused on both performance outcomes and behavioral expectations. Employees are encouraged to actively seek feedback and engage in open discussions regarding their performance and development.

Coaching and support may include on-the-job guidance, mentoring, training opportunities, or resource allocation to help employees succeed in their roles.

- FORMAL PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
Formal performance reviews are conducted at defined intervals, typically annually or bi-annually. The review process includes a comprehensive assessment of the employee’s performance against established goals, competencies, and role responsibilities.

Employees may be required to complete a self-assessment as part of the review process. Managers will provide written feedback and ratings, where applicable, and discuss performance outcomes with the employee in a formal review meeting.

- PERFORMANCE RATINGS AND OUTCOMES
Where a rating system is used, performance ratings are assigned based on documented performance evidence and objective criteria. Ratings are used to support decisions related to compensation adjustments, incentives, promotions, development planning, and corrective action.

The Company reserves the right to calibrate performance ratings across teams or departments to ensure consistency and fairness.

- PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT
When an employee’s performance does not meet expectations, the Company will make reasonable efforts to support improvement through feedback, coaching, and development initiatives.

If performance issues persist, the employee may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). A PIP outlines specific performance deficiencies, expected improvements, support resources, timelines, and consequences of non-improvement. Failure to meet the requirements of a PIP may result in further disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

- EMPLOYEE DEVELOPMENT AND CAREER GROWTH
Performance management supports employee development by identifying strengths, development areas, and career aspirations. Development plans may include training, skill development, stretch assignments, mentoring, or role progression opportunities.

The Company encourages employees to take ownership of their professional growth, with support from managers and HR.

- ROLE OF MANAGERS AND EMPLOYEES
Managers are responsible for setting expectations, providing feedback, conducting fair evaluations, and supporting employee development. Employees are responsible for understanding performance expectations, actively participating in the performance management process, and striving to meet or exceed agreed-upon goals.

- FAIRNESS AND NON-DISCRIMINATION
Performance evaluations and related decisions are conducted in a fair, consistent, and non-discriminatory manner. The Company does not tolerate bias, favoritism, or retaliation in the performance management process.

Employees who believe their performance evaluation was unfair may raise concerns through the appropriate internal channels, including HR.

- DOCUMENTATION AND CONFIDENTIALITY
All performance-related discussions, evaluations, and documentation are confidential and maintained securely by HR. Performance records may be used for employment-related decisions and compliance purposes.

- IMPACT ON COMPENSATION AND EMPLOYMENT
Performance outcomes may influence compensation reviews, incentives, promotions, role changes, or continued employment. Strong performance may be rewarded, while sustained poor performance may lead to corrective action or termination in accordance with Company policy.

- POLICY REVIEW AND AMENDMENT
The Company reserves the right to amend or modify this Performance Management Policy at any time to reflect changes in business needs, organizational structure, or legal requirements. Employees will be informed of material changes.
