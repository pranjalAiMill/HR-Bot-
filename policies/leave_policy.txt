Leave Policy

- This document defines the leave entitlements, rules, and approval mechanisms applicable to all employees of the organization.

Casual Leave (CL)

- Casual Leave is intended for short-term personal needs or unforeseen situations.

- Employees are entitled to 12 Casual Leave days per calendar year.

- Casual Leave may be used for personal reasons, family matters, or emergencies.

- A maximum of 2 consecutive Casual Leave days may be availed.

- Any extension beyond 2 days must be converted to another applicable leave type.

- Prior approval from the reporting manager is mandatory.

- In emergency situations, Casual Leave may be applied retrospectively with manager approval.

- Casual Leave cannot be carried forward or encashed.

- Unused Casual Leave lapses at the end of the calendar year.

- Casual Leave cannot be clubbed with Earned Leave or Sick Leave.

Sick Leave (SL)

- Sick Leave is provided to support employees during illness or medical recovery.

- Employees are entitled to 10 Sick Leave days per year.

- Sick Leave may be availed without prior approval but must be informed to the manager.

- A medical certificate is required for Sick Leave exceeding 2 consecutive days.

- Sick Leave can be carried forward up to a maximum of 30 days.

- Sick Leave is not encashable.

- Misuse of Sick Leave may result in disciplinary action.

Earned Leave (EL)

- Earned Leave is designed for planned or long-duration leave.

- Employees earn 1.5 Earned Leave days per month.

- Earned Leave can be availed after completing 3 months of service.

- Earned Leave must be taken for a minimum of 3 consecutive days.

- Prior manager approval is mandatory.

- Earned Leave can be carried forward up to 45 days.

- Excess balance beyond the cap will be forfeited.

- Earned Leave may be encashed at the time of separation as per company policy.

Maternity Leave

- Maternity Leave is provided in accordance with applicable labor laws.

- Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave.

- Leave may be availed before and/or after childbirth.

- Employees must notify HR at least 8 weeks in advance.

- Medical documentation is mandatory.

- Maternity Leave does not impact other leave balances.

- All employment benefits remain active during maternity leave.

Leave Without Pay (LWP)

- Leave Without Pay applies when all applicable leave balances are exhausted.

- LWP requires prior approval from both Manager and HR.

- LWP days will be deducted from monthly salary.

- Extended LWP may impact performance reviews and bonuses.

- Unauthorized absence may be treated as disciplinary action.

Leave Approval Process

- All leave requests must be submitted through the official HR portal.

- Approval flow:

 - Employee → Reporting Manager → HR (if applicable)

- Leaves taken without approval may be marked as Leave Without Pay.

- HR reserves the right to audit leave usage.
