Policy 8. LEAVE POLICY

Policy Notice: This Leave Policy applies to all corporate employees. All leave must be applied for, approved, and recorded through the HR Management System (HRMS). Leave taken without prior approval will be treated as unauthorized absence and will be unpaid. Supporting documentation may be required for specific leave types as detailed in each section below.

1. PURPOSE
This Corporate Leave Policy defines the entitlements, eligibility criteria, approval processes, and documentation requirements for the six categories of leave available to all corporate employees of the organization. The organization is committed to supporting employees through personal, medical, and family-related circumstances while maintaining operational continuity and treating every employee fairly and consistently.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all corporate employees including full-time, part-time, and fixed-term contract staff across all corporate functions such as Finance, Human Resources, Legal, Marketing, Technology, Operations, Sales, Strategy, and the Executive Office. Where applicable local legislation provides greater entitlements than what is stated in this policy, the legal minimum shall always apply. Part-time employees receive pro-rated leave entitlements based on their contracted hours.

3. GENERAL PRINCIPLES
All leave must be applied for through the HRMS self-service portal and approved by the line manager before the leave commences, except in genuine emergencies where same-day notification is required. Leave entitlements operate on a calendar year basis running from January 1 to December 31, unless otherwise stated in a specific leave section. Misrepresentation of the reason for any leave, or submission of fraudulent documentation, constitutes gross misconduct and may result in immediate termination of employment. All leave-related data is managed in strict confidence in accordance with the organization's Data Security Policy. The organization does not permit employees to work for another employer or conduct personal commercial activity during any period of approved paid leave.

4. LEAVE TYPES

4.1 ANNUAL LEAVE
What It Is
Annual leave is the core paid time-off entitlement provided to all corporate employees for rest, vacation, and personal time. The organization strongly encourages all employees to take their full annual leave entitlement every year. Chronic non-utilization of annual leave is not considered a mark of high performance and will be actively flagged by HR to the relevant line manager.
Entitlement
Annual leave is granted on a service-tiered basis. Employees with zero to two years of continuous service are entitled to twenty working days per calendar year. Employees with three to five years of continuous service are entitled to twenty-three working days per calendar year. Employees with six to ten years of continuous service are entitled to twenty-five working days per calendar year. Employees with more than ten years of continuous service are entitled to twenty-eight working days per calendar year. All entitlements are in addition to public holidays applicable to the employee's work location.
Eligibility
All confirmed corporate employees are eligible from their first day of employment. Annual leave accrues on a pro-rata basis during the probationary period but may only be taken during probation with prior line manager approval.
Accrual
Annual leave accrues monthly at the rate of one-twelfth of the annual entitlement per completed calendar month of service. Employees who join mid-month begin accruing from the first day of the following complete calendar month.
How to Apply
For leave of up to five consecutive days, the employee must submit the request in the HRMS with a minimum of two weeks advance notice. For leave of six or more consecutive days, the employee must submit the request with a minimum of four weeks advance notice. The line manager must approve or decline the request within five working days of submission. A maximum of three employees from the same team may be on annual leave simultaneously, subject to manager discretion and business requirements.
Restricted Leave Periods
Certain business-critical periods such as financial year-end, quarterly reporting cycles, and major product launches may be designated as Restricted Leave Periods during which annual leave requests may be declined or may require VP-level approval. Employees are informed of all restricted periods at the start of each calendar year through an official HR communication.
Carry-Over
A maximum of five unused annual leave days may be carried over to the following calendar year, provided written manager approval is obtained before December 31. Carried-over leave must be used by March 31 of the following year or it is permanently forfeited. Carry-over beyond five days requires VP-HR approval and is only granted in exceptional documented circumstances such as extended illness or business-critical project demands.
Leave Encashment
Payment in lieu of annual leave during active employment is not permitted under any circumstances. Upon resignation or termination of employment for any reason, all accrued and unused annual leave is paid out in the employee's final salary at their base daily rate.

4.2 SICK LEAVE
What It Is
Sick leave provides paid time off for corporate employees who are genuinely unable to attend work due to personal illness, injury, or a medical condition that prevents them from fulfilling their duties. The organization takes a supportive and compassionate approach to employee health while maintaining clear standards of accountability and documentation.
Entitlement
All confirmed corporate employees who have completed their probationary period are entitled to ten fully paid working days of sick leave per calendar year. From the eleventh day of sick leave in the same calendar year onward, pay reduces to fifty percent of the employee's base salary for the next ten days. Beyond twenty days of sick leave in the same calendar year, only statutory sick pay or the applicable statutory entitlement is payable. Employees who are still within their probationary period are entitled to the statutory minimum only. The sick leave entitlement resets on January 1 of each calendar year.
Notification — How to Report a Sick Day
The employee must call their line manager directly by phone no later than one hour before their scheduled start time on the first day of absence. A text message or email alone is not acceptable as the primary form of notification. Following the phone call, the employee must update their attendance status in the HRMS portal to sick leave within two hours of their scheduled start time. If the employee is physically unable to notify the organization due to the severity of their condition, a family member or designated person may notify on their behalf. The employee must follow up personally as soon as they are reasonably able to do so. The employee must also send a daily update to their line manager if the absence is expected to continue beyond the first day.
Medical Documentation Requirements
Self-declaration is accepted for absences of one to two consecutive working days, and no medical certificate is required during this period. For any absence of three or more consecutive working days, a medical certificate from a licensed medical practitioner is mandatory. For absences exceeding ten consecutive working days, the medical certificate must be accompanied by a notification to the HR Business Partner. For absences exceeding four consecutive weeks, the HR Business Partner will initiate an Occupational Health referral. The medical certificate must be submitted to HR within two working days of the employee returning to work. Failure to provide the required documentation may result in the absence being reclassified as unauthorized and unpaid.
Sick Leave During Annual Leave
If an employee falls genuinely ill during a period of pre-approved annual leave and is able to provide a medical certificate covering three or more consecutive sick days, those days may be recredited as sick leave and the corresponding annual leave days rescheduled, subject to manager and HR approval.
Return-to-Work
For any absence of three or more consecutive working days, the line manager must conduct a brief, confidential Return-to-Work interview on the employee's first day back. The purpose of the interview is to welcome the employee back, confirm they are fit to return to full duties, and identify any support or adjustments that may be needed. The interview outcome is documented in the HRMS.
Monitoring
HR reviews sick leave records on a monthly basis. Repeated patterns of short-term absence such as consistent absence on Mondays, Fridays, or days immediately before or after public holidays may trigger a welfare review conversation involving the employee, the line manager, and the HR Business Partner. The organization may use the Bradford Factor as one data point in identifying patterns but will never use it in isolation to initiate disciplinary action. Individual circumstances, including any disclosed disability or ongoing health condition, are always taken into full account.

4.3 MATERNITY LEAVE
What It Is
Maternity leave provides eligible corporate employees who are pregnant or have recently given birth with a structured and well-supported period of paid and unpaid leave to recover from childbirth and to care for their newborn child. The organization is committed to providing a maternity leave offer that genuinely supports working parents throughout this important life stage.
Entitlement
The total maternity leave entitlement is fifty-two weeks. The first sixteen weeks are paid at one hundred percent of the employee's average weekly earnings. From week seventeen through week twenty-six, leave is paid at fifty percent of the employee's average weekly earnings. From week twenty-seven through week thirty-nine, Statutory Maternity Pay is payable at the applicable statutory rate. From week forty through week fifty-two, the leave is unpaid.
Eligibility for Enhanced Pay
To qualify for the enhanced maternity pay described above, the employee must have completed a minimum of twelve months of continuous service with the organization at the time maternity leave begins. Employees with less than twelve months of continuous service at that point are entitled to Statutory Maternity Pay only from week one of their leave.
Compulsory Maternity Leave
The first two weeks immediately following childbirth are compulsory maternity leave. The employee must not return to work during this period under any circumstances, regardless of personal preference or any agreement with the line manager.
How to Notify
The employee must notify HR and their line manager no later than the end of the fifteenth week before the Expected Week of Childbirth. The notification must be accompanied by the completed Maternity Leave Notification Form, which is available on the company intranet, along with a MATB1 certificate or equivalent medical confirmation of the expected due date. HR will confirm the maternity leave start and end dates in writing within twenty-eight days of receiving the formal notification.
Keeping in Touch Days
By mutual written agreement between the employee and their line manager, up to ten Keeping in Touch days may be worked during the maternity leave period. These days do not affect the employee's maternity pay entitlement and do not trigger a formal return to work. Keeping in Touch days are paid at the employee's normal daily rate.
Return to Work
An employee returning from the first twenty-six weeks of maternity leave has the right to return to exactly the same job on exactly the same terms and conditions. An employee returning from weeks twenty-seven through fifty-two has the right to return to the same role or, where that is not reasonably practicable, a suitable alternative role on no less favorable terms and conditions. The employee must provide a minimum of eight weeks written notice to HR if they intend to return on a date that differs from the originally agreed return date. A phased return or flexible working arrangement upon return may be formally requested and will be genuinely considered by the line manager and HR on its individual merits.
Failure to Return
If an employee does not return to work following maternity leave and has received enhanced maternity pay above the statutory rate, the organization reserves the right to recover the enhanced element from the employee in accordance with the terms set out in the Maternity Leave Agreement signed before the leave commences.

4.4 CASUAL LEAVE
What It Is
Casual leave is short-term paid leave provided to corporate employees to attend to urgent personal or domestic matters that arise unexpectedly and that cannot reasonably be handled outside of working hours. Casual leave is distinct from sick leave, which is reserved for personal illness, and from annual leave, which is for planned time off. It is intended for genuine, short-notice personal needs that require the employee's personal attention during the working day.
Entitlement
All confirmed corporate employees are entitled to a maximum of seven working days of casual leave per calendar year. Casual leave cannot be accumulated, carried forward to the following year, or encashed at any time. Any unused casual leave at the end of the calendar year is forfeited entirely.
Eligibility
All confirmed corporate employees who have successfully completed their probationary period are eligible for casual leave.
What Casual Leave Can Be Used For
Casual leave is intended for situations such as attending to an urgent personal matter at a government office, bank, or other institution that operates only during business hours. It may also be used for accompanying a close family member to a medical appointment that cannot be scheduled outside of working hours, attending a child's school event or parent-teacher meeting, dealing with an urgent domestic matter at home such as an emergency repair or utility failure, or attending to personal legal or financial matters that can only be conducted during business hours.
What Casual Leave Cannot Be Used For
Casual leave must not be used for planned vacations or leisure travel, for which annual leave must be applied. It cannot be used for personal illness or medical appointments for the employee themselves, for which sick leave must be taken. It cannot be used for recurring or predictable personal commitments that could reasonably be planned in advance, or for extending a public holiday into a longer break.
How to Apply
Where the need is foreseeable, the employee must submit the casual leave request in the HRMS at least one working day in advance and obtain manager approval before the leave commences. Where the need arises on the same day and is genuinely unforeseeable, the employee must notify the line manager by phone as early as possible and must update the HRMS to casual leave within two hours of the scheduled start time.
Maximum Consecutive Days
Casual leave may not be taken for more than two consecutive working days at a time. Any personal matter requiring more than two consecutive days of absence should be submitted as annual leave.
Approval
Casual leave is subject to line manager approval. While the organization is genuinely supportive of personal needs, managers may decline a request where the employee has already exhausted their casual leave entitlement for the year, where the team is critically under-resourced on the requested date, or where the request does not appear to meet the intended purpose of this leave type. Employees who believe a casual leave request has been unreasonably declined may raise the matter with the HR Business Partner.

4.5 EMERGENCY LEAVE
What It Is
Emergency leave is paid leave provided to corporate employees to deal with a sudden, unforeseen, and serious personal emergency — particularly one involving a family member or dependent — that requires the employee's immediate personal presence and that could not have been anticipated or planned for in advance. Emergency leave is distinct from casual leave in that it is reserved exclusively for situations of genuine urgency and significant severity.
Entitlement
All confirmed corporate employees are entitled to a maximum of three working days of emergency leave per calendar year. Emergency leave cannot be accumulated, carried forward, or encashed. Any unused emergency leave at the end of the calendar year is forfeited.
Eligibility
Emergency leave is available to all corporate employees from their very first day of employment, including employees who are still within their probationary period. This reflects the organization's recognition that genuine emergencies can occur at any stage of employment.
What Emergency Leave Is For
Emergency leave is intended for situations such as a sudden serious illness, accident, or medical emergency involving a spouse, child, parent, sibling, or other close dependent that requires the employee to be physically present. It covers the hospitalization of a close family member requiring the employee's immediate attendance, a sudden and unexpected bereavement requiring the employee to be with family, or a sudden and serious domestic emergency at the employee's home such as a fire, flooding, a break-in, or a gas leak that demands immediate personal attention. It may also be used for any other sudden, serious, and genuinely unpredictable circumstance that makes it impossible for the employee to attend work and that cannot reasonably be delegated or deferred.
What Emergency Leave Is Not For
Emergency leave must not be used for planned or foreseeable personal appointments or commitments, for which casual leave or annual leave should be applied. It is not for personal illness of the employee, for which sick leave must be taken. It is not for matters that could reasonably be handled by another person, and it must not be used simply to extend a weekend or a public holiday.
How to Notify
Given the nature of emergencies, advance notice is not always possible. The employee must contact their line manager by phone as early as possible, ideally before the scheduled start time. If the line manager is unreachable, the employee must contact the next-level manager or the designated department alternate. Following the phone call, the employee must update the HRMS to emergency leave within two hours of the scheduled start time or as soon as it is practically possible to do so given the nature of the emergency. The employee must provide a brief explanation of the nature of the emergency to their line manager. Extensive personal detail is not required, but sufficient context must be provided for the manager to understand that the leave is genuine and qualifies under this policy.
Duration and Extension
Emergency leave is typically approved for one day at a time, with the expectation that the employee will return to work as soon as the immediate emergency has been addressed. If the emergency requires the employee to be absent for more than three days, the employee must contact HR to discuss whether an additional leave type such as annual leave or casual leave is applicable for any remaining days beyond the three-day emergency leave entitlement.
Documentation
The organization may request brief supporting context for emergency leave claims, such as a doctor's note in the case of a family medical emergency or a police or fire service reference number in the case of a domestic emergency. This is applied with sensitivity and in good faith, particularly in cases where an employee has claimed emergency leave multiple times within the same calendar year.
Manager Responsibilities
Line managers must treat emergency leave requests with immediacy and compassion. Emergency leave must not be unreasonably denied when the circumstances are clearly genuine. Managers are expected to proactively check on the employee's wellbeing upon their return and to offer support through the Employee Assistance Programme where it may be helpful.

4.6 PUBLIC HOLIDAYS
What It Is
Public holidays are paid days off that all corporate employees are entitled to on nationally or regionally gazetted public holidays applicable to their primary work location. Public holidays are entirely separate from and in addition to the annual leave entitlement and do not in any way reduce the employee's annual leave balance.
Entitlement
All corporate employees are entitled to all public holidays gazetted for their primary place of work. The complete list of applicable public holidays for each office location is published in the Annual Public Holiday Schedule, which HR issues at the beginning of each calendar year and posts on the company intranet. No application or approval is required for public holidays as they are automatically reflected in the HRMS and in the monthly payroll.
Eligibility
All corporate employees are entitled to public holidays from their very first day of employment, including employees on probation.
Pay on Public Holidays
Public holidays are fully paid at the employee's standard base daily rate without any requirement for the employee to apply or take any action.
Working on a Public Holiday
Where the business requires an employee to work on a gazetted public holiday — for example in client-facing, operational, or technology support roles — the employee must be given reasonable advance notice of at least forty-eight hours, except in the case of a genuine operational emergency. In return for working on the public holiday, the employee will receive either a full compensatory day off in lieu to be taken within ninety calendar days of the original public holiday, or additional pay at one and a half times the employee's standard daily rate for the day worked. The arrangement chosen must be confirmed in writing by the line manager and submitted to HR before the public holiday occurs.
Public Holiday Falling During Annual Leave
If a gazetted public holiday falls within a period of approved annual leave, the employee's annual leave balance is not reduced for that day. The day is recorded as a public holiday rather than an annual leave day, and the employee is credited with one additional annual leave day to be taken at a mutually agreed date within the same calendar year.
Public Holiday Falling During Sick Leave
If a gazetted public holiday falls within a period of certified sick leave, the sick leave entitlement for that day is paused and the day is recorded as a public holiday rather than a sick day. The sick leave count for that day is not consumed from the employee's sick leave balance.
Part-Time Employees
Part-time employees are entitled to public holidays that fall on their scheduled working days. Where a public holiday falls on a day that a part-time employee does not ordinarily work, they are not entitled to a day off in lieu unless this has been expressly agreed in their individual contract of employment.

5. LEAVE APPLICATION PROCESS
5.1 Standard Leave — Annual Leave and Casual Leave
The employee must log in to the HRMS Self-Service Portal and navigate to the leave section. They select the leave type, enter the start and end dates, add any relevant notes, and submit the request. The line manager receives an automatic notification and must approve or decline within five working days. The employee receives an automated confirmation or decline notification. Once approved, the leave is immediately reflected in the employee's leave balance and the team shared calendar.
5.2 Emergency and Same-Day Leave — Sick Leave, Emergency Leave, and Unplanned Casual Leave
The employee must call the line manager by phone before or as close to the scheduled start time as possible. Following the call, the employee must update the HRMS with the correct leave type within two hours of the scheduled start time. Any required documentation must be submitted to HR upon returning to work.
5.3 Maternity Leave
The employee speaks with the HR Business Partner and line manager upon confirming the pregnancy, which is recommended by week twelve of pregnancy and is mandatory no later than week twenty-five. The Maternity Leave Notification Form and MATB1 certificate are submitted to HR no later than the fifteenth week before the Expected Week of Childbirth. HR confirms the leave dates and pay entitlement in writing within twenty-eight days of receiving the completed documentation. Payroll is updated automatically by HR once the confirmation is complete.

6. MISUSE OF LEAVE
The organization takes misuse of leave entitlements very seriously. The following constitute leave policy violations and may result in disciplinary action up to and including dismissal for gross misconduct. Taking leave without approval is treated as unauthorized absence, which is unpaid and subject to formal disciplinary action. Misrepresenting the reason for leave — such as claiming sick leave when the employee is not genuinely unwell, or claiming emergency leave for a non-qualifying situation — is a disciplinary offense. Submitting false, altered, or fraudulent medical certificates or supporting documentation constitutes gross misconduct. Working for another employer or conducting personal commercial activity during any period of approved paid leave is prohibited and will be treated as a serious breach of the employment contract. Deliberately fragmenting absences to avoid medical certificate requirements or attendance monitoring triggers is also treated as a disciplinary matter.

7. CONFIDENTIALITY
All leave records — particularly those relating to medical conditions, pregnancy, and maternity — are maintained in strict confidence within the HRMS. Access to leave records is restricted to the employee themselves, their line manager, the HR Business Partner, and the Payroll team on a strictly need-to-know basis. Unauthorized disclosure of any employee's leave information to colleagues or to unauthorized third parties is a disciplinary offense and may also constitute a breach of applicable data protection legislation.

8. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
Employees are responsible for applying for leave on time through the HRMS, using leave for its intended and approved purpose, providing required documentation promptly upon return, and following all notification procedures set out in this policy.
Line managers are responsible for reviewing and actioning leave requests within the stipulated timeframes, managing team coverage during periods of absence, applying this policy consistently and fairly across the team, and actively supporting employees in taking their full annual leave entitlement each year.
The HR Business Partner is responsible for handling complex or disputed leave situations, coordinating maternity leave arrangements, ensuring consistent policy application across all departments, and maintaining confidential and accurate leave records in the HRMS.
The Payroll team is responsible for accurately processing all leave-related pay adjustments in the monthly payroll cycle in accordance with the Payroll Policy.

9. POLICY REVIEW
This policy is reviewed annually by the HR Department. Changes to applicable employment legislation, the organization's operating model, or feedback from employees and managers may trigger an interim review before the scheduled annual review date. Employees will be notified of any material revisions through official internal communications and will be required to re-acknowledge the updated policy through the HRMS.
