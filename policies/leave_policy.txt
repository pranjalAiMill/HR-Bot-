8. LEAVE POLICY

8.1 Purpose and Organizational Philosophy
8.1.1 Why This Policy Exists
— The Foundation of Work-Life Balance. The Leave Policy is one of the most fundamentally important documents in the Company's entire policy framework. It establishes the principles, standards, and frameworks through which employees are entitled to time away from work for rest, recovery, personal responsibilities, family needs, and life events — while ensuring that the Company maintains operational effectiveness and meets its business obligations. Where the Employment Policy referenced as HR-EP-001 governs the terms and conditions of employment, and the Performance Management Policy referenced as HR-PMP-001 governs how performance is evaluated, this policy governs how employees balance their work responsibilities with their personal lives, health needs, and family obligations. Leave entitlements are not discretionary benefits provided at the Company's convenience. They are fundamental rights that support employee well-being, work-life balance, and sustainable employment relationships, and this policy is the framework through which those rights are protected and administered.
— The Organizational Imperative. The Company operates in a business environment where employee well-being, engagement, and retention are critical to organizational success. Research consistently demonstrates that employees who have adequate time for rest, recovery, personal responsibilities, and family needs are more productive, more engaged, and more committed to their organizations. A workforce that is overworked, stressed, or unable to attend to personal and family needs cannot sustain high performance, cannot maintain work-life balance, and cannot contribute at their highest potential. This policy exists to ensure that the Company provides comprehensive, fair, and legally compliant leave entitlements that support employee well-being while maintaining operational effectiveness. The Company recognizes that supporting employees' need for time away from work is not only a legal obligation but also a strategic investment in workforce health, engagement, and retention.
— The Human Imperative. This policy has been written with a genuine recognition that employees are whole people with lives, responsibilities, and needs that extend beyond their work. Employees need time to rest and recover from work demands, to attend to personal health and medical needs, to care for family members, to celebrate important life events, to grieve losses, to manage emergencies, and to maintain the balance between work and personal life that enables sustainable, healthy, and fulfilling employment. This policy is not a declaration of the Company's generosity. It is a recognition of human needs and a commitment to supporting employees as whole people, not merely as workers. The Company invests in this policy, and in the systems and processes that support it, because it genuinely believes that employees who are supported in their personal lives are better able to contribute effectively to organizational success.
— The Philosophy of Mutual Respect and Balance. Leave administration requires a balance between employee needs and business requirements. The Company is committed to providing generous leave entitlements and flexible leave administration, while employees are expected to use leave responsibly, to provide appropriate notice, and to consider business needs when planning leave. This policy is built on the conviction that both parties to the employment relationship have responsibilities — the Company's responsibility to provide adequate leave and fair administration, and the employee's responsibility to use leave appropriately and to communicate effectively about leave needs. Leave administration functions most effectively when both parties respect each other's needs and work together to find solutions that balance individual and organizational requirements.
— A Living and Evolving Document. Like all enterprise policies, the Leave Policy is a living document that will be reviewed, updated, and strengthened as applicable employment law evolves, as the Company's business needs change, as workforce demographics and needs shift, and as practical experience provides insights into how the policy can be made more effective. Every employee is expected to engage with this policy seriously, to understand their leave entitlements, and to treat it as a genuine and relevant guide to one of the most important aspects of their employment relationship.

8.2 Scope and Applicability
8.2.1 Who This Policy Applies To
— Universal Application to All Employees. This policy applies universally and without exception to every individual engaged by the Company in any employment capacity — regardless of seniority, tenure, professional standing, department, business unit, geographic location, or technical background. The most senior executive in the organization is entitled to the same leave entitlements and subject to the same leave administration standards as the most junior team member, and the Company's commitment to fair and consistent leave administration extends to every level of the organization. While specific leave entitlements may vary based on employment classification, length of service, role requirements, and applicable law, the fundamental framework of leave rights and responsibilities applies universally.
— All Categories of Employment Covered. This policy covers permanent full-time employees, part-time employees, employees on fixed-term or rolling contracts, interns and trainees, and any other individual engaged in an employment relationship with the Company. Each of these individuals, regardless of the nature or duration of their employment, is entitled to leave benefits appropriate to their employment classification and circumstances, subject to applicable legal requirements. The specific application of certain policy provisions may vary based on employment classification — for example, part-time employees may receive prorated leave entitlements — but the fundamental framework of leave rights and administration applies to all employees.
— Seniority Confers No Exemption. Senior leaders, managers, and those with elevated organizational responsibilities are not exempt from this policy — they are subject to the same leave administration standards and are expected to model appropriate leave usage and work-life balance. Leaders who work excessive hours, who do not take adequate leave, or who create expectations that discourage leave usage send a message that leave is not valued, which is a message the Company will not tolerate being sent at any level. Leaders are expected to take their own leave, to support their team members' leave usage, and to create an environment in which work-life balance is genuinely valued.
8.2.2 Where This Policy Applies
— All Work Locations and Arrangements. This policy applies in every location and arrangement in which Company work is performed — whether on Company premises, at client sites, at home in a remote or hybrid arrangement, while traveling on Company business, or in any other location where an employee is performing work on behalf of the Company. Leave entitlements and administration do not vary based on the employee's physical location, and remote and hybrid employees have the same leave rights and responsibilities as on-site employees.
— All Phases of the Employment Relationship. This policy applies throughout the entire lifecycle of the employment relationship — from the first day of employment through active employment, during periods of leave, and through the conclusion of the employment relationship. Leave entitlements begin accruing from the first day of employment, and employees are entitled to use leave as soon as it is accrued, subject to any probationary period restrictions that may apply. Leave administration continues throughout employment, and final leave balances are settled upon termination of employment in accordance with applicable law and Company policy.
8.2.3 Relationship With Other Policies
— An Integrated Leave Framework. This Leave Policy operates as one component of the Company's broader human resources and employment framework. It must be read and applied in conjunction with the Employment Policy referenced as HR-EP-001, the Code of Conduct referenced as HR-COC-001, the Work From Home Policy referenced as HR-WFH-001, and all other applicable Company policies. Where a specific policy provides more detailed guidance on a particular aspect of leave — such as the Work From Home Policy's treatment of leave during remote work — that specific policy governs. Where a leave concern arises that falls outside the scope of any specific policy, this Leave Policy provides the governing framework.
— Conflict Resolution. In the event of any apparent conflict between the standards established in this policy and those established in any other Company policy, or between this policy and applicable employment law, employees must seek guidance from HR before taking action. Employees must not act unilaterally on their own interpretation of any apparent conflict between policies and must not use an apparent conflict as justification for departing from leave standards that would otherwise apply. Where applicable employment law provides more generous leave entitlements than this policy, the legal provisions shall prevail.

8.3 General Leave Principles and Administration
8.3.1 Fundamental Leave Principles
— Leave as a Right and Responsibility. Leave entitlements are fundamental rights that employees earn through their service to the Company, and the Company is committed to providing leave benefits that meet or exceed legal requirements and that support employee well-being. At the same time, leave usage is a responsibility that requires employees to plan appropriately, to communicate effectively, to consider business needs, and to use leave in a manner that balances personal needs with organizational requirements. The Company recognizes that both parties to the employment relationship have obligations — the Company's obligation to provide adequate leave and fair administration, and the employee's obligation to use leave responsibly and to communicate about leave needs.
— Fair and Consistent Administration. Leave administration must be fair, consistent, and transparent, applying the same standards and processes to all employees regardless of role, department, or relationship with management. The Company is committed to administering leave consistently, to avoiding favoritism or arbitrary decisions, and to ensuring that all employees understand their leave entitlements and how to access them. Managers are expected to apply leave policies fairly and consistently, and HR monitors leave administration to ensure compliance with policy standards.
— Balance Between Employee Needs and Business Requirements. Leave administration requires balancing employee needs for time away from work with business requirements for operational continuity and service delivery. The Company is committed to approving leave requests whenever possible, recognizing that employees need time for rest, personal responsibilities, and life events. At the same time, the Company must ensure that leave approvals do not compromise operational effectiveness, client service, or team performance. The Company works with employees to find solutions that balance individual and organizational needs, and employees are expected to be flexible and considerate of business requirements when planning leave.
8.3.2 Leave Request and Approval Process
— Advance Notice Requirements. Employees are required to request leave in advance whenever possible, providing sufficient notice to enable the Company to plan for coverage and to ensure operational continuity. Advance notice requirements vary by leave type and duration, with longer leave periods requiring earlier notice. Emergency and unforeseen circumstances are recognized as exceptions to advance notice requirements, and the Company is committed to accommodating emergency leave needs promptly and compassionately. Employees must not make personal travel arrangements or commitments until leave is approved, and the Company is not responsible for costs incurred for arrangements made before leave approval.
— Leave Request Submission. All leave requests must be submitted through the Company's designated HR system or in writing to the employee's reporting manager and HR. Leave requests must include the type of leave, the dates of absence, the reason for leave where required, and any supporting documentation that may be necessary. Employees are responsible for ensuring that leave requests are submitted accurately and completely, and for providing any additional information that may be required for approval. The Company maintains systems and processes that make leave request submission easy and accessible, and employees are expected to use these systems appropriately.
— Approval Authority and Decision-Making. Leave requests are reviewed and approved by the employee's immediate supervisor, with HR providing oversight and support to ensure policy compliance. Approval decisions are based on business requirements, workload considerations, staffing needs, the employee's leave balance, and the reasonableness of the request. Managers are expected to approve leave requests whenever possible, recognizing that employees need time away from work, and to work with employees to find solutions when direct approval is not immediately possible. Extended or exceptional leave requests may require additional approvals from senior management or HR, and the Company maintains processes for handling complex leave situations.
— Response Timeframes. The Company is committed to responding to leave requests promptly, typically within three business days for standard requests and within one business day for emergency requests. Managers are expected to review and respond to leave requests in a timely manner, and employees are entitled to know the status of their leave requests. Delays in leave approval that are not justified by business needs are not acceptable, and employees who experience unreasonable delays may escalate their concerns to HR.
8.3.3 Leave Record Keeping and Tracking
— Accurate Leave Records. The Company maintains accurate records of all leave accruals, usage, and balances for every employee, ensuring that leave entitlements are tracked correctly and that employees have visibility into their leave status. Leave records are maintained in the HRMS, are accessible to employees and managers, and are updated in real-time as leave is requested, approved, and used. Employees are responsible for reviewing their leave records regularly and for reporting any discrepancies to HR immediately.
— Leave Balance Visibility. Employees have access to their leave balances through the HRMS, enabling them to see accrued leave, used leave, available leave, and any carryover or expiration information. Leave balance visibility enables employees to plan their leave usage effectively, to understand their entitlements, and to make informed decisions about leave requests. The Company provides regular leave balance statements and notifications to help employees stay informed about their leave status.
— Audit and Compliance. HR conducts regular audits of leave records to ensure accuracy, to identify patterns or concerns, and to ensure compliance with policy and legal requirements. Leave audits help identify discrepancies, prevent abuse, and ensure that leave administration is fair and consistent. Employees are expected to cooperate with leave audits and to provide accurate information when requested.

8.4 Annual Leave and Paid Time Off
8.4.1 Purpose and Philosophy of Annual Leave
— Annual Leave as Essential Rest and Recovery. Annual leave, also referred to as Paid Time Off (PTO) or vacation leave, is provided to allow employees time away from work for rest, relaxation, personal matters, vacation, and planned time away from work. Annual leave recognizes that employees need regular breaks from work to rest, recover, recharge, and attend to personal responsibilities and interests. Research consistently demonstrates that employees who take regular vacation time are more productive, more engaged, and healthier than those who do not. The Company is committed to encouraging employees to use their annual leave regularly, recognizing that adequate rest and recovery are essential to sustained performance and well-being.
— Annual Leave as a Right, Not a Privilege. Annual leave is not a discretionary benefit that the Company may grant or withhold at its convenience. It is a fundamental right that employees earn through their service, and the Company is committed to providing annual leave entitlements that meet or exceed legal requirements and that support employee well-being. The Company recognizes that employees need and deserve time away from work, and is committed to approving annual leave requests whenever possible, subject only to legitimate business requirements that cannot be accommodated through alternative arrangements.
8.4.2 Eligibility and Accrual
— Universal Eligibility. Annual leave is available to all full-time employees from their first day of employment. Part-time employees receive annual leave on a prorated basis in accordance with their scheduled working hours and employment classification. The Company recognizes that all employees, regardless of their employment classification, need time for rest and personal activities, and provides annual leave entitlements that are fair and appropriate to each employment category.
— Accrual Mechanisms. Annual leave accrues periodically in line with the Company's payroll cycle, beginning from the employee's date of hire. Accrual rates are established based on length of service, with employees typically accruing leave at a standard rate that may increase with tenure. Accrual for partial years of service is prorated based on the employee's start date and the number of months worked during the accrual period. The Company maintains clear accrual schedules that are communicated to employees and that enable employees to understand how leave accrues and when it becomes available for use.
— Maximum Accrual Limits. Annual leave accrual is subject to maximum accumulation limits designed to encourage regular leave usage and to prevent excessive leave balances that create operational and financial liabilities. Once an employee reaches the maximum allowable balance, additional annual leave will not accrue until the balance is reduced below the maximum. Maximum accrual limits are established based on operational considerations, financial planning, and best practices, and are communicated clearly to employees. Employees are encouraged to use annual leave regularly to maintain work-life balance and to avoid reaching maximum accrual limits.
8.4.3 Annual Leave Entitlements
— Standard Entitlement. Eligible full-time employees are entitled to fifteen (15) working days of annual leave per calendar year, based on a standard five-day workweek. Annual leave entitlement is calculated based on working days, excluding weekends and public holidays, unless otherwise specified in the employment contract or applicable law. The Company may revise annual leave entitlement based on years of service, role requirements, or business considerations, and any changes will be communicated in writing and applied consistently.
— Enhanced Entitlements Based on Service. The Company recognizes that long-serving employees have contributed significantly to the organization and may provide enhanced annual leave entitlements based on years of service. Enhanced entitlements typically begin after a specified period of service, such as five years or ten years, and provide additional annual leave days as a recognition of service and contribution. Enhanced entitlements are communicated clearly to employees and are applied automatically based on service records.
— Role-Based Entitlements. Certain roles or employment categories may have different annual leave entitlements based on business requirements, market practices, or contractual arrangements. Role-based entitlements are established in employment contracts, are communicated clearly to affected employees, and are applied consistently within each role category. The Company ensures that role-based entitlements are fair, are justified by business needs, and do not create arbitrary or discriminatory differences.
8.4.4 Usage of Annual Leave
— Purpose and Flexibility. Annual leave may be used for vacation, personal commitments, planned time away from work, or any other purpose that the employee chooses. The Company does not require employees to justify the purpose of annual leave usage, recognizing that employees need flexibility to use leave for various personal needs and interests. Annual leave provides employees with the freedom to plan time away from work according to their personal preferences and circumstances.
— Minimum Increments and Partial Days. Annual leave may be taken as full days or partial days, subject to minimum increment requirements established by the Company. Minimum increments are typically half-days or full days, and the Company maintains clear guidelines on partial day leave usage. Partial day leave enables employees to attend to personal matters without using a full day of leave, providing flexibility while maintaining operational effectiveness.
— Advance Planning and Notice. Employees are encouraged to plan annual leave in advance and to submit leave requests as far in advance as possible to enable the Company to plan for coverage and to ensure operational continuity. Longer periods of annual leave, such as extended vacations, require earlier notice to enable adequate planning. The Company recognizes that advance planning is not always possible and accommodates last-minute leave requests when business requirements allow.
— Peak Periods and Blackout Dates. The Company may establish peak periods or blackout dates during which annual leave requests may be restricted or require additional approval due to business requirements, seasonal demands, or critical project deadlines. Peak periods and blackout dates are communicated to employees in advance, and the Company works with employees to find alternative arrangements when possible. The Company minimizes blackout periods and ensures that they are justified by genuine business needs.
8.4.5 Carryover and Expiration
— Carryover Provisions. Unused annual leave may be carried forward to the next calendar year up to a maximum cap determined by the Company. Carryover provisions enable employees to accumulate leave for extended vacations or to save leave for future needs, while maximum caps prevent excessive accumulation that creates operational and financial liabilities. Carryover limits are established based on operational considerations and are communicated clearly to employees. Employees are encouraged to use annual leave regularly rather than accumulating large balances, recognizing that regular rest and recovery are essential to well-being and performance.
— Expiration of Unused Leave. Annual leave that exceeds the maximum carryover limit or that is not used within a specified timeframe may expire and be forfeited. Expiration policies are designed to encourage regular leave usage and to prevent excessive accumulation, and are communicated clearly to employees with adequate notice. The Company provides regular reminders to employees about leave balances and expiration dates to help employees plan leave usage effectively.
8.4.6 Annual Leave at Separation
— Payout Upon Termination. Upon resignation, retirement, or termination of employment, accrued and unused annual leave will be paid out in the final settlement where required by applicable law. Where not legally required, payout will be governed by Company policy, which typically provides for payout of accrued annual leave up to the maximum carryover limit. Payout calculations are based on the employee's current salary rate and accrued leave balance at the time of separation, and are processed through the final payroll cycle.
— Final Leave Settlement. Final leave settlement is calculated and processed as part of the employee's final pay, and employees receive a detailed statement showing leave accruals, usage, and payout calculations. The Company ensures that final leave settlements are accurate, are processed promptly, and comply with all applicable legal requirements. Employees who have questions about their final leave settlement may contact HR for clarification and resolution.

8.5 Sick Leave and Medical Leave
8.5.1 Purpose and Philosophy of Sick Leave
— Sick Leave as Health Protection. Sick leave is provided to allow employees time away from work to recover from illness or injury, to attend medical appointments, to care for personal health needs, and to prevent the spread of illness in the workplace. Sick leave recognizes that employees need time to address health issues without financial penalty, that coming to work while ill can spread illness to colleagues and clients, and that adequate recovery time is essential to health and performance. The Company is committed to providing generous sick leave entitlements and to administering sick leave with compassion and understanding, recognizing that health issues are often unpredictable and that employees need support during illness.
— Supporting Employee Health and Well-Being. The Company recognizes that employee health and well-being are essential to individual fulfillment and organizational success, and is committed to supporting employees in maintaining and recovering their health. Sick leave is not merely a benefit — it is a fundamental support for employee health, and the Company administers sick leave in a manner that prioritizes health and recovery over strict policy enforcement. The Company encourages employees to use sick leave when needed, to take adequate time for recovery, and to prioritize health over work demands.
8.5.2 Eligibility and Accrual
— Universal Eligibility. Sick leave is available to all eligible employees from their first day of employment, recognizing that health issues can arise at any time and that all employees need protection against loss of income due to illness. Part-time employees receive sick leave on a prorated basis or as required by applicable law, ensuring that all employees have access to sick leave protection regardless of their employment classification.
— Accrual Mechanisms. Sick leave accrues periodically from the first day of employment, typically at a rate that provides adequate protection against illness while encouraging appropriate usage. Accrual rates may vary based on employment classification, length of service, or applicable legal requirements. Sick leave accrual is subject to a maximum accumulation cap that enables employees to build a reserve for extended illness while preventing excessive accumulation. The Company maintains clear accrual schedules that enable employees to understand how sick leave accrues and when it becomes available.
8.5.3 Permissible Uses of Sick Leave
— Employee's Own Illness or Injury. Sick leave may be used for the employee's own physical or mental illness, injury, or medical condition, including acute illness, chronic conditions, injuries, mental health needs, and any other health-related absence. The Company recognizes that health issues are diverse and that employees need flexibility to use sick leave for various health needs. Employees are not required to disclose specific diagnoses or medical details, and the Company respects employee privacy while ensuring that sick leave is used appropriately.
— Medical Appointments and Preventive Care. Sick leave may be used for medical, dental, or vision appointments, including routine check-ups, preventive care, specialist consultations, diagnostic procedures, and treatment appointments. The Company recognizes that preventive care is essential to maintaining health and encourages employees to attend regular medical appointments. Employees are encouraged to schedule medical appointments in advance when possible and to provide notice to their managers, but the Company accommodates both planned and unplanned medical appointments.
— Family Member Care. Sick leave may be used to care for an immediate family member who is ill, injured, or requires medical attention, recognizing that employees have responsibilities to care for family members and that family health needs can be urgent and unpredictable. Family member care includes caring for children, spouses, parents, and other immediate family members as defined by Company policy and applicable law. The Company administers family care leave with compassion, recognizing that caring for ill family members is an important responsibility.
— Domestic Violence and Sexual Assault Support. Where applicable under law, sick leave may be used for recovery or support related to domestic violence or sexual assault, recognizing that these situations require time for medical care, legal proceedings, safety planning, and recovery. The Company administers this leave with sensitivity and confidentiality, ensuring that employees who need this support receive it without judgment or additional burden.
8.5.4 Notification and Documentation Requirements
— Notification Requirements. Employees must notify their supervisor as soon as possible when sick leave is required, preferably before the start of the scheduled workday or as soon as the need becomes apparent. The Company recognizes that illness can be sudden and unpredictable, and accommodates last-minute notifications when necessary. Employees are expected to provide reasonable notice when possible and to communicate about expected duration when known. For planned medical appointments, advance notice should be provided whenever feasible to enable operational planning.
— Medical Documentation Requirements. The Company may require medical documentation for absences exceeding a specified number of consecutive days, typically three to five days, or in cases where there is a suspected pattern or misuse of sick leave. Medical documentation should confirm the dates of absence and fitness to return to work, without requiring disclosure of specific diagnoses or detailed medical information. The Company respects employee privacy and requests only the minimum documentation necessary to verify the need for sick leave and to ensure appropriate return-to-work planning.
— Return-to-Work Clearances. In cases of extended illness, serious injury, or certain medical conditions, the Company may require a fitness-for-duty clearance or medical clearance prior to resumption of duties. Return-to-work clearances ensure that employees are medically fit to perform their job duties safely and effectively, and protect both the employee and the workplace. The Company works with employees and healthcare providers to facilitate appropriate return-to-work planning and to ensure that return-to-work processes are supportive and reasonable.
8.5.5 Carryover and Payout
— Carryover Provisions. Unused sick leave carries forward year to year up to the applicable maximum accumulation cap, enabling employees to build a reserve for extended illness or unexpected health needs. Carryover provisions recognize that health issues can be unpredictable and that employees need protection against extended illness. Maximum accumulation caps prevent excessive accumulation while providing adequate protection, and are established based on operational considerations and best practices.
— Payout Upon Separation. Sick leave is generally not paid out upon separation from employment unless required by applicable law. The Company recognizes that sick leave is intended for health protection during employment and that payout upon separation is not typically required or provided. However, the Company complies with all applicable legal requirements regarding sick leave payout, and employees are informed of their rights and entitlements upon separation.

8.6 Casual Leave and Personal Leave
8.6.1 Purpose and Philosophy of Casual Leave
— Casual Leave as Flexibility for Unplanned Needs. Casual leave is intended to provide flexibility for employees to attend to short-term personal matters, emergencies, or unforeseen situations that cannot be planned in advance. Casual leave recognizes that employees have personal responsibilities and needs that arise unexpectedly and that require immediate attention, and provides a mechanism for addressing these needs without using annual leave or sick leave. Casual leave enables employees to manage personal matters efficiently while maintaining work responsibilities.
— Supporting Work-Life Balance. Casual leave supports work-life balance by providing employees with flexibility to attend to personal matters without excessive planning or justification. The Company recognizes that employees have diverse personal responsibilities and that providing flexibility in leave usage supports employee well-being and engagement. Casual leave is administered with understanding and flexibility, recognizing that personal matters can be urgent and important.
8.6.2 Eligibility and Allocation
— Annual Allocation. Casual leave is granted annually to eligible employees and is credited at the beginning of the calendar year or prorated for new hires joining mid-year. Casual leave does not accrue over time but is provided as an annual allocation that employees can use throughout the year. The annual allocation is established based on operational considerations and employee needs, and is communicated clearly to employees at the beginning of each year.
— Standard Allocation. Eligible full-time employees typically receive a standard allocation of casual leave days per calendar year, with part-time employees receiving prorated allocations based on their working hours. The standard allocation is designed to provide adequate flexibility for personal matters while maintaining operational effectiveness. The Company may adjust casual leave allocations based on business needs, but any changes are communicated clearly and applied consistently.
8.6.3 Usage Guidelines
— Permissible Uses. Casual leave may be used for personal errands, family responsibilities, religious or cultural observances, urgent personal matters, or other short-term personal needs that cannot be planned in advance. Casual leave is not intended for leisure travel or extended vacations, which should be covered under annual leave. The Company provides flexibility in casual leave usage, recognizing that employees have diverse personal needs and that strict limitations can create unnecessary burden.
— Duration and Limitations. Casual leave is typically limited to short durations, typically one to two days at a time, and may not be combined with weekends or holidays to extend time off without valid justification. The Company recognizes that some personal matters may require longer periods, and accommodates extended casual leave when justified by circumstances. Approval of casual leave is subject to business requirements, and the Company works with employees to find solutions that balance personal and business needs.
8.6.4 Expiry and Non-Payout
— Annual Expiry. Unused casual leave lapses at the end of the calendar year and does not carry forward to the next year. Expiry policies encourage employees to use casual leave during the year and prevent accumulation that could create operational challenges. The Company provides regular reminders about casual leave balances and expiration dates to help employees plan usage effectively.
— No Payout Upon Separation. Casual leave is not paid out upon separation from employment, recognizing that casual leave is intended for use during employment and that payout is not required or provided. Employees are encouraged to use casual leave during the year to address personal matters and to maintain work-life balance.

8.7 Maternity Leave
8.7.1 Purpose and Philosophy of Maternity Leave
— Supporting Women During Pregnancy and Childbirth. Maternity leave is provided to support female employees during pregnancy, childbirth, and postnatal recovery, in accordance with applicable labor laws and the Company's commitment to supporting employees during important life events. The Company recognizes that pregnancy and childbirth are significant life events that require time for health, recovery, and bonding with the newborn, and is committed to providing generous maternity leave entitlements that support both the employee and the newborn. Maternity leave is not merely a legal requirement — it is a fundamental support for working mothers and their families.
— Commitment to Health, Well-Being, and Job Security. The Company is committed to ensuring the health, well-being, and job security of employees during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postnatal period. The Company recognizes that adequate maternity leave is essential to maternal and infant health, that job security during leave is critical to financial stability, and that supporting working mothers is both a legal obligation and a moral responsibility. The Company administers maternity leave with compassion, understanding, and support, recognizing that this is a significant and important time in an employee's life.
8.7.2 Eligibility and Entitlement
— Eligibility Criteria. Maternity leave is available to female employees who meet the eligibility criteria prescribed under applicable labor laws and who have completed the required period of service, where applicable. Eligibility criteria typically include minimum service requirements, which may vary by jurisdiction, and the Company ensures that all eligible employees are informed of their maternity leave rights and entitlements. The Company complies with all applicable legal requirements and provides maternity leave that meets or exceeds legal minimums.
— Standard Entitlement. Female employees are entitled to twenty-six (26) weeks of paid maternity leave, in accordance with applicable labor laws. This leave includes both pre-natal and post-natal periods and must be availed in accordance with legal requirements and medical advice. The leave may be taken before the expected date of delivery and continued after childbirth, as medically advised, providing flexibility to accommodate individual circumstances and medical needs. The Company recognizes that pregnancy and childbirth are individual experiences and provides flexibility in leave timing while ensuring compliance with legal requirements.
— Adoption and Commissioning Motherhood. In cases of adoption or commissioning motherhood (where applicable under law), maternity leave entitlement shall be provided as prescribed by statutory provisions and Company policy. The Company recognizes that adoption and surrogacy are also significant life events that require time for bonding and adjustment, and provides leave entitlements that support these situations. Leave entitlements for adoption and commissioning motherhood are communicated clearly to eligible employees and are administered with the same support and understanding as traditional maternity leave.
8.7.3 Payment and Benefits During Maternity Leave
— Full Payment During Leave. Maternity leave is fully paid at the employee's regular rate of pay, subject to applicable laws and Company policy. Payment will be processed through the regular payroll cycle during the approved maternity leave period, ensuring that employees receive continuous income during their leave. The Company recognizes that financial stability during maternity leave is essential to employee well-being and family security, and is committed to providing paid maternity leave that supports employees during this important time.
— Continuation of Benefits. During maternity leave, all employment benefits, including health insurance and other applicable benefits, will continue as per Company policy and legal requirements. Benefit continuation ensures that employees and their families maintain health coverage and other protections during maternity leave, and the Company is committed to ensuring that benefit continuation is seamless and uninterrupted. Employees are informed of benefit continuation arrangements and are supported in maintaining their benefits during leave.
8.7.4 Application and Documentation
— Early Notification and Planning. Employees must notify their reporting manager and HR in writing as early as reasonably possible regarding their intention to avail maternity leave. Early notification enables the Company to plan for coverage, to ensure operational continuity, and to provide appropriate support to the employee. The Company recognizes that pregnancy timing can be sensitive and provides a supportive environment for employees to communicate their maternity leave plans.
— Medical Certification. Medical certification confirming pregnancy and the expected date of delivery may be required to process the maternity leave request. Medical certification helps the Company plan for leave timing, ensures that leave is taken in accordance with medical advice, and supports appropriate return-to-work planning. The Company requests only the minimum medical information necessary for leave administration and respects employee privacy throughout the process.
8.7.5 Job Protection and Return to Work
— Guaranteed Reinstatement. Upon return from maternity leave, the employee will be reinstated to the same position or an equivalent position with no loss of pay, seniority, or service benefits, as mandated by law and Company policy. Job protection ensures that employees can take maternity leave without fear of job loss or career impact, and the Company is committed to ensuring that maternity leave does not negatively affect an employee's career progression or opportunities. The Company recognizes that supporting working mothers is essential to gender equity and organizational success.
— Return-to-Work Planning. Employees are expected to inform their manager and HR of their intended return-to-work date in advance, enabling the Company to plan for their return and to ensure a smooth transition back to work. The Company provides return-to-work support, including orientation to any changes that occurred during leave, reintroduction to team and projects, and flexible arrangements that support the transition back to work. Where required, a fitness-for-duty or medical clearance may be requested prior to resumption of duties, ensuring that the employee is medically fit to return to work safely.
8.7.6 Extension and Additional Leave
— Extension Requests. Any extension of leave beyond the statutory maternity leave period must be requested separately and will be subject to approval based on business needs and employee circumstances. Extension requests may be covered under accrued paid leave, such as annual leave or sick leave, or may be granted as Leave Without Pay, as applicable. The Company works with employees to find solutions that balance personal needs with business requirements, and provides flexibility when possible.
— Flexible Return Arrangements. The Company may provide flexible return arrangements, such as part-time return, phased return, or remote work options, to support employees transitioning back to work after maternity leave. Flexible arrangements recognize that the transition back to work can be challenging and that providing flexibility supports both the employee and the organization. The Company is committed to working with employees to find return arrangements that work for all parties.

8.8 Paternity Leave
8.8.1 Purpose and Philosophy of Paternity Leave
— Supporting Fathers and Partners. Paternity leave is provided to support eligible employees following the birth of a child or the legal adoption of a child, recognizing that fathers and partners play an important role in supporting the primary caregiver, bonding with the child, and managing family responsibilities during the immediate post-birth or post-adoption period. The Company recognizes that supporting fathers and partners is essential to gender equity, family well-being, and work-life balance, and is committed to providing paternity leave that enables employees to be present and engaged during this important time.
— Promoting Gender Equity and Shared Parenting. Paternity leave promotes gender equity by enabling fathers and partners to share in parenting responsibilities and by reducing the burden on mothers as the sole caregivers. The Company recognizes that shared parenting benefits families, children, and society, and is committed to providing paternity leave that supports this important goal. Paternity leave is not merely a benefit — it is a commitment to gender equity and family support.
8.8.2 Eligibility and Entitlement
— Eligibility Criteria. Paternity leave is available to male employees and partners who are legally recognized as the father or parent of a newborn child or an adopted child. Eligibility may be subject to the employee having completed a minimum period of continuous service with the Company, as defined by applicable law or Company guidelines. The Company ensures that all eligible employees are informed of their paternity leave rights and entitlements, and provides support in accessing paternity leave.
— Duration and Entitlement. Eligible employees may avail paternity leave for a defined number of working days as prescribed by Company policy or applicable labor laws. Paternity leave duration is typically shorter than maternity leave but provides adequate time for fathers and partners to support their families and bond with their children. The Company provides paternity leave entitlements that meet or exceed legal requirements and that support family needs.
— Usage Requirements. Paternity leave must generally be taken within a specified period following the birth or adoption of the child, typically within the first few months, and may be required to be taken in a continuous block unless otherwise approved. The Company recognizes that family circumstances vary and provides flexibility in paternity leave usage when possible, while ensuring that leave is taken in a manner that supports family needs and business requirements.
8.8.3 Application and Documentation
— Notification Requirements. Employees intending to avail paternity leave must notify their reporting manager and HR as early as reasonably possible, enabling the Company to plan for coverage and to provide appropriate support. Early notification is particularly important when the expected birth or adoption date is known, but the Company accommodates last-minute notifications when circumstances require it.
— Supporting Documentation. Supporting documentation, such as a birth certificate, hospital confirmation, or adoption papers, may be required to process the paternity leave request. Documentation helps verify eligibility and supports appropriate leave administration, and the Company requests only the minimum documentation necessary. The Company administers paternity leave with understanding and support, recognizing that this is an important and often busy time for families.
8.8.4 Payment and Benefits
— Payment During Leave. Paternity leave will be paid or unpaid based on Company policy and statutory requirements. The Company is committed to providing paid paternity leave whenever possible, recognizing that financial support during this time is important to families. Payment arrangements are communicated clearly to eligible employees and are processed through the regular payroll cycle.
— Benefit Continuation. During approved paid paternity leave, employment benefits will continue in accordance with Company policy and legal requirements. Benefit continuation ensures that employees and their families maintain coverage during paternity leave, and the Company is committed to ensuring seamless benefit administration. Employees are informed of benefit continuation arrangements and are supported in maintaining their benefits during leave.

8.9 Parental and Adoption Leave
8.9.1 Purpose and Philosophy
— Supporting All Forms of Parenthood. Parental and adoption leave is provided to employees who become parents through adoption, surrogacy, or other legally recognized arrangements, recognizing that all forms of parenthood are significant life events that require time for bonding, adjustment, and family integration. The Company recognizes that adoption and surrogacy are important ways that families are formed and is committed to providing leave that supports these families equally with biological families.
— Enabling Bonding and Adjustment. Parental and adoption leave enables employees to bond with and care for a newly placed child and to adjust to new family responsibilities, recognizing that the transition to parenthood through adoption or surrogacy can be both joyful and challenging. The Company provides leave that gives families adequate time to establish routines, to build relationships, and to navigate the early stages of family life.
8.9.2 Eligibility and Entitlement
— Eligibility Criteria. Parental and adoption leave is available to employees who have legally adopted a child or become parents through surrogacy or similar lawful arrangements, subject to eligibility criteria established by the Company and applicable laws. The Company ensures that all eligible employees are informed of their parental and adoption leave rights and provides support in accessing these benefits.
— Duration and Structure. Eligible employees are entitled to eight (8) weeks of paid parental or adoption leave, which must be taken within twelve (12) months of the child's birth, adoption, or placement. The leave is generally taken as a continuous period, with flexible or reduced-schedule arrangements considered on a case-by-case basis subject to business needs and approval. The Company provides flexibility in leave timing and structure when possible, recognizing that family circumstances vary.
8.9.3 Application Process
— Request Submission. Employees must submit a written request for parental or adoption leave to their reporting manager and HR, along with relevant supporting documentation such as adoption orders or legal placement papers. Advance notice should be provided wherever possible to enable planning and coverage, but the Company accommodates situations where advance notice is not possible.
— Documentation Requirements. Supporting documentation helps verify eligibility and supports appropriate leave administration. The Company requests only the minimum documentation necessary and administers parental and adoption leave with understanding and support, recognizing that this is an important time for families.
8.9.4 Integration with Other Leave
— Separate Entitlement. Parental and adoption leave is separate from annual leave and sick leave, providing dedicated time for family bonding and adjustment. The Company recognizes that parental and adoption leave serves a specific purpose and should not be deducted from other leave entitlements.
— Extension Options. Employees may be permitted to use accrued paid leave, such as annual leave, to extend time off beyond the approved parental leave period, subject to approval and legal requirements. The Company works with employees to find solutions that balance family needs with business requirements and provides flexibility when possible.

8.10 Bereavement Leave
8.10.1 Purpose and Philosophy
— Supporting Employees During Loss. Bereavement leave is provided to allow employees time away from work to grieve, attend funeral or memorial services, and manage personal and family affairs following the death of a loved one. The Company recognizes the emotional impact of loss and aims to administer this leave with compassion, sensitivity, and understanding. Bereavement leave recognizes that grief is a natural and important process that requires time and space, and that employees need support during difficult times.
— Compassionate Administration. The Company administers bereavement leave with compassion, recognizing that loss affects people differently and that employees need flexibility and support during bereavement. The Company does not require excessive documentation or justification for bereavement leave, and works with employees to provide the time and support they need during difficult periods.
8.10.2 Eligibility and Entitlement
— Immediate Family Members. Employees may avail paid bereavement leave in the event of the death of an immediate family member, including spouse, children, parents, siblings, and grandparents. The Company provides a standard bereavement leave entitlement for immediate family members, typically three to five working days, recognizing that immediate family loss requires adequate time for grieving and funeral arrangements.
— Extended Family and Significant Relationships. Limited paid leave may also be granted for extended family members or other significant personal relationships, subject to managerial approval and individual circumstances. The Company recognizes that loss affects people differently and that relationships vary in significance, and provides flexibility in bereavement leave administration. Extended family bereavement leave is evaluated on a case-by-case basis, with consideration given to the nature of the relationship and the employee's needs.
8.10.3 Usage and Timing
— Timing of Leave. Bereavement leave should generally be taken within a reasonable period following the death or funeral services, recognizing that employees need time immediately following loss and for funeral arrangements. The Company provides flexibility in bereavement leave timing, accommodating both immediate needs and longer-term grief support when necessary.
— Consecutive or Intermittent Usage. Leave may be taken consecutively or intermittently, depending on individual circumstances and operational requirements. The Company recognizes that grief is a process that may require time at different stages, and provides flexibility in bereavement leave usage when possible. Employees are encouraged to communicate their needs and to work with their managers to find arrangements that support their grieving process.
8.10.4 Documentation and Additional Support
— Minimal Documentation. While the Company seeks to minimize administrative burden during periods of loss, documentation such as an obituary, funeral notice, or death certificate may be requested in exceptional cases or where misuse is suspected. The Company administers bereavement leave with trust and compassion, requesting documentation only when necessary and with sensitivity to the employee's situation.
— Additional Time Off Options. If additional time off is required beyond the standard bereavement leave entitlement, employees may request annual leave, sick leave, or Leave Without Pay. Managers are encouraged to provide flexibility where reasonable, recognizing that grief is a process that may require additional time and support. The Company works with employees to find solutions that support their needs during difficult times.

8.11 Leave Without Pay
8.11.1 Purpose and Circumstances
— When Leave Without Pay Is Appropriate. Leave Without Pay (LWOP) is granted when an employee requires time away from work and has exhausted all applicable paid leave balances, or when the reason for absence does not qualify under paid leave categories. LWOP provides flexibility for extended absences, personal sabbaticals, educational pursuits, or other circumstances that require time away from work but do not qualify for paid leave. The Company recognizes that employees may have legitimate needs for extended time away from work and provides LWOP as a mechanism for accommodating these needs while maintaining the employment relationship.
— Maintaining Employment Relationship. LWOP maintains the employment relationship during extended absences, enabling employees to return to work after the leave period. The Company recognizes that maintaining employment relationships during extended absences can benefit both employees and the organization, and provides LWOP as a mechanism for supporting employees while preserving organizational investment in talent.
8.11.2 Eligibility and Approval
— Eligibility Requirements. Employees must generally have completed their probationary period to be considered for Leave Without Pay, unless otherwise required by law or exceptional circumstances. The Company evaluates LWOP requests based on individual circumstances, business needs, and the reasonableness of the request. LWOP is not automatically granted and requires approval based on business considerations and employee circumstances.
— Approval Process. All Leave Without Pay requests must be submitted in writing and approved in advance by the reporting manager and HR. Approval is based on the nature of the request, length of service, performance history, duration of leave, and business requirements. Extended LWOP requests may require additional approvals from senior management, and the Company evaluates each request on its merits, considering both employee needs and business requirements.
8.11.3 Conditions During Leave
— Salary and Accrual. During Leave Without Pay, employees will not receive salary and may not accrue benefits such as annual leave or sick leave. The Company recognizes that LWOP creates financial impact for employees and works with employees to understand the implications of LWOP before approval. Employees are informed of the financial and benefit implications of LWOP and are expected to plan accordingly.
— Benefit Continuation. Certain benefits, such as health insurance, may be continued subject to employee contribution and applicable laws. Benefit continuation during LWOP enables employees to maintain coverage during extended absences, and the Company works with employees to arrange benefit continuation when possible. Employees are informed of benefit continuation options and costs, and are responsible for making arrangements for benefit continuation during LWOP.
8.11.4 Return to Work
— Return Obligations. Employees are required to return to work on the agreed date, and failure to return or communicate extensions may be treated as job abandonment or voluntary resignation. The Company expects employees to honor their commitments regarding LWOP duration and return dates, and works with employees to plan for return to work. Employees who need to extend LWOP must request extensions in advance and receive approval before the original return date.
— Reintegration Support. The Company provides reintegration support for employees returning from extended LWOP, including orientation to changes that occurred during leave, reintroduction to team and projects, and flexible arrangements that support the transition back to work. The Company recognizes that returning from extended leave can be challenging and provides support to ensure successful reintegration.

8.12 Compensatory Off and Overtime Compensation
8.12.1 Purpose and Philosophy
— Recognizing Additional Work. Compensatory Off (Comp Off) is provided to employees who are required to work on scheduled holidays, weekly rest days, or approved non-working days due to business requirements. Comp Off recognizes that employees who work additional hours or on non-working days deserve time off in compensation, and provides a mechanism for balancing work demands with employee rest and recovery needs. The Company is committed to ensuring that employees who work additional hours receive appropriate compensation, whether through Comp Off or overtime pay.
— Balancing Work Demands and Employee Needs. Comp Off administration requires balancing business needs for additional work with employee needs for rest and time off. The Company is committed to minimizing the need for work on non-working days, to providing Comp Off promptly when additional work is required, and to ensuring that Comp Off is used to support employee well-being and work-life balance.
8.12.2 Eligibility and Approval
— Prior Approval Requirement. Compensatory Off is granted only when prior written approval has been obtained from management for work on non-working days. Unauthorized work on non-working days does not automatically qualify for compensatory leave, and employees are expected to obtain approval before working on non-working days. The Company recognizes that emergency situations may arise, but expects employees to obtain approval as soon as possible and to document additional work appropriately.
— Approval Process. Comp Off requests are evaluated based on business justification, the nature of the work performed, and operational requirements. The Company ensures that Comp Off is granted fairly and consistently, and that employees who work additional hours receive appropriate compensation. Managers are responsible for approving Comp Off requests and for ensuring that Comp Off is granted when additional work is required.
8.12.3 Usage and Expiration
— Usage Requirements. Compensatory Off must be availed within a specified period from the date it is earned, typically within three to six months, and is subject to managerial approval based on operational needs. The Company encourages employees to use Comp Off promptly to support work-life balance and to prevent accumulation that could create operational challenges. Comp Off usage is subject to the same approval processes as other leave types, and employees are expected to plan Comp Off usage in coordination with their managers.
— Expiration Policy. Unused compensatory off may lapse if not utilized within the prescribed timeframe, encouraging employees to use Comp Off during the specified period. The Company provides regular reminders about Comp Off balances and expiration dates to help employees plan usage effectively. Expiration policies are designed to encourage timely usage while recognizing that business needs may sometimes delay Comp Off usage.

8.13 Statutory and Special Leave
8.13.1 Compliance with Legal Requirements
— Full Legal Compliance. The Company complies fully with all applicable statutory and special leave requirements as mandated by local, state, and national labor laws. This includes, but is not limited to, public holidays, jury duty, military service, family and medical leave, voting leave, and other legally protected absences. The Company recognizes that legal compliance is not optional and ensures that all statutory leave requirements are met or exceeded.
— Legal Provisions Prevail. Statutory leave will be administered strictly in accordance with legal requirements, and where such leave provides greater benefits than Company policy, the statutory provisions shall prevail. The Company ensures that employees receive the maximum benefits available under law and that Company policy supplements rather than limits legal entitlements. Employees are informed of their statutory leave rights and are supported in accessing these benefits.
8.13.2 Public Holidays
— Recognition of Public Holidays. The Company recognizes all public holidays as designated by applicable law and provides paid time off for public holidays in accordance with legal requirements and Company policy. Public holidays are communicated to employees in advance, and the Company ensures that employees are aware of public holiday schedules and any special arrangements that may apply.
— Work on Public Holidays. When business requirements necessitate work on public holidays, employees are compensated through Comp Off, overtime pay, or other mechanisms as required by law and Company policy. The Company minimizes the need for work on public holidays and ensures that employees who work on public holidays receive appropriate compensation and time off.
8.13.3 Jury Duty and Legal Obligations
— Supporting Civic Duty. The Company provides leave for jury duty and other legal obligations, recognizing that employees have civic responsibilities and that supporting these obligations is important to the functioning of the justice system. Jury duty leave is provided with pay where required by law, and the Company ensures that employees are not penalized for fulfilling their civic obligations.
— Documentation and Coordination. Employees are expected to provide documentation of jury duty requirements and to coordinate with their managers to minimize disruption to work. The Company works with employees to accommodate jury duty obligations while maintaining operational effectiveness, and provides support throughout the process.
8.13.4 Military Service Leave
— Supporting Military Obligations. The Company provides leave for military service obligations, including active duty, reserve duty, and training requirements, in accordance with applicable law. Military service leave recognizes that employees may have military obligations and that supporting these obligations is important to national defense and employee well-being. The Company complies with all applicable laws regarding military service leave and ensures that employees are not penalized for fulfilling their military obligations.
— Job Protection and Reinstatement. Employees on military service leave are protected under applicable law, and the Company ensures that military service does not negatively affect employment status, benefits, or career progression. The Company provides support for employees returning from military service and ensures that reintegration is smooth and supportive.

8.14 Leave Abuse and Disciplinary Action
8.14.1 Definition of Leave Abuse
— What Constitutes Leave Abuse. Abuse of leave includes, but is not limited to, frequent unscheduled absences without legitimate reason, repeated patterned leave that suggests misuse, falsification of leave records or documentation, misuse of sick leave for non-medical purposes, failure to follow leave procedures, or any other conduct that violates leave policy or misrepresents the need for leave. Leave abuse undermines the trust that is essential to leave administration and creates unfair burden on colleagues and the organization.
— Patterns and Red Flags. The Company monitors leave usage patterns to identify potential abuse, including excessive Monday or Friday absences, patterns around holidays or weekends, frequent last-minute absences, or other patterns that suggest misuse. While patterns alone do not constitute proof of abuse, they may trigger investigation and require explanation. The Company investigates potential abuse fairly and thoroughly, providing employees with opportunities to explain patterns and circumstances.
8.14.2 Investigation and Disciplinary Process
— Fair Investigation. When leave abuse is suspected, the Company conducts a fair and thorough investigation, gathering information, reviewing leave records, and providing the employee with opportunities to explain circumstances. Investigations are conducted by HR in conjunction with the employee's manager, and employees are informed of concerns and given opportunities to respond. The Company does not assume abuse without investigation and provides employees with fair process throughout.
— Disciplinary Consequences. Abuse of leave may result in corrective or disciplinary action in accordance with the Company's disciplinary policy, up to and including termination of employment. The severity of disciplinary action depends on the nature and extent of the abuse, the employee's history, and the impact on the organization. The Company is committed to addressing leave abuse fairly but firmly, recognizing that abuse undermines trust and creates unfair burden.
8.14.3 Prevention and Education
— Clear Communication. The Company provides clear communication about leave policies, procedures, and expectations, ensuring that employees understand their rights and responsibilities. Clear communication helps prevent unintentional violations and ensures that employees know how to use leave appropriately. The Company provides regular reminders about leave policies and is available to answer questions and provide guidance.
— Training and Support. The Company provides training and support to help employees understand leave policies and to use leave appropriately. Training covers leave types, procedures, documentation requirements, and expectations, and is provided during onboarding and through regular communications. The Company is committed to helping employees use leave appropriately and to preventing abuse through education and support.

8.15 Leave Administration Systems and Technology
8.15.1 Leave Management Systems
— Automated Leave Administration. The Company maintains automated leave management systems that enable employees to request leave, track balances, and receive approvals efficiently. Leave management systems are integrated with the HRMS, provide real-time balance information, and streamline the leave administration process. The Company invests in technology that makes leave administration easy, accessible, and transparent for employees and managers.
— System Accessibility and Usability. Leave management systems are accessible from any device with internet connectivity, enabling employees to request leave and check balances from anywhere. Systems are designed to be user-friendly and intuitive, requiring minimal training and support. The Company provides system training during onboarding and is available to assist employees with system usage when needed.
8.15.2 Reporting and Analytics
— Leave Usage Reporting. The Company maintains reporting and analytics capabilities that enable tracking of leave usage patterns, identification of trends, and analysis of leave administration effectiveness. Reporting helps identify areas for improvement, supports decision-making about leave policies, and enables the Company to understand how leave is being used across the organization. Reports are used to inform policy development and to ensure that leave administration is effective and fair.
— Data-Driven Decision Making. Leave analytics enable data-driven decision making about leave policies, procedures, and administration. The Company uses analytics to understand leave usage patterns, to identify potential issues, and to make informed decisions about leave program improvements. Analytics help ensure that leave policies are effective, fair, and aligned with employee needs and business requirements.

8.16 Policy Compliance, Governance, and Review
8.16.1 Responsibility for Policy Compliance
— Every Employee's Personal Responsibility. Compliance with this Leave Policy is the personal responsibility of every employee. Employees are responsible for understanding their leave entitlements, for using leave appropriately, for following leave procedures, for providing accurate information in leave requests, and for respecting business needs when planning leave. Compliance is not merely about following procedures — it is about using leave responsibly, communicating effectively, and balancing personal needs with organizational requirements.
— Manager Responsibility for Fair Administration. Managers are responsible for administering leave fairly and consistently, for approving leave requests when possible, for planning for coverage during employee absences, and for supporting employees' work-life balance. Managers who fail to administer leave fairly, who create barriers to leave usage, or who do not support employee work-life balance are not fulfilling their responsibilities and may be subject to performance management themselves.
— HR Responsibility for Policy Administration. HR is responsible for administering this policy, for maintaining accurate leave records, for ensuring compliance with legal requirements, for providing guidance and support to employees and managers, and for monitoring leave administration for fairness and consistency. HR is also responsible for ensuring that leave systems function effectively, that policies are communicated clearly, and that leave administration supports both employee needs and business requirements.
8.16.2 Employee Acknowledgment
— Formal Acknowledgment Is Expected. While formal acknowledgment of this policy may not be required in the same way as compliance policies, employees are expected to be familiar with this policy, to understand their leave entitlements, and to use leave appropriately. The Company communicates this policy clearly during onboarding and through regular communications, and employees are expected to take responsibility for understanding and utilizing their leave entitlements.
8.16.3 Annual Policy Review
— Regular Review to Maintain Relevance and Currency. This Leave Policy will be formally reviewed on an annual basis by HR, or earlier if required by a material change in applicable employment law, business needs, or workforce demographics. The review process includes assessment of policy effectiveness, evaluation of leave usage patterns, consideration of feedback from employees and managers, identification of emerging needs, and updates to ensure that the policy remains relevant, effective, and compliant with legal requirements. All updates will be approved by the Chief Human Resources Officer, communicated to all employees through the HRMS and official Company communication channels, and integrated into the leave administration framework. The Company's commitment to maintaining a current, comprehensive, and genuinely effective Leave Policy is both a legal obligation and a fundamental expression of its commitment to employee well-being, work-life balance, and sustainable employment relationships.

8.20 Emergency Leave and Unforeseen Circumstances
8.20.1 Definition and Recognition
— What Constitutes Emergency Leave. Emergency leave is leave taken for unforeseen circumstances that require immediate attention and cannot be planned in advance. Emergency situations include medical emergencies, family emergencies, natural disasters, accidents, and other urgent situations that require immediate employee attention. The Company recognizes that emergencies are unpredictable and administers emergency leave with compassion and understanding.
— Immediate Approval and Support. Emergency leave requests are processed immediately, with minimal documentation requirements and maximum flexibility. The Company recognizes that employees facing emergencies need immediate support, not bureaucratic processes, and ensures that emergency leave is approved promptly and without unnecessary delay. Managers and HR are expected to respond to emergency leave requests immediately and to provide support throughout the emergency situation.
8.20.2 Documentation and Follow-Up
— Minimal Documentation Requirements. Emergency leave requires minimal documentation, recognizing that employees facing emergencies should not be burdened with administrative requirements. The Company may request basic information about the nature of the emergency and expected duration, but does not require extensive documentation for emergency leave. The Company trusts employees to use emergency leave appropriately and requests documentation only when necessary.
— Post-Emergency Follow-Up. After emergency leave, the Company may follow up with employees to ensure that they are okay, to provide support, and to understand if additional leave or accommodations are needed. Post-emergency follow-up is supportive, not investigative, and is designed to help employees recover and return to work successfully. The Company provides employee assistance resources and support for employees dealing with emergencies.
