Onboarding Assistant Policy

1. Purpose
This policy exists to provide every new employee with a structured, consistent, and welcoming onboarding experience that sets the foundation for long-term success within the organization. Joining a new workplace can be both exciting and overwhelming, and it is the Company's responsibility to ensure that this transition is as smooth, informative, and supportive as possible. The onboarding process is designed to help new employees understand their roles and responsibilities clearly, familiarize themselves with company culture, values, and ways of working, and build the confidence and connections they need to contribute meaningfully from the very beginning. A well-executed onboarding experience not only accelerates individual productivity but also strengthens employee engagement, reduces early attrition, and reflects the Company's genuine commitment to its people.

2. Scope
This policy applies to all newly hired employees joining the organization at any level, grade, or function. It also applies to existing employees who are transitioning into new roles, departments, or locations, as they too require structured support to navigate their new responsibilities effectively. The onboarding framework outlined in this policy is applicable across all business units, offices, and work arrangements including on-site, remote, and hybrid employees. HR Business Partners are responsible for overseeing the implementation of this policy within their respective functions and ensuring a consistent experience across the organization.

3. Onboarding Stages
The onboarding journey is structured across four key stages, each designed to build progressively on the last and ensure that the employee is fully equipped, informed, and integrated by the end of the probation period.

a. Stage 1 — Pre-Joining
The pre-joining stage begins from the moment an offer is accepted and is critical in making the employee feel welcomed and prepared before their first day. A positive pre-joining experience significantly reduces first-day anxiety and sets a professional and caring tone from the outset.
-  The formal offer letter is issued promptly upon acceptance, clearly outlining the compensation structure, designation, reporting relationship, joining date, and all applicable terms and conditions of employment.
- Background verification is initiated in accordance with the Company's BGV policy, covering employment history, educational qualifications, identity, and any other checks relevant to the role.
- The new employee is provided with a clear document submission checklist and guided through the process of submitting all required documents such as identity proof, address proof, educational certificates, previous employment records, and bank account details within the stipulated timeline.
- IT asset allocation is coordinated in advance so that the employee's laptop, system access, email ID, and required software are fully configured and ready on Day 1, avoiding unnecessary delays to productivity.
- A personalized welcome communication is sent to the new joiner prior to their start date, including details of their first-day schedule, reporting location or virtual joining instructions, key contacts, dress code, and any other relevant information to help them feel prepared and expected.
- The reporting manager is notified and briefed in advance to ensure they are available and prepared to receive the new employee on Day 1.


Stage 2 — Day 1 Orientation
The first day is one of the most significant days in an employee's journey with the Company. It creates a lasting first impression and must be managed with care, warmth, and structure. The Day 1 orientation is designed to make the new employee feel genuinely welcomed while also ensuring they receive the foundational information they need to begin their role with confidence.
- New employees are introduced to the Company's vision, mission, and core values, helping them understand the broader purpose behind their work and how their role contributes to organizational goals.
- A comprehensive policy overview is provided, covering key HR policies including leave, attendance, code of conduct, payroll, and information security, with employees encouraged to ask questions and seek clarification.
- A dedicated Code of Conduct briefing is conducted to ensure the employee understands the ethical standards, professional expectations, and behavioral norms that govern life at the Company.
- A role clarity session is held with the reporting manager to discuss the employee's specific responsibilities, immediate priorities, key stakeholders, and performance expectations from the outset.
- IT systems setup is completed on Day 1, including HRMS access, email configuration, collaboration tools, and any role-specific software or platforms the employee will require to carry out their work.
- New employees are introduced to their immediate team, buddy or mentor (if assigned), and key cross-functional contacts to begin building their internal network from the very first day.


Stage 3 — First 30 Days
The first 30 days are focused on helping the new employee settle into their role, begin contributing meaningfully, and build strong working relationships within the team and across the organization.
- Goal setting sessions are conducted between the employee and their reporting manager to define clear, measurable objectives for the probation period, aligned with team and business priorities.
- A structured training and process familiarization plan is put in place, covering all relevant tools, systems, workflows, and processes the employee needs to understand to perform their role effectively.
- Team integration activities are organized to help the new employee build rapport with colleagues, understand team dynamics, and feel genuinely welcomed as part of the group.
- A structured feedback session is conducted at the end of the first 30 days, where the reporting manager shares initial observations and the employee has the opportunity to raise any concerns, gaps, or support needs they may have encountered during their first month.
- HR conducts a separate check-in with the new employee during this period to assess their overall onboarding experience and address any concerns that may not have been raised with the manager directly.


Stage 4 — 60 to 90 Day Review
The 60 to 90 day review marks the formal mid-to-end point of the probation period and is a critical stage for both the employee and the organization. It is an opportunity to assess performance, provide meaningful feedback, and make informed decisions about confirmation of employment.
- A structured probation performance evaluation is conducted by the reporting manager, assessing the employee's performance against the goals set during the first 30 days, their attitude, adaptability, collaboration, and overall contribution to the team.
- A formal confirmation discussion is held between HR, the reporting manager, and the employee to communicate the outcome of the evaluation transparently and respectfully.
- Where performance has been satisfactory, the confirmation process is initiated without delay, providing the employee with the recognition and security they deserve.
- Where concerns have been identified, HR and the manager will collaboratively develop a structured support plan with clear improvement targets and a defined review timeline before a final decision is made.
- Employee feedback on the onboarding experience is formally collected at this stage to support continuous improvement of the process for future joiners.


4. Confirmation & Outcomes
Successful completion of the probation period and a satisfactory performance evaluation leads to formal confirmation of employment. This is a significant milestone for both the employee and the organization and is handled with appropriate formality and recognition.
- A formal confirmation letter is issued to the employee, acknowledging the successful completion of their probation period and confirming their permanent employment status with the Company.
- Full employee benefits are activated upon confirmation, including eligibility for benefits that may have been deferred during the probation period such as enhanced leave entitlements, insurance upgrades, or additional allowances as applicable.
- The employee is formally granted permanent employment status, with all associated rights, protections, and entitlements as defined under the Company's HR policies and applicable labor legislation.
- The confirmation is recorded in the HRMS and the employee's personal file, and relevant departments including Finance and IT are notified to update records and activate any additional entitlements accordingly.
- HR schedules a brief post-confirmation discussion to acknowledge the employee's achievement, share any relevant next steps, and reinforce the Company's commitment to their continued growth and development.


5. Roles & Responsibilities
The success of the onboarding process depends on the active participation and accountability of multiple stakeholders across the organization.
- HR is responsible for designing, coordinating, and continuously improving the onboarding framework, ensuring timely communication at every stage, and serving as a consistent point of support for new employees throughout the process.
- Reporting Managers are responsible for actively participating in the onboarding of their new team members, conducting role clarity and goal setting sessions, providing regular feedback, and completing performance evaluations on time.
- IT & Admin are responsible for ensuring that all assets, systems, and access requirements are ready before the employee's first day.
- New Employees are expected to engage actively with the onboarding process, complete all required documentation and training on time, ask questions, and raise concerns early rather than allowing them to go unaddressed.

6. Policy Review
This policy will be reviewed annually by the HR department, or earlier in response to significant organizational changes, feedback from new joiners, or updates to applicable employment legislation. All revisions will be communicated to relevant stakeholders and updated on the HRMS portal.
