11. PAYROLL AND COMPENSATION POLICY

11.1 Purpose and Organizational Philosophy
11.1.1 Why This Policy Exists
— The Foundation of Fair and Accurate Compensation. The Payroll and Compensation Policy is one of the most operationally critical documents in the Company's entire policy framework. It establishes the principles, standards, and comprehensive frameworks through which employees are compensated for their work — ensuring that compensation is accurate, timely, fair, and compliant with all applicable laws and regulations. Where the Employment Policy referenced as HR-EP-001 governs the terms and conditions of employment, and the Performance Management Policy referenced as HR-PMP-001 governs how performance is evaluated, this policy governs how compensation is calculated, administered, and delivered. Payroll and compensation administration is not merely an administrative function. It is a fundamental responsibility that affects every employee's financial security, trust in the organization, and sense of being valued, and this policy is the framework through which that responsibility is fulfilled.
— The Organizational Imperative. The Company operates in a business environment where accurate, timely, and fair compensation is essential to employee trust, engagement, and retention. Research consistently demonstrates that compensation accuracy, transparency, and fairness are among the most important factors in employee satisfaction and organizational trust. A single payroll error, delay, or perceived unfairness can significantly damage employee trust and engagement. This policy exists to ensure that the Company maintains the highest standards of payroll integrity, compensation fairness, and administrative excellence — standards that protect both employees and the organization and that demonstrate the Company's commitment to treating employees with respect and fairness.
— The Human Imperative. This policy has been written with a genuine recognition that compensation is not merely a financial transaction — it is a fundamental expression of the value the Company places on employee contributions and a critical factor in employees' financial security and well-being. Employees depend on accurate and timely compensation to meet their financial obligations, support their families, and plan for their futures. Payroll errors, delays, or lack of transparency can cause significant stress, financial hardship, and loss of trust. This policy is not a declaration of the Company's administrative capability. It is a recognition of the critical importance of compensation accuracy and a commitment to ensuring that every employee receives what they have earned, when they have earned it, and in a manner that is transparent and fair.
— The Philosophy of Transparency and Fairness. Effective payroll and compensation administration requires transparency, fairness, and clear communication. Employees have the right to understand how their compensation is calculated, what deductions are made and why, and how their compensation may change over time. This policy is built on the conviction that transparency builds trust, that fairness ensures equity, and that clear communication prevents misunderstandings and disputes. Payroll and compensation administration is most effective when it is transparent, fair, and well-communicated, and this policy ensures that approach is applied consistently to every employee.
— A Living and Evolving Document. Like all enterprise policies, the Payroll and Compensation Policy is a living document that will be reviewed, updated, and strengthened as applicable laws and regulations evolve, as the Company's compensation philosophy and practices develop, as payroll systems and technologies advance, and as practical experience provides insights into how the policy can be made more effective. Every employee who receives compensation, every manager who makes compensation decisions, and every HR and Finance professional who administers payroll is expected to contribute to the continuous improvement of payroll and compensation administration, ensuring that it remains accurate, fair, and supportive of employee well-being.

11.2 Scope and Applicability
11.2.1 Who This Policy Applies To
— Universal Application to All Employees. This policy applies universally and without exception to every individual engaged by the Company in any employment capacity — regardless of seniority, role, department, business unit, geographic location, or employment classification. The most senior executive in the organization is subject to the same payroll and compensation administration standards as the most junior team member, recognizing that accurate and fair compensation is essential for all employees. The specific application of certain policy provisions may vary based on employment classification, role, or applicable law, but the fundamental framework of payroll and compensation administration applies to all employees.
— All Categories of Employment. This policy covers permanent full-time employees, part-time employees, employees on fixed-term contracts, and any other individual engaged in an employment relationship with the Company. Each of these individuals, regardless of the nature or duration of their employment, is entitled to accurate, timely, and fair compensation administration appropriate to their employment classification and circumstances. The Company recognizes that all employees deserve accurate and fair compensation administration regardless of their employment category.
11.2.2 Where This Policy Applies
— All Work Locations and Arrangements. This policy applies in every location and arrangement in which Company work is performed — whether on Company premises, at client sites, in remote or hybrid arrangements, or in any other work location. Remote and hybrid employees are subject to the same payroll and compensation administration standards as on-site employees, with accommodations for remote delivery of payroll information and support. The Company ensures that payroll and compensation administration is effective regardless of work location.
— All Phases of the Employment Relationship. This policy applies throughout the entire employment relationship, from the first day of employment through active employment and through the conclusion of the employment relationship. Payroll and compensation administration begins with the first payroll cycle and continues throughout employment, including final settlement upon termination. The Company ensures that payroll and compensation administration is comprehensive and continuous throughout the employment relationship.
11.2.3 Relationship With Other Policies
— An Integrated Compensation Framework. This Payroll and Compensation Policy operates as one component of the Company's broader human resources and employment framework. It must be read and applied in conjunction with the Employment Policy referenced as HR-EP-001, the Performance Management Policy referenced as HR-PMP-001, the Leave Policy referenced as HR-LP-001, and all other applicable Company policies. Where a specific policy provides more detailed guidance on a particular aspect of compensation — such as the Performance Management Policy's treatment of performance-based compensation — that specific policy governs. Where a payroll or compensation concern arises that falls outside the scope of any specific policy, this Payroll and Compensation Policy provides the governing framework.
— Conflict Resolution. In the event of any apparent conflict between the standards established in this policy and those established in any other Company policy, or between this policy and applicable employment law, employees must seek guidance from HR or Finance before taking action. Employees must not act unilaterally on their own interpretation of any apparent conflict between policies and must not use an apparent conflict as justification for departing from payroll and compensation standards that would otherwise apply. Where applicable employment law provides more generous compensation or payroll protections than this policy, the legal provisions shall prevail.

11.3 Payroll Administration and Processing
11.3.1 Payroll Cycle and Schedule
— Monthly Payroll Processing. Payroll is processed on a monthly cycle, with salaries credited directly to employees' registered bank accounts in accordance with the official payroll schedule published at the start of each financial year. The payroll schedule is communicated to all employees in advance and is maintained consistently throughout the year. The Company ensures that payroll is processed on time and that employees receive their compensation as scheduled. Any changes to the payroll schedule are communicated well in advance to enable employees to plan accordingly.
— Payroll Schedule Publication. The official payroll schedule is published at the start of each financial year and is available through the HRMS portal and other communication channels. The schedule specifies payroll processing dates, salary credit dates, and any special considerations or exceptions. The Company ensures that the payroll schedule is clear, accessible, and communicated effectively to all employees.
11.3.2 Payroll Processing Procedures
— End-to-End Payroll Management. The HR and Finance departments work in close coordination to ensure that payroll is processed without error, delay, or omission. A dedicated payroll team oversees the end-to-end process, from data collation and verification to final disbursement and reconciliation. The Company maintains rigorous payroll processing procedures that ensure accuracy, timeliness, and compliance. Payroll processing includes data collection, verification, calculation, approval, disbursement, and reconciliation.
— Data Collection and Verification. Payroll data is collected from multiple sources, including attendance records, leave records, time sheets, reimbursement claims, and other relevant sources. Data is verified for accuracy and completeness before processing. The Company ensures that payroll data is accurate and that verification processes are thorough. Data verification includes checking for completeness, accuracy, and compliance with policies and procedures.
— Calculation and Processing. Payroll calculations are performed using approved formulas and systems, ensuring accuracy and consistency. Calculations include gross salary, deductions, net salary, and all applicable adjustments. The Company ensures that calculations are accurate and that they comply with all applicable laws and policies. Payroll systems are maintained and updated to ensure accuracy and compliance.
— Approval and Authorization. Payroll requires appropriate approval and authorization before disbursement, ensuring that all payments are authorized and that controls are maintained. Approval processes ensure that payroll is reviewed and authorized by appropriate personnel. The Company maintains approval processes that ensure control and accountability.
— Disbursement and Reconciliation. Payroll is disbursed through direct deposit to employees' registered bank accounts, and disbursements are reconciled to ensure accuracy. The Company ensures that disbursements are accurate and that reconciliation processes identify and resolve any discrepancies. Reconciliation includes comparing disbursements to payroll records and identifying and resolving any differences.
11.3.3 Payslip Issuance and Access
— Electronic Payslip Delivery. Payslips are issued electronically through the HRMS portal on or before the last working day of each month, ensuring that employees have timely access to their payroll information. Electronic delivery provides secure, accessible, and environmentally friendly payslip distribution. The Company ensures that payslips are delivered on time and that employees have easy access to their payroll information.
— Payslip Review and Verification. Employees are strongly encouraged to review their payslips carefully upon receipt to identify any discrepancies at the earliest possible stage. Payslip review helps identify errors quickly and enables prompt resolution. The Company provides guidance on payslip review and encourages employees to verify their payslips regularly. Employees are expected to review their payslips and to report any discrepancies promptly.
11.3.4 Bank Account Management
— Bank Account Registration. Employees must register their bank account details with HR for payroll processing, and any changes to registered bank account details must be submitted to HR in writing at least 15 working days before the next payroll cycle to ensure the update is captured in time. Bank account registration ensures accurate and timely salary credit. The Company ensures that bank account registration is secure and that changes are processed promptly. Employees are responsible for maintaining accurate bank account information and for notifying HR of any changes.
— Bank Account Verification. Bank account details are verified before payroll processing to ensure accuracy and to prevent errors. Verification includes checking account numbers, bank names, and other relevant details. The Company ensures that bank account verification is thorough and that errors are prevented. Employees are expected to provide accurate bank account information and to verify that information is correct.
11.3.5 Payroll Queries and Discrepancies
— Query Submission Process. Payroll queries or discrepancies must be raised formally with HR within 7 working days of payslip issuance. Queries raised beyond this window may not be addressed retroactively for the same month, recognizing that payroll processing timelines require prompt query resolution. The Company ensures that query processes are clear and that employees understand how to raise queries. Query submission should include employee ID, pay period, description of the issue, and any relevant documentation.
— Query Resolution. Payroll queries are investigated promptly and resolved fairly, with clear communication to employees about the investigation process and resolution. The Company ensures that queries are resolved accurately and that employees are informed of resolutions. Query resolution includes investigation, correction if necessary, and communication to the employee. Employees are expected to cooperate with query investigations and to provide any requested information.
11.3.6 Payroll Delays and Disruptions
— Proactive Communication. In the event of any delays or system-related disruptions, employees will be proactively notified by HR with revised credit timelines and an explanation of the cause. Proactive communication helps manage expectations and reduces anxiety. The Company ensures that delays are communicated promptly and that employees are informed of revised timelines. Communication includes the reason for the delay, expected resolution timeline, and any actions employees need to take.
— Emergency Payroll Support. Employees who have not received their salary within two working days of the scheduled credit date should contact HR or Finance immediately with their employee ID and bank account details for prompt investigation. Emergency support ensures that payroll issues are addressed quickly and that employees receive their compensation as soon as possible. The Company maintains emergency payroll support processes that enable rapid investigation and resolution.

11.4 Compensation Structure and Components
11.4.1 Total Compensation Philosophy
— Comprehensive Compensation Approach. An employee's total compensation package is structured based on their role, grade, and employment terms, and includes multiple components designed to provide competitive, fair, and comprehensive compensation. Total compensation includes base salary, allowances, benefits, incentives, and other components that together provide a competitive compensation package. The Company ensures that total compensation is competitive, fair, and aligned with market standards and organizational objectives.
— Market Competitiveness. Compensation is designed to be competitive with market standards for similar roles, ensuring that the Company can attract and retain talented employees. Market competitiveness is maintained through regular market analysis and compensation reviews. The Company ensures that compensation remains competitive and that employees are compensated fairly relative to market standards.
11.4.2 Base Salary Components
— Basic Salary. Basic salary is the core component of compensation and serves as the basis for statutory calculations, including provident fund, gratuity, and other statutory benefits. Basic salary is established based on role, experience, performance, and market standards. The Company ensures that basic salary is fair and that it serves as an appropriate foundation for total compensation.
— House Rent Allowance (HRA). House Rent Allowance is calculated as a percentage of Basic Salary and is provided to support employees' housing costs. HRA may be tax-exempt under applicable tax laws when certain conditions are met. The Company ensures that HRA is calculated fairly and that employees understand HRA provisions and tax implications.
— Special Allowance. Special Allowance is a flexible component that may be provided based on role, grade, location, or other factors. Special Allowance provides flexibility in compensation structure and enables the Company to tailor compensation to individual circumstances. The Company ensures that Special Allowance is used appropriately and that it supports fair and competitive compensation.
11.4.3 Variable Compensation
— Performance Bonus. Performance Bonus is linked to appraisal outcomes and business performance, providing variable compensation that rewards performance excellence and business success. Performance bonuses are calculated based on individual performance, team performance, and organizational performance. The Company ensures that performance bonuses are fair, transparent, and aligned with performance objectives.
— Incentive Programs. The Company may provide various incentive programs that reward achievement of specific goals, milestones, or outcomes. Incentive programs motivate performance and recognize achievement. The Company ensures that incentive programs are fair, transparent, and aligned with business objectives.
11.4.4 Statutory Components and Deductions
— Provident Fund (PF). Provident Fund is a statutory contribution made by both employee and employer, providing retirement savings and social security. PF contributions are calculated based on basic salary and are made in accordance with applicable laws. The Company ensures that PF contributions are made accurately and on time, and that employees understand PF provisions and benefits.
— Professional Tax. Professional Tax is deducted as per applicable state regulations, and the amount varies by state and salary level. Professional Tax is a statutory requirement and is deducted in accordance with applicable laws. The Company ensures that Professional Tax is deducted accurately and that employees understand Professional Tax provisions.
— Income Tax (TDS). Income Tax is deducted monthly based on tax slab and declared investments, ensuring compliance with tax obligations and minimizing year-end tax liability. TDS is calculated based on annual salary projections and declared investments. The Company ensures that TDS is calculated accurately and that employees understand TDS provisions and their tax obligations.
— Other Statutory Contributions. Other statutory contributions may apply based on applicable laws, location, or other factors. The Company ensures that all applicable statutory contributions are made accurately and in compliance with legal requirements. Employees are informed of all statutory contributions and their purposes.
11.4.5 Investment Declarations and Tax Planning
— Investment Declaration Requirements. Employees are required to submit their investment declarations and supporting documents within the timelines prescribed by Finance to avoid excess or shortfall in TDS deductions. Investment declarations enable accurate TDS calculation and tax planning. The Company ensures that investment declaration processes are clear and that employees understand their obligations. Failure to submit investment declarations on time may result in higher tax deductions in the final months of the financial year.
— Tax Planning Support. The Company provides tax planning support and guidance to help employees understand their tax obligations and optimize their tax planning. Tax planning support helps employees make informed decisions about investments and tax-saving opportunities. The Company ensures that tax planning support is available and that employees understand their options.

11.5 Salary Adjustments and Variations
11.5.1 Understanding Salary Variations
— Normal Salary Variations. Net salary credited each month may vary from the previous month due to a number of documented and authorized factors. Salary variations are normal and may result from various factors including leave usage, tax adjustments, benefit deductions, and other authorized adjustments. The Company ensures that salary variations are clearly explained and that employees understand why their salary may vary. All adjustments are clearly reflected and itemized in the employee's payslip.
— Transparent Adjustment Communication. All salary adjustments are clearly communicated and itemized in payslips, enabling employees to understand exactly what adjustments were made and why. Transparent communication builds trust and prevents misunderstandings. The Company ensures that adjustment communication is clear and that employees can easily understand salary variations.
11.5.2 Leave Without Pay (LWP) Adjustments
— LWP Application. Leave Without Pay (LWP) is applied when an employee is absent beyond their available leave balance or is absent without prior approval. LWP ensures that compensation is adjusted appropriately for unpaid absences. The Company ensures that LWP is applied fairly and that employees understand LWP provisions and calculations.
— LWP Calculation Formula. The formula used to calculate LWP deductions is: (Monthly Gross Salary ÷ Total Working Days in the Month) × Number of LWP Days. This formula ensures that LWP deductions are proportional to the unpaid absence. The Company ensures that LWP calculations are accurate and that employees understand how LWP is calculated.
11.5.3 Other Common Adjustments
— Tax Revisions. Tax revisions may result in salary adjustments when tax calculations are updated based on new information, corrections, or changes in tax laws. Tax revisions ensure that tax deductions are accurate and that employees' tax obligations are met correctly. The Company ensures that tax revisions are applied accurately and that employees are informed of tax adjustments.
— Late Attendance Penalties. Late attendance penalties may be applied in accordance with Company attendance policies, resulting in salary adjustments. Attendance penalties are applied fairly and in accordance with established policies. The Company ensures that attendance penalties are applied consistently and that employees understand attendance requirements and penalties.
— Insurance Premium Deductions. Insurance premium deductions may result in salary adjustments when insurance premiums are deducted from salary. Insurance deductions are applied in accordance with benefit enrollment and coverage. The Company ensures that insurance deductions are accurate and that employees understand their insurance coverage and costs.
— Salary Advance Recovery. Recovery of salary advances may result in salary adjustments when salary advances are repaid through payroll deductions. Salary advance recovery is applied in accordance with advance agreements and repayment schedules. The Company ensures that salary advance recovery is applied accurately and that employees understand advance repayment terms.
11.5.4 Adjustment Authorization and Approval
— Management Authorization. All adjustments are authorized by the appropriate level of management before processing, ensuring that adjustments are approved and that controls are maintained. Authorization processes ensure that adjustments are reviewed and approved by appropriate personnel. The Company maintains authorization processes that ensure control and accountability.
— Adjustment Query Process. Employees who believe an adjustment has been applied incorrectly are encouraged to raise a formal query with HR within 7 working days of receiving their payslip. Query processes enable employees to challenge adjustments and to have concerns addressed. The Company ensures that adjustment queries are investigated promptly and that employees receive fair resolution.

11.6 Reimbursements and Expense Management
11.6.1 Reimbursement Philosophy
— Supporting Work-Related Expenses. The Company reimburses employees for eligible work-related expenses incurred genuinely in the course of their professional duties. This reflects the Company's commitment to ensuring that employees are not financially disadvantaged as a result of carrying out their responsibilities. Reimbursement policies ensure that employees are reimbursed for legitimate business expenses and that the Company's financial interests are protected. The Company ensures that reimbursement policies are fair, clear, and consistently applied.
— Eligible Expense Categories. Reimbursable expenses may include travel costs, accommodation, client entertainment, mobile and internet allowances, and other categories as defined in the employee's offer letter or applicable reimbursement policy. Eligible expenses are clearly defined and communicated to employees. The Company ensures that eligible expenses are reasonable, necessary, and aligned with business needs.
11.6.2 Reimbursement Submission Process
— Claim Submission Requirements. Employees must submit their reimbursement claims through the HRMS portal or the prescribed claim form, accompanied by all required supporting documents such as original receipts, tax invoices, boarding passes, or travel records. Claim submission requirements ensure that reimbursement claims are properly documented and that claims can be verified. The Company ensures that claim submission processes are clear and that employees understand requirements.
— Supporting Documentation. Supporting documentation is required for all reimbursement claims, including receipts, invoices, and other relevant documents that verify expenses. Documentation requirements ensure that expenses are legitimate and verifiable. The Company ensures that documentation requirements are clear and that employees understand what documentation is needed.
11.6.3 Reimbursement Processing
— Claim Review and Approval. Reimbursement claims are reviewed and approved by appropriate personnel, ensuring that claims are legitimate, properly documented, and within policy guidelines. Claim review processes ensure that reimbursements are appropriate and that the Company's financial interests are protected. The Company ensures that claim review is thorough and that approvals are based on policy compliance and business justification.
— Processing Timelines. Approved reimbursements are processed and credited to the employee's registered bank account within 7 to 10 working days of final claim approval. Processing timelines ensure that employees receive reimbursements promptly. The Company ensures that reimbursement processing is efficient and that timelines are met consistently.
11.6.4 Reimbursement Guidelines and Requirements
— Submission Deadlines. Claims submitted after the prescribed monthly deadline may be held over to the following month's processing cycle without exception, unless prior approval for a late submission has been granted by HR. Submission deadlines ensure that reimbursement processing is efficient and that claims are processed in a timely manner. The Company ensures that deadlines are communicated clearly and that late submissions are handled appropriately.
— Documentation Retention. Employees are strongly advised to retain personal copies of all submitted documents until the reimbursement has been confirmed and credited, as originals may be required for audit or verification purposes. Documentation retention ensures that employees have records of their claims and that documentation is available if needed. The Company ensures that documentation retention requirements are communicated clearly.
— Incomplete Claims. Incomplete or undocumented claims will not be processed and will be returned to the employee for correction and resubmission within the stipulated window. Incomplete claim handling ensures that only properly documented claims are processed. The Company ensures that incomplete claims are identified promptly and that employees are informed of what is needed to complete their claims.
11.6.5 Reimbursement Compliance and Fraud Prevention
— Fraud Prevention. Reimbursement claims that are found to be fraudulent, exaggerated, or unsupported by genuine documentation will be treated as a serious disciplinary matter and may result in recovery of amounts paid and further action in accordance with the Company's code of conduct. Fraud prevention protects the Company's financial interests and ensures that reimbursement policies are respected. The Company ensures that fraudulent claims are identified and that appropriate action is taken.
— Audit and Verification. Reimbursement claims may be subject to audit and verification to ensure compliance with policies and to prevent fraud. Audit processes ensure that reimbursements are legitimate and that policies are followed. The Company ensures that audit processes are thorough and that they protect both the Company and employees.
