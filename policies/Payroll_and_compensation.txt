POLICY 9: PAYROLL POLICY 

1. PURPOSE
This Payroll Policy establishes the organization's standards, processes, timelines, and controls governing the accurate, timely, and lawful payment of employee compensation. It ensures that all employees understand how and when they are paid, what their pay comprises, how errors are corrected, and what they are entitled to in respect of allowances, deductions, and benefits. The organization is committed to ensuring payroll is processed with precision, transparency, and absolute compliance with applicable tax and employment law.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all employees of the organization in all locations, including full-time staff, part-time staff, and fixed-term contract employees. Agency workers and contractors are paid under the terms of their respective agency or commercial agreements and are not governed by this policy unless explicitly stated.

3. PAYROLL CYCLE
3.1 Monthly Salary Payment
All permanent and fixed-term employees are paid on a monthly payroll cycle. Salaries are processed by the Payroll team on the last working day of each calendar month and credited to the employee's designated bank account within one (1) to three (3) banking business days, subject to the employee's bank's processing times.
3.2 Payroll Cut-Off Date
Any changes affecting the current month's payroll — including new joiner details, bank account changes, variable pay submissions, expense reimbursements, salary adjustments, and overtime claims — must be submitted to the Payroll team by the 15th calendar day of each month (the "Payroll Cut-Off Date"). Changes received after this date will be processed in the following month's payroll cycle, except in cases of payroll error (see Section 8).
3.3 New Joiners
New employees who join on or before the 15th of the month will receive their first salary payment at the end of that calendar month (pro-rated for the days worked). Employees who join after the 15th will receive their first payment at the end of the following month, covering both the partial joining month and the full second month.

4. COMPONENTS OF PAY
4.1 Base Salary
Base salary is the fixed, guaranteed monthly compensation agreed in the employee's contract of employment. It is paid in full every month regardless of fluctuations in business performance, unless the employee is subject to unpaid leave, suspension without pay, or salary reduction as a result of a formal process.
4.2 Variable Pay & Bonuses
Variable pay — including performance bonuses, sales incentives, and profit-sharing distributions — is paid in accordance with the terms of the relevant Variable Pay Scheme in effect at the time. Bonus payments are discretionary unless explicitly guaranteed in writing in the employment contract or a separate bonus letter. Variable pay is subject to applicable income tax and social contributions.
4.3 Overtime Pay
Overtime is only payable to employees whose contract of employment expressly provides for overtime payment. Non-contractual overtime worked by salaried employees is not automatically payable and must be pre-approved by the line manager. Where overtime pay is applicable, it is calculated as follows:
- Weekday overtime (Monday–Saturday): 1.25x the hourly rate.
- Sunday and public holiday overtime: 1.5x the hourly rate.
All overtime must be submitted via the Time and Attendance module in the HRMS by the Payroll Cut-Off Date each month.
4.4 Allowances
- Employees may be entitled to the following allowances depending on their role, grade, and location:
- Travel / Commuting Allowance: A fixed monthly allowance for employees in roles that require regular travel, as specified in the employment contract.
- Mobile Phone Allowance: A fixed monthly allowance for employees required to be contactable outside of standard office hours, as specified in the contract.
- Meal / Food Allowance: Available to employees in qualifying roles or locations as specified in the compensation schedule.
- Housing Allowance: Applicable to expatriate employees and certain specialist roles where the organization has agreed to provide housing support, as set out in the relocation or expatriate agreement.
- Night Shift / Unsociable Hours Allowance: A supplement for employees regularly working shifts between 10:00 PM and 6:00 AM, at the rate specified in the applicable compensation schedule.
All allowances are subject to applicable tax treatment in the employee's jurisdiction.
4.5 Statutory Deductions
The organization is legally required to deduct the following from employee gross pay prior to payment:
- Income Tax / Pay As You Earn (PAYE) — at the rate applicable to the employee's tax code and jurisdiction.
- Employee National Insurance Contributions (NIC) or equivalent social security contributions.
- Court-ordered attachments of earnings (where applicable).
- Any other statutory deductions required by applicable law.

4.6 Voluntary Deductions
With the employee's written consent, the following voluntary deductions may be applied:
- Pension / Provident Fund contributions (employee share).
- Health insurance premium employee contributions.
- Employee share purchase scheme contributions.
- Salary sacrifice arrangements (cycle-to-work, childcare vouchers, annual travel pass, etc.).
- Union membership dues (where applicable).
- Loan repayments (for approved company loans or salary advances).

5. PAYSLIPS
5.1 Payslip Issuance
Electronic payslips are issued to every employee via the HRMS self-service portal on or before the salary payment date each month. Employees are notified by email when their payslip is available. Payslips are password-protected and accessible only by the individual employee.
5.2 Payslip Contents
Every payslip contains the following information:
- Employee name, employee ID, and department.
- Pay period covered.
- Gross pay (base salary plus any additional pay elements).
- Itemized breakdown of all earnings (base, bonus, overtime, allowances).
- Itemized breakdown of all deductions (tax, NIC, pension, etc.).
- Net pay (the amount credited to the employee's bank account).
- Year-to-date (YTD) totals for gross pay, tax, and other deductions.
- Employer's NIC contribution (for reference).
- Employer's pension contribution (for reference).
- Tax code in effect.
- Cumulative leave balance (where integrated with HRMS).

5.3 Retention of Payslips
Payslips are stored securely in the HRMS for a minimum of seven (7) years from the date of issue. Employees who leave the organization retain access to their payslips for twelve (12) months following their last day of employment. Employees requiring payslips beyond this period must submit a written request to the Payroll team.

6. BANK ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT
6.1 Bank Account Registration
Employees must register their bank account details (account holder name, account number, sort code / IFSC code, and bank name) in the HRMS Self-Service Portal within their first three (3) working days of employment to ensure timely payment. The organization will only pay salaries into a bank account registered in the employee's own name.
6.2 Bank Account Changes
Employees wishing to update their bank account details must do so through the HRMS Self-Service Portal. Changes must be submitted before the Payroll Cut-Off Date (15th of the month) to take effect in the current month's payroll. The Payroll team will send an email confirmation to the employee's corporate email address when a bank account change is processed. If you receive such a notification and have not made a change, contact the Payroll team immediately.
6.3 Failed Payments
If a salary payment fails due to incorrect bank details or a closed account, the Payroll team will notify the employee on the payment date. The employee must provide corrected bank details by 12:00 PM the following business day. Re-payment will be processed within two (2) business days of receipt of the corrected details.

7. SALARY ADVANCES
Salary advances (early payment of a portion of the current month's earned salary) may be requested in genuine financial hardship situations. The following conditions apply:
- The employee must have completed their probationary period.
- The request must not exceed fifty percent (50%) of the employee's current month's net salary.
- A maximum of two (2) salary advances may be granted per calendar year.
- The advance is recovered in full from the employee's salary in the month following the advance.
- The request must be submitted in writing to HR and Finance, with supporting context. Approval is at the discretion of the HR Business Partner and Finance Director.


8. PAYROLL ERRORS & CORRECTIONS
8.1 Reporting an Error
Employees who believe there is an error in their payslip must report it in writing to the Payroll team (payroll@[company].com) within ten (10) working days of receiving the payslip, providing details of the discrepancy and supporting documentation where available.
8.2 Underpayment
Where an underpayment is confirmed, the organization will process a corrective payment to the employee within five (5) working days of the error being confirmed, regardless of the standard payroll cycle.
8.3 Overpayment
Where an overpayment has occurred, the Payroll team will notify the employee in writing, explaining the nature and amount of the overpayment. The recovery of overpayments will be agreed with the employee in advance and will be handled in a manner that does not cause undue financial hardship. Where possible, recovery will be made in installments over a period not exceeding three (3) months. Any disputes regarding overpayment recovery must be raised with the HR Business Partner.

9. FINAL PAY ON TERMINATION
Upon termination of employment (for any reason), the employee's final payroll will be processed in the standard monthly cycle and will include:
- Base salary for all days worked up to and including the last day of employment (pro-rated if not a full month).
- Any accrued but untaken annual leave, paid at the employee's base daily rate.
- Any outstanding expenses that have been approved and not yet reimbursed.
- Any contractually guaranteed bonus or commission payments due as of the termination date.
- Deduction of any outstanding salary advances or company loans.
- Deduction of any salary paid in advance in lieu of notice (where applicable).
A final payslip will be issued via the HRMS. Access to the HRMS for payslip retrieval will remain available to the former employee for twelve (12) months.

10. EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
10.1 Eligible Expenses
The organization reimburses employees for reasonable, pre-approved, and properly documented business expenses incurred in the course of performing their duties, including:
- Business travel (flights, trains, taxis, mileage for use of personal vehicle at the approved mileage rate).
- Accommodation for approved business travel.
- Business meals and client entertainment (within the approved per-person limits set out in the Expenses Policy).
- Professional membership fees that are directly relevant to the employee's role (subject to manager approval).
- Work-related training materials and books (subject to L&D policy limits).
10.2 Expense Submission
All expenses must be submitted through the Expense Management module in the HRMS, with original receipts attached (digital or paper). Expenses must be submitted within thirty (30) days of the expenditure. Claims submitted more than thirty (30) days after the expense date will not be reimbursed without a written explanation approved by the Finance Director.
10.3 Approval Process
Expenses up to [currency equivalent of USD 500]: Approved by the line manager.
Expenses above [currency equivalent of USD 500]: Approved by the line manager and the Finance Director.
Expenses above [currency equivalent of USD 2,000]: Require VP-level approval.
Approved expenses submitted before the Payroll Cut-Off Date are reimbursed in that month's payroll.
