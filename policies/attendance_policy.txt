2 Attendance Policy

2.1 Purpose
This policy establishes clear guidelines for employee attendance, punctuality, and time tracking
to ensure operational efficiency while maintaining work-life balance.

2.2 Scope
This policy applies to all full-time, part-time, and contract employees across all departments and
locations.

2.3 Policy Details

2.3.1 Working Hours
Standard working hours: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Monday to Friday)
Total hours per week: 40 hours (excluding lunch break)
Lunch break: 1 hour (not included in working hours)
Grace period: 15 minutes (attendance marked late after 9:15 AM)

2.3.2 Attendance Marking
• Attendance must be marked through the official HRMS system or biometric device
• Employees working remotely must mark attendance through the company portal
• Failure to mark attendance may result in Leave Without Pay (LWP)
• In case of system failure, notify your manager and HR immediately

2.3.3 Late Arrivals
• Arrival after 9:15 AM is considered late
• Late arrivals must be informed to the reporting manager via email/chat
• 3 late arrivals in a month = 1 day of Casual Leave deduction
• Frequent late arrivals (5+ times per month) may lead to disciplinary action
• Medical or emergency situations will be considered on a case-by-case basis

2.3.4 Early Departures
• Any departure before 6:00 PM requires manager approval
• Early departure requests must be submitted at least 2 hours in advance
• Emergency early departures should be communicated immediately
• Frequent unapproved early departures may result in leave deduction

2.3.5 Attendance Violations
Progressive disciplinary action:
• 1st violation: Verbal warning
• 2nd violation: Written warning
• 3rd violation: Final warning with performance improvement plan
• 4th violation: Termination consideration

2.3.6 Monthly Review
• Attendance records are reviewed on the last working day of each month
• Managers receive attendance reports for their teams
• HR conducts quarterly attendance audits
• Discrepancies should be raised within 5 working days

2.4 Special Situations

2.4.1 Probation Period
Probationary employees are expected to maintain 100% attendance except for approved
leaves. Poor attendance during probation may result in extension or termination of employment.

2.4.2 Remote Work Attendance
Employees working from home must be available during core hours (10:00 AM - 4:00 PM) and
mark attendance as per WFH Policy (HR-WFH-001).

2.4.3 Field Work & Client Visits
Employees on field assignments must inform their manager and mark attendance with location
details in the HRMS system.
