POLICY 1: ATTENDANCE & PUNCTUALITY POLICY

1. PURPOSE
This Attendance and Punctuality Policy establishes clear standards and expectations for employee attendance, timekeeping, and absence management across the organization. Reliable attendance is foundational to operational efficiency, team cohesion, client service, and the achievement of organizational goals. This policy ensures all employees are treated fairly, consistently, and with respect while also upholding the organization's ability to function effectively.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all individuals employed by the organization, including full-time employees, part-time employees, fixed-term contract employees, and temporary staff across all departments, business units, and geographic locations. Where local labor laws or collective bargaining agreements impose different requirements, those requirements shall apply alongside this policy, and any conflicts shall be resolved in favor of the more employee-protective standard.

3. POLICY STATEMENT
The organization recognizes that employees are humans with lives outside of work and that illness, emergencies, and personal circumstances are unavoidable. This policy is designed to balance the business's operational requirements with a compassionate and structured approach to managing absence. The organization is committed to applying this policy without discrimination based on gender, age, race, disability, pregnancy, religion, or any other protected characteristic.

4. DEFINITIONS
- Absence: Any period during which an employee is not present at work during their scheduled working hours, regardless of reason.
- Unplanned Absence: An absence that occurs without advance notice — generally, notification provided less than two (2) hours before the scheduled start time.
- Planned Absence: An absence that has been applied for through the HR system and approved by the line manager in advance.
- Tardiness / Late Arrival: Arriving more than five (5) minutes after the scheduled start time without prior approval from the line manager.
- Early Departure: Leaving the workplace more than fifteen (15) minutes before the scheduled end of shift without manager approval.
- Absence Pattern: A recurring tendency of absences that follow a recognizable pattern, such as consistent absences on Mondays or Fridays, absences immediately before or after public holidays, or absences immediately following the denial of a leave request.
- Consecutive Absence: An unbroken period of three (3) or more calendar working days of absence.
- AWOL (Absent Without Leave): Failure to report to work for two (2) or more consecutive working days without providing any notification or obtaining approved leave. AWOL may be treated as voluntary abandonment of employment.
- Return-to-Work Interview: A structured, documented meeting conducted by the line manager with an employee upon their return from any absence of three (3) or more consecutive working days.
- Occupational Health (OH): The organization's internal or contracted medical advisory function that provides independent, impartial advice on fitness for work and workplace adjustments.

5. WORKING HOURS & SCHEDULING
5.1 Standard Working Hours
Standard business hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday, inclusive of a one (1) hour unpaid lunch break, totaling forty (40) working hours per week. Employees in operational or client-facing roles may have different scheduled hours as defined in their employment contract or offer letter.
5.2 Shift Schedules
Employees assigned to shift-based roles are governed by their shift schedule. Shift rosters are communicated a minimum of two (2) weeks in advance. Shift changes requested by the organization will be communicated with a minimum of forty-eight (48) hours' notice except in genuine emergencies. Employees requesting shift swaps must obtain written approval from their manager at least twenty-four (24) hours prior to the scheduled shift.
5.3 Grace Period
A grace period of five (5) minutes is extended for late arrival. Arrival beyond this grace period is recorded as a late instance in the attendance system. Three (3) or more late instances within any rolling thirty (30)-day period will result in a formal discussion with the line manager and may trigger Stage 1 of the Attendance Review Process (see Section 8).
5.4 Flexible Working
Employees who have been granted a flexible working arrangement under the Work From Home Policy (HR-POL-006) remain subject to this attendance policy. Core hours for flexible workers are 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM unless an alternative arrangement has been formally approved.

6. REPORTING AN ABSENCE
6.1 Notification Requirement
Every employee must personally notify their direct line manager of any unplanned absence as early as possible and no later than one (1) hour before their scheduled start time on the first day of absence. Sending a text message or email alone is not acceptable as the primary form of notification unless the manager has explicitly authorized such a method in writing.
6.2 Notification Process
Step 1 — The employee must call their line manager by phone. If the manager is unreachable, the employee must contact the next-level manager or designated department alternate.
Step 2 — Following the phone call, the employee must update their status in the HR attendance portal (HRMS) within two (2) hours of their scheduled start time.
Step 3 — For absences lasting more than three (3) consecutive days, the employee must provide a medical certificate from a licensed medical practitioner.
Step 4 — If absence is expected to exceed five (5) working days, the employee must contact the HR Business Partner directly to explore leave entitlement and support options.
6.3 Failure to Notify
Failure to provide adequate and timely notification of absence, absent exceptional mitigating circumstances, will result in the absence being classified as unauthorized. Unauthorized absence is unpaid and may trigger disciplinary proceedings.
6.4 Third-Party Notification
In extraordinary circumstances where the employee is physically incapable of notifying the organization themselves (e.g., hospitalization, serious accident), a family member, caregiver, or representative may notify on their behalf. This must be followed by the employee's own notification as soon as they are reasonably able to do so.

7. MEDICAL DOCUMENTATION
A valid medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner must be submitted to HR within two (2) working days of return in the following circumstances:

-  Any illness-related absence of three (3) or more consecutive working days.
-  Any absence where HR or the line manager has identified a concerning pattern of short-term illness.
-  Return from any long-term absence of four (4) or more weeks.
-  Any absence arising from a workplace injury or occupational disease.
-  Any absence during a formal attendance improvement plan period.

The organization reserves the right to request an independent Occupational Health assessment in addition to the medical certificate where it is reasonable and necessary to do so. All medical information is treated as strictly confidential and stored in compliance with applicable data protection laws.

8. ATTENDANCE REVIEW PROCESS
8.1 Attendance Records
All attendance data is recorded in the organization's HRMS. Employees are responsible for accurately logging their attendance using the designated system (biometric scanner, badge swipe, or digital portal). Deliberate falsification of attendance records — including clocking in or out on behalf of another employee — constitutes gross misconduct and may result in immediate dismissal.
8.2 Monthly Review
HR and line managers review attendance data on a rolling monthly basis. Where attendance falls below acceptable standards, the following staged approach applies:
Stage 1 — Informal Discussion: The manager meets informally with the employee to discuss attendance concerns, understand contributing factors, and agree on improvement expectations. A note of the discussion is placed on file. No formal warning is issued at this stage.
Stage 2 — Formal Counselling Session: If attendance does not improve within thirty (30) days of the Stage 1 discussion, a formal meeting is convened with HR present. Attendance targets are documented, and a support plan is agreed upon. A formal record is placed on the employee's HR file.
Stage 3 — Written Warning: If the agreed targets are not met within sixty (60) days of the Stage 2 session, a formal written warning is issued. This warning remains on the employee's file for twelve (12) months.
Stage 4 — Final Written Warning or Termination: Continued failure to meet attendance expectations following a written warning may result in a final written warning or termination of employment on the grounds of capability or conduct, depending on the nature of the absence.
8.3 Bradford Factor
The organization may utilize the Bradford Factor scoring model to identify patterns of intermittent absence. The formula is: Bradford Score = S² × D, where S equals the number of absence instances in a rolling twelve (12)-month period, and D equals the total number of working days absent. Bradford Factor scores are considered as one data point among many and are never used in isolation to trigger disciplinary action. Individual circumstances, including disability, pregnancy-related illness, and approved conditions, are always taken into account.

9. LONG-TERM ABSENCE
An employee absent from work for four (4) or more consecutive weeks is classified as being on long-term absence. The following applies:
- HR will make contact with the employee no later than the end of week two (2) to provide support information and check on wellbeing.
- A formal welfare meeting will be held by week four (4), either in person or by phone/video call, depending on the employee's condition.
- An Occupational Health referral will be made at week six (6) if no clear return-to-work date has been established.
- The organization will explore all reasonable adjustments — including phased return, reduced hours, altered duties, or workspace modifications — before considering dismissal on grounds of capability.
- Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) or the company sick pay scheme (whichever is applicable) will be administered in accordance with the employee's contract and local law.
- The organization will not contact the employee excessively or in a manner that impedes recovery. Contact will be proportionate, respectful, and purposeful.


10. RETURN TO WORK
Upon return from any absence of three (3) or more consecutive days, the line manager will conduct and document a Return-to-Work Interview within the employee's first day back. The purpose of this interview is to:

- Welcome the employee back warmly and check on their wellbeing.
- Confirm that the employee is fit and able to perform their duties.
- Identify and arrange any support, adjustments, or temporary modifications to workload.
- Update the employee on developments that occurred during their absence.
- Record and acknowledge the reason for absence.
- Identify if the absence triggers any further support processes such as an Occupational Health referral.

Return-to-Work Interview records are stored confidentially in the employee's HR file.

11. SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES
11.1 Disability-Related Absence
Absences that are caused by or linked to a registered or disclosed disability will be monitored and managed separately. The organization has a duty to make reasonable adjustments under applicable equality law. Disability-related absences will not automatically trigger the attendance review process without independent HR and legal review.
11.2 Pregnancy-Related Absence
Absences arising from pregnancy-related illness or complications must not be used to trigger attendance reviews or disciplinary action. Such absences are recorded separately and are protected under applicable employment legislation.
11.3 Workplace Injury or Industrial Disease
Absences caused by a work-related injury or industrial disease are managed in close coordination with the Health and Safety team and Occupational Health, and will not be used punitively in attendance reviews.

12. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
Employee: Report absences in a timely manner, provide required documentation, participate in welfare and return-to-work meetings, and meet attendance standards.
Line Manager: Monitor attendance records, conduct return-to-work interviews, initiate the attendance review process where required, and support employees with reasonable adjustments.
HR Business Partner: Provide guidance to managers, maintain confidential records, manage long-term absence cases, liaise with Occupational Health, and ensure policy is applied consistently and lawfully.
Occupational Health: Provide independent medical advice on fitness for work, reasonable adjustments, and long-term prognosis.

13. CONFIDENTIALITY
All attendance and medical records are maintained in strict confidence. Access is restricted to the employee, their line manager, HR, Occupational Health, and senior management on a strictly need-to-know basis. Unauthorized disclosure of an employee's health or attendance information to colleagues or third parties constitutes a disciplinary offense.

14. POLICY REVIEW
This policy will be reviewed annually by the HR Department. Any significant changes to legislation or organizational structure may trigger an interim review. Employees will be notified of any revisions via internal communications.
