POLICY 10: ONBOARDING POLICY & ASSISTANT FRAMEWORK

1. PURPOSE
This Onboarding Policy defines the organization's approach to welcoming, integrating, and enabling new employees to become fully productive, engaged, and culturally connected members of the organization as quickly and smoothly as possible. The organization recognizes that the onboarding experience has a profound and lasting impact on employee engagement, retention, and long-term performance. This policy establishes the standards, timelines, responsibilities, and tools — including the AI-powered Onboarding Assistant — that together form the organization's end-to-end onboarding experience.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all new employees joining the organization on a permanent or fixed-term contract. It also applies to employees who are transferred between business units or geographic locations, and to employees returning from an extended leave of absence of six (6) or more months, who will undergo a modified re-onboarding process.
It further applies to all stakeholders involved in the onboarding process, including HR, IT, Facilities, Legal, Finance, and the hiring manager.

3. ONBOARDING PHILOSOPHY
The organization's onboarding philosophy is built on four pillars:
- Connection: New employees are connected to the culture, values, people, and community of the organization from their very first interaction.
- Clarity: New employees know exactly what is expected of them, how they will be measured, where to go for help, and what resources are available to them.
- Competence: New employees receive the training, tools, access, and support they need to become productive in their role as quickly as possible.
- Confidence: New employees feel valued, welcomed, and confident in their decision to join the organization.

4. PRE-BOARDING (OFFER ACCEPTANCE TO DAY 1)
The onboarding experience begins the moment a candidate accepts an offer of employment — not on their first day. The following activities are completed during the pre-boarding phase:
4.1 Offer Documentation
Within three (3) business days of offer acceptance, HR dispatches the following documentation to the new employee's personal email address:
- Signed employment contract.
- Employee Handbook (digital copy).
- HR Policy Manual acknowledgement form.
- Background check authorization form (where applicable).
- Bank account and tax form collection request.
- Benefits enrollment guide and forms.

4.2 AI Onboarding Assistant Activation
The new employee is granted early access to the organization's AI-powered Onboarding Assistant via a personal login link. The Onboarding Assistant guides the new employee through:
- Completing all pre-joining digital paperwork.
- Understanding the organization's culture, values, and strategic priorities.
- Learning about the team they will be joining and key stakeholders.
- Getting answers to common pre-joining questions (start time, dress code, where to go on Day 1, parking, lunch, etc.).
- Accessing a personalized onboarding checklist and timeline.
- Completing mandatory pre-joining compliance modules (data protection, code of conduct, health and safety awareness).
The Onboarding Assistant is available 24/7, enabling new joiners in different time zones and with varying schedules to progress at their own pace.
4.3 IT & System Setup
The IT team is notified of every confirmed new joiner a minimum of ten (10) working days before their start date by HR. IT is responsible for provisioning the following before Day 1:
- Corporate laptop or desktop computer, configured and ready.
- Corporate email account and calendar.
- Access to all core systems required for the role.
- VPN access and security software.
- Corporate mobile phone (where applicable to the role).
- Microsoft Teams / collaboration platform profile.
- Building access card or visitor registration (in liaison with Facilities).
Failure by IT to provision access on time must be escalated to the IT Director and HR within forty-eight (48) hours of the identified delay.
4.4 Pre-Joining Manager Contact
The new employee's line manager is required to make personal contact — by phone or video call — at least five (5) working days before the employee's start date to welcome them, confirm Day 1 logistics, answer any immediate questions, and create an early personal connection.
4.5 Buddy Assignment
Every new employee is assigned an Onboarding Buddy — an experienced peer from within or adjacent to their team — at least five (5) working days before their start date. The Buddy is responsible for providing informal guidance, social integration support, and day-to-day assistance during the new employee's first ninety (90) days. Buddy selection and briefing is managed by HR in coordination with the hiring manager.

5. DAY 1 EXPERIENCE
Day 1 must be a positive, memorable, and well-organized experience. The following standards apply:
5.1 Welcome Arrangements
The new employee must be met at reception (or virtually, for remote starters) by either their line manager or their Buddy. A welcome message from the senior leader of the business unit must be sent on Day 1 via email or the collaboration platform.
5.2 Day 1 Schedule
A detailed Day 1 schedule is prepared by HR in advance and shared with both the new employee and their manager. The schedule must include:
- Welcome meeting with the line manager.
- Tour of the workplace (or virtual office tour for remote starters).
- Introduction to immediate team members.
- Device collection and IT setup session.
- HR induction briefing (payroll details, HRMS walkthrough, leave system, benefits).
- Health and safety briefing (fire exits, first aid, emergency procedures).
- Access to the Onboarding Assistant for checklist progress review.
- A relaxed team lunch or virtual social activity.
5.3 Documentation Completion
All remaining joining documentation (bank details, emergency contacts, benefits enrollment, tax declarations) must be completed in the HRMS on Day 1, with HR support available throughout.

6. STRUCTURED ONBOARDING PROGRAMME (FIRST 90 DAYS)
The organization operates a structured 30-60-90 day onboarding framework for all new employees.
6.1 First 30 Days — Learn & Connect
The primary goal of the first thirty (30) days is for the new employee to understand the organization, their team, their role, and the culture. During this period:
- The Onboarding Assistant provides a daily check-in prompt to guide the employee through their onboarding checklist.
- The new employee completes all mandatory induction training (see Section 7).
- The line manager conducts a formal one-to-one check-in meeting each week.
- The new employee meets with a minimum of ten (10) key stakeholders (guided by the stakeholder mapping exercise in the Onboarding Assistant).
- A formal thirty (30)-day review meeting is held with the line manager and HR to assess integration progress and identify any support needs.

6.2 Days 31 to 60 — Contribute & Develop
During this phase, the new employee begins taking on full responsibilities and contributing independently. The focus shifts from passive learning to active performance:

- The employee's SMART performance goals for the current cycle are formally set and documented in the HRMS.
- The employee identifies key learning and development priorities with their manager.
- Weekly one-to-ones continue between the employee and manager.
- The Buddy relationship continues, with at least one structured conversation per fortnight.
- A formal sixty (60)-day review is conducted by the line manager.

6.3 Days 61 to 90 — Perform & Integrate
By the end of ninety (90) days, the employee should be operating fully independently in their role and feeling genuinely part of the organizational community:
- The employee's performance against their thirty (30)-day goals is reviewed.
- The employee completes a ninety (90)-day self-assessment in the HRMS.
- A formal ninety (90)-day review is conducted by the line manager, with HR present.
- The Buddy relationship is formally concluded (though the social connection is encouraged to continue).
- HR conducts a New Joiner Experience Survey with the employee to capture feedback on the onboarding process.
- If on probation, the probationary review timeline is activated (see Performance Management Policy HR-POL-005).


7. ONBOARDING FOR REMOTE & HYBRID EMPLOYEES
Remote and hybrid new joiners receive the full onboarding experience described in this policy, delivered virtually. Additional provisions for remote starters include:
- IT equipment is couriered to the employee's registered home address, arriving no later than the working day before their start date.
- A virtual welcome session is organized with the entire team via video conferencing on Day 1.
- The line manager commits to daily video check-ins during the employee's first two (2) weeks.
- The Onboarding Assistant is the primary support tool and is supplemented by more frequent HR check-ins.
- The employee is invited to attend the nearest office location for at least one (1) week during their first thirty (30) days to build in-person connections with the team.


8. ONBOARDING ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES
- HR Department: Owns and orchestrates the end-to-end onboarding program. Responsible for documentation, HRMS setup, Onboarding Assistant management, induction training scheduling, and milestone reviews.
- Line Manager: Responsible for the personal welcome, Day 1 schedule execution, weekly one-to-ones, goal setting, and the thirty, sixty, and ninety-day formal reviews. The manager's engagement is the single most important factor in onboarding success.
- IT Department: Responsible for ensuring all technology, access, and equipment is fully provisioned before Day 1. Any provisioning failures must be reported and resolved within twenty-four (24) hours.
- Onboarding Buddy: Provides informal guidance, social support, and cultural integration assistance. Meets with the new employee at least once per week during the first ninety (90) days.
- New Employee: Takes an active and proactive role in their own onboarding. Completes all mandatory training, tasks, and check-ins on time. Engages honestly with milestone reviews and surveys.

9. ONBOARDING SUCCESS METRICS
The HR team tracks the following metrics to continuously evaluate and improve the onboarding experience:
- Time to full productivity (assessed by line manager at ninety-day review).
- Ninety-day retention rate (percentage of new joiners still employed at day ninety).
- New Joiner Satisfaction Score (via the thirty, sixty, and ninety-day surveys).
- Mandatory training completion rate within thirty days.
- Day 1 IT readiness rate (percentage of new joiners with full system access on Day 1).
- Buddy satisfaction rating.
