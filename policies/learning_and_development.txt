Learning & Development Policy

1. Purpose
This policy exists to establish a clear and structured framework for learning and development across the organization — one that enhances individual capability, supports meaningful career growth, and drives sustained organizational performance. The Company recognizes that its people are its most valuable asset, and that investing in their continuous development is not a discretionary activity but a strategic imperative. In a rapidly evolving business environment, the ability to learn, adapt, and grow is what distinguishes high-performing organizations from the rest.
This policy reflects the Company's commitment to building a culture of continuous learning where every employee — regardless of their role, grade, or tenure — has access to the tools, programs, and opportunities they need to grow professionally and contribute at their highest potential. It is designed to ensure that learning initiatives are purposeful, equitable, and aligned with both individual aspirations and broader business objectives. The Company views learning not as a one-time event but as an ongoing journey, and this policy is the foundation upon which that journey is built.

2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees of the Company across all levels, functions, locations, and work arrangements including on-site, remote, and hybrid employees. While the commitment to development is universal, specific program eligibility, access, and prioritization may vary based on role requirements, performance ratings, business needs, and the overall talent development strategy at any given point in time. Contractors and third-party personnel may be included in specific compliance or role-critical training programs at the discretion of HR and the relevant business unit.

3. Learning Categories
The Company's learning and development framework is organized into four core categories, each designed to address a distinct and important dimension of employee growth and organizational capability.

3.1 Mandatory Compliance Training
Mandatory compliance training is non-negotiable and applies to all employees without exception. These programs are designed to ensure that every individual understands and adheres to the legal, regulatory, ethical, and policy obligations that govern their conduct within the organization and in relation to external stakeholders.
- All employees are required to complete mandatory compliance training within the timelines prescribed by HR, typically within the first 30 days of joining and annually thereafter.
- Topics covered under this category include but are not limited to Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH), data protection and information security, anti-bribery and anti-corruption, workplace safety, and the Company's Code of Conduct.
- Non-completion of mandatory training within the stipulated timeline will be flagged by HR and may be treated as a compliance breach, with escalation to the reporting manager and relevant department head.
- Completion records are maintained in the HRMS and are available for audit and regulatory review at any time.


3.2 Functional & Technical Training
Functional and technical training is role-specific and is designed to equip employees with the domain knowledge, technical skills, and process expertise required to perform their responsibilities at the highest level of competence.
- These programs are identified through a combination of role requirements, skill gap assessments, manager inputs, and evolving business needs.
- Training may be delivered through internal subject matter experts, external trainers, industry certifications, or a blended approach depending on the nature of the skill being developed.
- Employees are encouraged to proactively discuss their functional development needs with their reporting manager during goal-setting and appraisal conversations.
- Newly hired employees will receive a structured functional induction plan as part of their onboarding to ensure they are equipped with the foundational knowledge required to perform their role from day one.
- Role-critical certifications that are required for regulatory or client compliance must be completed within the timelines specified by the relevant department head and HR.


3.3 Leadership Development
Leadership development programs are designed to build a strong, capable, and future-ready leadership pipeline within the organization. These programs target employees in managerial roles as well as those identified as high-potential talent who demonstrate the capability and aspiration to take on greater responsibility over time.
- Leadership programs may include structured management development courses, executive coaching, mentoring arrangements, cross-functional project exposure, and participation in external leadership forums or institutions.
- Identification of participants for leadership programs is carried out through the annual talent review and succession planning process, in consultation with senior leadership and HR.
- Employees nominated for leadership development programs are expected to engage fully, complete all program requirements, and apply their learning within their teams and day-to-day responsibilities.
- Leadership development is viewed as a long-term investment by the Company, and participants are expected to demonstrate a commensurate commitment to the organization in return.
- Outcomes from leadership programs will be considered in succession planning, promotion decisions, and role progression discussions.


3.4 Behavioral & Soft Skills Training
Behavioral and soft skills training addresses the interpersonal, communication, and professional effectiveness dimensions of an employee's development — capabilities that are often just as critical to success as technical expertise.
- Programs under this category include but are not limited to communication and presentation skills, time management and productivity, emotional intelligence, conflict resolution, collaboration, customer service excellence, and cross-cultural awareness.
- These programs are available to all employees and are particularly encouraged for those transitioning into new roles, taking on team management responsibilities, or identified as requiring development in specific behavioral areas through the appraisal process.
- Soft skills development needs may also be identified through 360-degree feedback, peer assessments, or as part of an individual's personal development plan.
- The Company strongly believes that behavioral excellence is a hallmark of a high-performance culture and actively supports employees in developing these capabilities at every stage of their careers.

4. Training Methods
The Company employs a variety of training delivery methods to ensure that learning experiences are engaging, accessible, and suited to the diverse needs of its workforce. The choice of method for any given program will be determined based on the nature of the content, the target audience, cost considerations, and desired outcomes.
- Instructor-Led Programs provide a structured, interactive, and collaborative learning environment, ideal for complex topics that benefit from real-time discussion, facilitation, and group learning dynamics.
- E-Learning Modules offer flexibility and self-paced learning, enabling employees to access training content at a time and place that suits their schedule — particularly valuable for mandatory compliance training and foundational knowledge programs.
- Workshops and Seminars provide focused, intensive learning experiences that are particularly effective for skill application, case study discussions, and peer-to-peer learning.
- External Certifications allow employees to gain recognized professional qualifications that enhance both their individual credibility and the organization's capability profile. These may be fully or partially sponsored by the Company based on relevance and business need.
- The Company may also leverage blended learning approaches, combining multiple methods to create a richer and more effective development experience where appropriate.


5. Eligibility & Governance
The Company is committed to making learning and development opportunities accessible to as many employees as possible. However, given the need to prioritize resources effectively, participation in specific programs may be governed by the following criteria:
- Performance Ratings — Employees with strong or developing performance ratings will be given priority access to development programs, particularly high-investment or competitive nominations.
- Manager Nomination — Many programs require formal nomination by the reporting manager, who is best placed to assess the employee's readiness, relevance of the program to their role, and potential return on the development investment.
- Business Requirements — Certain programs will be prioritized based on immediate or emerging business needs, ensuring that organizational capability keeps pace with strategic demands.
- Talent Development Strategy — Program access may also be guided by the Company's broader talent management strategy, including succession planning priorities and high-potential identification.
For high-cost external programs, certifications, or sponsored education, employees may be required to sign a service commitment agreement stipulating a minimum period of continued employment following completion of the program. The terms of such agreements will be clearly communicated and mutually agreed upon before the training commences. In the event of voluntary resignation before the agreed commitment period, a proportionate recovery of the training investment may be applicable as outlined in the agreement.

6. Career Impact
Learning and development is intrinsically linked to an employee's career trajectory within the organization. The Company views demonstrated commitment to growth and the practical application of learning as key indicators of an employee's potential and readiness for advancement.
- Completion of relevant training programs and demonstration of applied learning will be considered as part of promotion decisions, particularly where a role requires specific competencies that have been developed through formal learning.
- Sustained capability development and the acquisition of new skills may positively influence compensation adjustments during appraisal cycles, particularly where the new skills directly contribute to business value.
- Employees identified through development programs as high-potential talent will be considered in succession planning discussions, positioning them for critical roles as the organization grows and evolves.
- Active participation in learning and development will support role progression, including lateral moves into new functions, expanded responsibilities, or readiness for more senior positions.
- Employees are encouraged to take ownership of their development journey, maintain an updated personal development plan in consultation with their manager, and approach learning as a continuous and career-defining commitment rather than a periodic obligation.


7. Employee & Manager Responsibilities
The effectiveness of the learning and development function depends on active engagement and accountability at every level of the organization.

- Employees are responsible for engaging proactively with available learning opportunities, completing assigned training within prescribed timelines, applying their learning in their day-to-day work, and taking ownership of their personal and professional development.
- Managers are responsible for identifying development needs within their teams, nominating employees for appropriate programs, supporting employees in applying their learning on the job, and incorporating development conversations into regular one-on-one and appraisal discussions.
- HR & Talent Development is responsible for designing, curating, and delivering a relevant and high-quality learning calendar, tracking participation and completion, measuring the impact of learning interventions, and continuously improving the development offering in line with organizational needs.

8. Policy Review
- This policy will be reviewed annually by the HR and Talent Development departments, or earlier in response to changes in business strategy, workforce needs, or applicable legislation.
- Feedback from employees, managers, and post-program evaluations will be incorporated to ensure the policy and associated programs remain impactful, relevant, and aligned with the Company's long-term vision.
