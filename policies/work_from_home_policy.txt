12. WORK FROM HOME POLICY

12.1 Purpose and Organizational Philosophy
12.1.1 Why This Policy Exists
— The Foundation of Remote Work Excellence. The Work From Home Policy is one of the most strategically important documents in the Company's entire policy framework. It establishes the principles, standards, and comprehensive frameworks through which employees may work remotely — ensuring that remote work arrangements are effective, productive, secure, and aligned with organizational objectives while providing employees with flexibility and work-life balance. Where the Employment Policy referenced as HR-EP-001 governs the terms and conditions of employment, and the Data Security Policy referenced as HR-DSP-001 governs how information is protected, this policy governs how work is performed outside of traditional office environments. Remote work is not merely a convenience or benefit. It is a strategic work arrangement that requires clear standards, effective management, and mutual commitment to success, and this policy is the framework through which that arrangement is managed.
— The Organizational Imperative. The Company operates in a business environment where remote work has become increasingly important for attracting and retaining talent, maintaining operational resilience, and providing flexibility that supports employee well-being and productivity. Research consistently demonstrates that well-managed remote work arrangements can enhance productivity, improve work-life balance, and increase employee satisfaction, while poorly managed remote work can lead to isolation, performance issues, and security risks. This policy exists to ensure that the Company maintains effective remote work arrangements that balance flexibility with accountability, that protect organizational interests while supporting employee needs, and that enable remote work success through clear standards and comprehensive support. The Company recognizes that remote work is a strategic capability that requires careful management and clear frameworks.
— The Human Imperative. This policy has been written with a genuine recognition that remote work can significantly impact employees' work experience, work-life balance, productivity, and well-being. Employees who work remotely need clear expectations, adequate support, appropriate technology, and effective communication to succeed. Remote work can provide flexibility and autonomy that enhances job satisfaction, but it can also create challenges including isolation, work-life boundary issues, and technology frustrations. This policy is not a declaration of the Company's willingness to allow remote work. It is a recognition of the importance of remote work arrangements and a commitment to ensuring that remote work is managed effectively, that employees receive the support they need to succeed, and that remote work arrangements benefit both employees and the organization.
— The Philosophy of Flexibility and Accountability. Effective remote work requires a balance between flexibility and accountability — flexibility that enables employees to work effectively in their chosen environment, and accountability that ensures performance, security, and compliance standards are maintained. This policy is built on the conviction that remote work can be highly effective when it is managed properly, when expectations are clear, when support is adequate, and when both employees and managers are committed to remote work success. Remote work is most effective when it provides flexibility while maintaining accountability, and this policy ensures that balance is achieved.
— A Living and Evolving Document. Like all enterprise policies, the Work From Home Policy is a living document that will be reviewed, updated, and strengthened as remote work practices evolve, as technology advances, as applicable laws and regulations change, and as practical experience provides insights into how the policy can be made more effective. Every employee who works remotely, every manager who manages remote teams, and every HR professional who administers remote work arrangements is expected to contribute to the continuous improvement of remote work practices, ensuring that the policy remains relevant, effective, and supportive of both individual and organizational success.

12.2 Scope and Applicability
12.2.1 Who This Policy Applies To
— Universal Application to Eligible Employees. This policy applies to all employees whose job responsibilities can be reasonably and effectively performed from a remote location, subject to eligibility criteria and approval processes. The Company recognizes that not all roles are suitable for remote work and that eligibility must be determined based on role requirements, business needs, and other relevant factors. The specific application of policy provisions may vary based on work arrangement type, role requirements, and business needs, but the fundamental framework of remote work standards applies to all employees working remotely.
— All Categories of Remote Work. This policy covers permanent remote work arrangements, hybrid arrangements combining office and remote work, temporary remote work arrangements, and any other remote work configuration approved by the Company. Each remote work arrangement, regardless of type or duration, is subject to the standards and requirements established in this policy. The Company ensures that all remote work arrangements are managed consistently and that all remote employees understand their responsibilities and expectations.
12.2.2 Where This Policy Applies
— All Remote Work Locations. This policy applies to all locations where remote work is performed, including employees' homes, co-working spaces, client locations, or any other approved remote work location. The Company recognizes that remote work can be performed in various locations and ensures that policy standards apply regardless of the specific remote work location. Employees are responsible for ensuring that their remote work location meets policy requirements for security, safety, and productivity.
— Geographic Considerations. This policy applies to remote work arrangements within India and may be adapted for international remote work arrangements based on applicable laws and business requirements. The Company ensures that remote work arrangements comply with applicable laws in the jurisdiction where work is performed and that policy standards are adapted as needed for different geographic contexts.
12.2.3 Relationship With Other Policies
— An Integrated Remote Work Framework. This Work From Home Policy operates as one component of the Company's broader human resources and employment framework. It must be read and applied in conjunction with the Employment Policy referenced as HR-EP-001, the Data Security Policy referenced as HR-DSP-001, the Information Security Policy referenced as HR-ISP-001, the Attendance Policy referenced as HR-AP-001, the Leave Policy referenced as HR-LP-001, the Performance Management Policy referenced as HR-PMP-001, the Code of Conduct referenced as HR-COC-001, and all other applicable Company policies. Where a specific policy provides more detailed guidance on a particular aspect of remote work — such as the Data Security Policy's treatment of remote data protection — that specific policy governs. Where a remote work concern arises that falls outside the scope of any specific policy, this Work From Home Policy provides the governing framework.
— Conflict Resolution. In the event of any apparent conflict between the standards established in this policy and those established in any other Company policy, employees must seek guidance from HR before taking action. Employees must not act unilaterally on their own interpretation of any apparent conflict between policies and must not use an apparent conflict as justification for departing from remote work standards that would otherwise apply.

12.3 Policy Statement and Principles
12.3.1 Work From Home as Discretionary Arrangement
— Company Discretion. Work From Home is a flexible work arrangement offered at the sole discretion of the Company and does not constitute a contractual right or guaranteed benefit. The Company reserves the right to approve, modify, suspend, or terminate remote work arrangements based on business needs, performance considerations, security requirements, or other legitimate business reasons. Remote work is a privilege that is granted based on role suitability, individual performance, and business needs, not an entitlement that employees can demand. The Company ensures that remote work decisions are made fairly and consistently, but maintains discretion to manage remote work arrangements in the best interests of the organization.
— No Guarantee of Continuation. Approval to work remotely may be withdrawn, modified, or suspended at any time due to operational requirements, compliance obligations, performance concerns, or security risks. Employees approved for remote work should understand that arrangements are subject to change and that the Company may require return to office work when business needs require it. The Company ensures that changes to remote work arrangements are communicated clearly and that employees are given reasonable notice when possible, but maintains the right to make changes as needed.
12.3.2 Standards and Expectations
— Equal Standards for Remote Employees. Employees approved for Work From Home are expected to maintain the same standards of professionalism, ethical conduct, productivity, and policy compliance as those working from Company offices. Remote work does not reduce performance expectations, policy compliance requirements, or professional standards. The Company ensures that remote employees understand that they are held to the same standards as office-based employees and that remote work is not a reduction in expectations or responsibilities.
— Performance and Productivity Standards. Remote employees are held to the same performance expectations, productivity standards, quality benchmarks, and delivery timelines as office-based employees. Performance assessments are based on work output, adherence to deadlines, responsiveness, collaboration, and achievement of agreed objectives. The Company ensures that performance standards are clear, that they are applied consistently, and that remote employees understand how their performance will be evaluated.

12.4 Eligibility Criteria and Role Suitability
12.4.1 Eligibility Factors
— Role Nature and Requirements. Eligibility for Work From Home is determined based on multiple factors, including the nature of the role, whether the role can be performed effectively remotely, data sensitivity requirements, customer interaction needs, regulatory obligations, and business requirements. The Company evaluates each role individually to determine whether remote work is suitable and whether the role can be performed effectively in a remote environment. Role suitability is the primary factor in determining remote work eligibility.
— Individual Performance and Capability. Individual eligibility factors include performance history, attendance record, demonstrated ability to work independently with minimal supervision, communication skills, self-discipline, and track record of meeting deadlines and deliverables. The Company evaluates individual employees to determine whether they have the capability and track record to succeed in a remote work environment. Individual performance and capability are important factors in remote work eligibility.
— Probationary Period Requirements. Employees are generally required to have completed their probationary period before being considered for Work From Home, unless otherwise approved by management due to exceptional circumstances or business continuity requirements. Probationary completion ensures that employees have demonstrated their capability and commitment before remote work arrangements are considered. The Company may make exceptions for exceptional circumstances but generally requires probationary completion for remote work eligibility.
12.4.2 Role Eligibility Designations
— Ineligible Roles. The Company reserves the right to designate certain roles as ineligible for WFH based on operational, regulatory, or security considerations. Some roles may require physical presence, direct customer interaction, access to secure facilities, or other requirements that make remote work unsuitable. The Company ensures that role eligibility designations are clear, that they are based on legitimate business reasons, and that employees understand why certain roles are not eligible for remote work.
— Conditional Eligibility. Some roles may be eligible for remote work with specific conditions, such as required office presence on certain days, mandatory in-person meetings, or other requirements that limit remote work flexibility. Conditional eligibility enables remote work while ensuring that business needs are met. The Company ensures that conditional eligibility requirements are clear and that they are communicated to employees when remote work arrangements are approved.

12.5 Types of Work From Home Arrangements
12.5.1 Permanent Remote Work
— Full-Time Remote Arrangements. Permanent remote work arrangements allow employees to work remotely on a full-time basis, with no regular requirement for office presence. Permanent remote work provides maximum flexibility and is suitable for roles that can be performed entirely remotely. The Company ensures that permanent remote work arrangements are approved only when role requirements and business needs support full-time remote work.
— Permanent Remote Work Considerations. Permanent remote work arrangements require careful consideration of role requirements, team collaboration needs, employee capability, and business continuity. The Company evaluates permanent remote work requests thoroughly and ensures that permanent arrangements are sustainable and effective. Permanent remote work may be subject to periodic review to ensure continued suitability.
12.5.2 Hybrid Work Arrangements
— Office and Remote Combination. Hybrid arrangements combine office and remote work, typically requiring employees to work from the office on certain days and remotely on other days. Hybrid arrangements provide flexibility while maintaining office presence and team collaboration. The Company ensures that hybrid arrangements are structured to meet business needs while providing flexibility to employees.
— Hybrid Schedule Structures. Hybrid schedules may be structured in various ways, including fixed days in office, flexible office presence requirements, or project-based office presence. Hybrid schedule structures are designed to balance flexibility with business needs. The Company ensures that hybrid schedules are clear, that they are communicated effectively, and that they support both employee flexibility and business requirements.
12.5.3 Temporary Remote Work Arrangements
— Short-Term Remote Work. Temporary remote work arrangements are approved for specific periods, such as during personal circumstances, health situations, or temporary business needs. Temporary arrangements provide flexibility for specific situations while maintaining the option to return to office work. The Company ensures that temporary arrangements are approved for legitimate reasons and that they are managed appropriately.
— Emergency and Business Continuity Remote Work. The Company may implement temporary remote work arrangements during emergencies, business continuity situations, or other circumstances that require remote work. Emergency remote work ensures business continuity while protecting employee health and safety. The Company ensures that emergency remote work arrangements are implemented effectively and that employees receive necessary support.

12.6 Request and Approval Process
12.6.1 Request Submission Requirements
— Formal Request Process. Employees seeking to work from home must submit a formal request to their reporting manager, outlining the proposed work arrangement, duration, work schedule, anticipated impact on job responsibilities, collaboration, and deliverables, and any other relevant information. Formal requests ensure that remote work decisions are made with complete information and that arrangements are properly documented. The Company ensures that request processes are clear and that employees understand what information is required.
— Request Documentation. Request documentation should include the proposed arrangement type, requested schedule, work location details, technology and connectivity information, and business justification. Comprehensive documentation helps managers evaluate requests effectively and ensures that arrangements are well-planned. The Company ensures that request documentation requirements are clear and that employees provide complete information.
12.6.2 Approval Process and Criteria
— Managerial and HR Review. All requests are subject to managerial and HR review and approval, ensuring that requests are evaluated fairly and that arrangements are approved only when appropriate. Managerial review considers role suitability, business needs, and team impact, while HR review ensures policy compliance and consistency. The Company ensures that approval processes are thorough and that decisions are made fairly and consistently.
— Approval Criteria. Approval decisions are based on role suitability, individual eligibility, business needs, team impact, and policy compliance. Approval criteria ensure that remote work arrangements are approved only when they support both employee needs and business objectives. The Company ensures that approval criteria are clear and that they are applied consistently.
12.6.3 Approval Conditions and Requirements
— Specific Conditions. Approval may be granted with specific conditions related to availability, reporting, communication, performance tracking, security requirements, or other factors. Conditions ensure that remote work arrangements are managed effectively and that business needs are met. The Company ensures that approval conditions are clear, that they are communicated to employees, and that compliance with conditions is monitored.
— Failure to Comply. Failure to comply with approved conditions may result in modification or revocation of WFH privileges. The Company takes compliance seriously and ensures that remote work arrangements are managed effectively. Employees are expected to comply with all approval conditions and to understand that non-compliance may result in loss of remote work privileges.

12.7 Working Hours, Scheduling, and Availability
12.7.1 Standard Working Hours
— Adherence to Assigned Hours. Employees working from home are required to adhere to their assigned working hours in accordance with Company policy and applicable Shops and Establishments Act provisions. Remote work does not eliminate working hour requirements, and employees are expected to work their full assigned hours. The Company ensures that working hour requirements are clear and that remote employees understand their schedule obligations.
— Core Business Hours. Employees must remain accessible and responsive during designated core business hours (typically 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM IST), ensuring that collaboration and communication can occur effectively. Core hours ensure that teams can collaborate and that business needs are met. The Company ensures that core hours are communicated clearly and that employees understand availability expectations.
12.7.2 Schedule Flexibility
— Flexible Scheduling Within Core Hours. Employees may have flexibility in their work schedule outside of core business hours, subject to manager approval and business needs. Flexible scheduling provides work-life balance while ensuring that business needs are met. The Company ensures that flexible scheduling is managed appropriately and that it supports both employee needs and business requirements.
— Schedule Communication. Any anticipated deviation from working hours or availability must be communicated to the reporting manager in advance. Schedule communication ensures that managers are aware of employee availability and that business needs can be planned accordingly. The Company expects employees to communicate schedule changes proactively and to ensure that business needs are not disrupted.
12.7.3 Overtime and Additional Hours
— Overtime Approval. Overtime, where applicable, must be pre-approved and will be governed by applicable labour laws. Overtime approval ensures that additional hours are authorized and that employees are compensated appropriately. The Company ensures that overtime policies are followed and that remote employees understand overtime requirements and approval processes.

12.8 Attendance and Time Management
12.8.1 Attendance Requirements
— Attendance Obligations. Employees working remotely must comply with Company attendance and time-tracking requirements, including accurately recording working hours through designated systems. Work From Home does not eliminate attendance obligations, and employees are expected to maintain accurate attendance records. The Company ensures that attendance requirements are clear and that remote employees understand their attendance obligations.
— Time Tracking Systems. Employees must use Company-approved time tracking systems to record their working hours accurately. Time tracking ensures that attendance is recorded and that working hours are documented. The Company ensures that time tracking systems are accessible and that employees understand how to use them effectively.
12.8.2 Attendance Compliance
— Accurate Time Reporting. Employees are responsible for accurate time reporting and must not misrepresent their working hours or attendance. Accurate reporting is essential for payroll, compliance, and performance management. The Company ensures that time reporting requirements are clear and that employees understand the importance of accurate reporting.
— Consequences of Misuse. Misuse of WFH arrangements or inaccurate time reporting may result in disciplinary action. The Company takes attendance compliance seriously and ensures that violations are addressed appropriately. Employees are expected to maintain accurate attendance records and to understand that violations may result in disciplinary action.

12.9 Performance Management and Accountability
12.9.1 Performance Expectations
— Equal Performance Standards. Employees working from home are held to the same performance expectations, productivity standards, quality benchmarks, and delivery timelines as office-based employees. Remote work does not reduce performance expectations, and employees are expected to meet the same standards regardless of work location. The Company ensures that performance standards are clear and that they are applied consistently to all employees.
— Performance Assessment Criteria. Performance assessments are based on work output, adherence to deadlines, responsiveness, collaboration, and achievement of agreed objectives. Performance criteria ensure that remote employees are evaluated fairly and that performance is measured objectively. The Company ensures that performance criteria are clear and that they are communicated to employees.
12.9.2 Performance Support and Development
— Feedback and Coaching. Where performance issues are identified, the Company may provide feedback, coaching, or support to help employees improve. Performance support ensures that remote employees receive the guidance they need to succeed. The Company is committed to helping remote employees succeed and provides support when performance concerns are identified.
— Performance Improvement. Continued failure to meet performance expectations may result in corrective action, including the withdrawal of WFH privileges or further disciplinary measures. Performance improvement processes ensure that performance issues are addressed and that employees have opportunities to improve. The Company ensures that performance improvement processes are fair and that they provide employees with opportunities to succeed.

12.10 Communication and Collaboration Expectations
12.10.1 Communication Standards
— Regular Communication Requirements. Consistent and effective communication is critical for successful remote work. Employees are expected to maintain regular communication with managers, team members, and stakeholders using Company-approved communication platforms. Regular communication ensures that teams stay connected and that business needs are met. The Company ensures that communication expectations are clear and that employees understand their communication obligations.
— Professional Communication. Employees must ensure professional conduct in all communications, including email, instant messaging, video calls, and other communication channels. Professional communication maintains organizational standards and ensures effective collaboration. The Company expects professional communication at all times and ensures that communication standards are maintained.
12.10.2 Collaboration and Participation
— Mandatory Participation. Participation in virtual meetings, training sessions, performance reviews, and team discussions is mandatory unless otherwise approved. Participation ensures that remote employees remain engaged and that team collaboration is maintained. The Company ensures that participation requirements are clear and that employees understand their obligations.
— Proactive Updates. Employees must remain proactive in sharing updates, progress, and challenges with managers and team members. Proactive communication ensures that managers are informed and that issues are addressed promptly. The Company expects employees to communicate proactively and to keep managers and teams informed of their work and any challenges they face.

12.11 Data Security, Confidentiality, and Privacy
12.11.1 Data Security Requirements
— Strict Policy Adherence. Employees working from home must strictly adhere to the Company's Data Security Policy referenced as HR-DSP-001 and Information Security Policy referenced as HR-ISP-001, and comply with applicable data protection laws including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act). Data security is critical in remote work environments, and employees are expected to maintain the same security standards as office-based employees. The Company ensures that data security requirements are clear and that remote employees understand their security obligations.
— Authorized Systems and Networks. Access to Company data must be limited to authorized systems, secure networks, and approved devices. Unauthorized access or use of unapproved systems creates security risks and violates policy. The Company ensures that authorized systems are clearly identified and that employees understand which systems and networks they may use.
12.11.2 Confidentiality and Privacy Protection
— Preventing Unauthorized Access. Employees are responsible for preventing unauthorized access to confidential information in the remote work environment. This includes securing work areas, protecting devices, and ensuring that confidential information is not accessible to unauthorized individuals. The Company ensures that confidentiality requirements are clear and that employees understand their responsibilities for protecting confidential information.
— Privacy Considerations. Employees must respect privacy requirements and ensure that their remote work environment does not compromise the privacy of Company information or the privacy of colleagues and clients. Privacy protection is essential in remote work environments. The Company ensures that privacy requirements are understood and that employees take appropriate measures to protect privacy.
12.11.3 Security Incident Reporting
— Immediate Reporting. Any actual or suspected data breach, loss of equipment, or security incident must be reported immediately to the Company's IT Security function or HR. Immediate reporting enables rapid response and minimizes potential damage. The Company ensures that reporting procedures are clear and that employees understand their obligation to report security incidents promptly.
— Incident Response. Security incidents are investigated and addressed promptly, with appropriate action taken to mitigate risks and prevent recurrence. Incident response ensures that security issues are resolved and that lessons are learned. The Company ensures that incident response is effective and that security is maintained.

12.12 Equipment, Technology, and Company Assets
12.12.1 Company-Provided Equipment
— Equipment Provision. The Company may provide laptops, access credentials, or other tools necessary for remote work. Equipment provision ensures that remote employees have the technology they need to work effectively. The Company ensures that equipment is provided when needed and that it meets security and functionality requirements.
— Equipment Ownership and Use. All Company-provided equipment remains Company property and must be used solely for official purposes. Employees are responsible for the proper use, maintenance, and protection of Company assets. The Company ensures that equipment use requirements are clear and that employees understand their responsibilities for Company equipment.
12.12.2 Employee Technology Requirements
— Reliable Internet Connectivity. Employees are responsible for maintaining reliable internet connectivity and a functional remote work setup. Reliable connectivity is essential for effective remote work. The Company expects employees to ensure that their technology setup supports effective remote work.
— Technology Standards. Remote work technology must meet Company standards for security, functionality, and performance. Technology standards ensure that remote work is secure and effective. The Company ensures that technology standards are clear and that employees understand what is required.
12.12.3 Equipment Return and Recovery
— Return Upon Request or Separation. Employees must return Company equipment upon request or separation from employment. Equipment return ensures that Company assets are recovered and that security is maintained. The Company ensures that return procedures are clear and that employees understand their obligations to return equipment.
— Equipment Security. Employees are responsible for securing Company equipment and preventing loss, theft, or damage. Equipment security protects Company assets and ensures that work can continue effectively. The Company expects employees to take appropriate measures to secure Company equipment.

12.13 Home Work Environment, Health, and Safety
12.13.1 Work Environment Requirements
— Safe and Suitable Environment. Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe, secure, and ergonomically suitable work environment at home. A suitable work environment supports productivity, health, and safety. The Company ensures that work environment requirements are clear and that employees understand what is needed for an effective remote work environment.
— Ergonomic Considerations. Employees should ensure that their work setup supports good ergonomics, including appropriate desk height, monitor positioning, and seating. Ergonomic considerations help prevent injury and support long-term health. The Company provides guidance on ergonomic setup and expects employees to create ergonomically appropriate work environments.
12.13.2 Health and Safety Practices
— Basic Safety Practices. While working remotely, employees must follow basic health and safety practices to reduce the risk of injury or illness. Safety practices protect employee health and ensure that work can continue effectively. The Company ensures that safety guidance is provided and that employees understand their responsibilities for maintaining a safe work environment.
— Work-Related Injury Reporting. Any work-related injury or incident occurring during approved working hours must be reported promptly in accordance with Company procedures and applicable Indian labour laws. Injury reporting ensures that employees receive appropriate support and that the Company can address safety concerns. The Company ensures that reporting procedures are clear and that employees understand their obligations to report work-related injuries.

12.14 Leave, Breaks, and Unavailability
12.14.1 Leave Policy Application
— Leave Requirements. Employees working from home remain subject to the Company's Leave Policy referenced as HR-LP-001 and Attendance Policy referenced as HR-AP-001. Work From Home does not substitute for approved leave, and employees must apply for leave when they are unable to work. The Company ensures that leave requirements are clear and that remote employees understand that they must take leave when unable to work.
— Leave Application Process. Employees must apply for leave if they are unwell, unavailable, or unable to perform work duties during scheduled working hours. Leave application ensures that absences are properly documented and that business needs are managed. The Company ensures that leave application processes are clear and that remote employees understand their leave obligations.
12.14.2 Breaks and Rest Periods
— Break Requirements. Employees are entitled to breaks and rest periods in accordance with Company policy and applicable labour laws. Break requirements ensure that employees have appropriate rest and that labour law requirements are met. The Company ensures that break requirements are communicated and that employees understand their break entitlements.
— Break Documentation. Break periods should be documented appropriately in time tracking systems to ensure compliance with labour law requirements. Break documentation ensures that labour law requirements are met and that employees receive appropriate rest. The Company ensures that break documentation requirements are clear.

12.15.1 Monitoring Rights and Practices
— Company Monitoring Rights. The Company reserves the right to monitor work output, system access, and usage of Company resources to ensure compliance with performance expectations, security standards, and applicable laws. Monitoring ensures that remote work is effective, secure, and compliant. The Company ensures that monitoring is conducted lawfully, reasonably, and proportionately.
— Lawful and Reasonable Monitoring. Monitoring activities will be conducted in a lawful, reasonable, and proportionate manner, respecting employee privacy while ensuring compliance and security. The Company ensures that monitoring practices are appropriate and that they balance business needs with employee privacy rights.
12.15.2 Compliance and Audit
— Regular Compliance Reviews. The Company conducts regular compliance reviews to ensure that remote work arrangements comply with policies, legal requirements, and business standards. Compliance reviews help identify issues and ensure that remote work is managed effectively. The Company ensures that compliance reviews are thorough and that they result in improvements when needed.
— Audit Procedures. Remote work arrangements may be subject to audit to verify compliance with policies, security requirements, and performance standards. Audits ensure that remote work is effective and compliant. The Company ensures that audit procedures are fair and that they support effective remote work management.

12.16.1 Company Rights to Modify or Terminate
— Modification Rights. The Company may modify, suspend, or terminate Work From Home arrangements at any time based on business needs, legal or regulatory requirements, performance concerns, or security risks. The Company maintains discretion to manage remote work arrangements in the best interests of the organization. The Company ensures that modifications are communicated clearly and that employees are given reasonable notice when possible.
— Termination Circumstances. Remote work arrangements may be terminated for various reasons, including business needs, performance issues, security concerns, policy violations, or other legitimate business reasons. Termination ensures that remote work arrangements remain aligned with business needs and that issues are addressed appropriately. The Company ensures that termination decisions are fair and that they are communicated clearly to employees.
12.16.2 Employee Request for Changes
— Change Request Process. Employees may request changes to their WFH arrangement by providing reasonable notice and following established request procedures. Change requests enable employees to adjust their remote work arrangements as their needs change. The Company ensures that change request processes are clear and that requests are evaluated fairly.
— Change Approval. Change requests are subject to the same approval processes as initial requests, ensuring that changes are evaluated appropriately and that business needs are considered. The Company ensures that change approvals are fair and that they balance employee needs with business requirements.

12.18.1 Responsibility for Policy Compliance

The Company's commitment to maintaining a current, comprehensive, and genuinely effective Work From Home Policy is both a strategic imperative and a fundamental expression of its commitment to flexible work arrangements that support employee well-being, productivity, and organizational success.
