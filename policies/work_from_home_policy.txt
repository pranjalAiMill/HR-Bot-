POLICY 6: WORK FROM HOME / HYBRID WORKING POLICY

1. PURPOSE
This Work From Home and Hybrid Working Policy establishes the organization's framework for flexible and remote working arrangements. The organization recognizes that flexible working can support employee wellbeing, productivity, and work-life balance, while also enabling the organization to attract and retain exceptional talent. This policy sets out the conditions, responsibilities, and safeguards that must be in place for remote and hybrid working to be effective, secure, and equitable.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all employees who have completed their probationary period and whose role is assessed as suitable for remote or hybrid working. Certain roles — due to their operational nature, security classification, or client-facing requirements — may not be eligible for remote working. Eligibility determinations are made by HR in consultation with the relevant line manager.

3. DEFINITIONS
- Remote Working (Full): An arrangement in which the employee works exclusively from a location other than the organization's premises for the duration of their contracted hours, on a permanent or fixed-term basis, as formally agreed with the organization.
- Hybrid Working: An arrangement in which the employee works from both the organization's premises and a remote location (typically their home) on a scheduled or flexible basis.
- Office-First: A model in which the organization's premises are the primary place of work, and remote working is permitted on a limited basis (up to two days per week) at the manager's discretion without formal agreement.
- Occasional Remote Working: Ad-hoc working from home for specific, short-term purposes (e.g., receiving a delivery, a non-infectious illness) which does not require a formal agreement but does require manager pre-approval.
- Primary Remote Location: The designated home address or other approved location from which the employee primarily performs their duties under a formal remote or hybrid arrangement.

4. ELIGIBILITY
Eligibility for remote or hybrid working is assessed on the basis of the following criteria:
- The employee has successfully completed their probationary period.
- The employee's role can be performed effectively and securely from a remote location.
- The employee has demonstrated the self-discipline, communication skills, and technological capability to work independently.
- The employee's performance rating is at least 3 (Meets Expectations) in the most recent appraisal cycle.
- The remote working location meets the minimum health, safety, and security requirements set out in this policy.
- The arrangement does not adversely impact the performance of the employee's team or department.
- Employees on a formal Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) may have their remote working privileges suspended at the discretion of the HR Business Partner.

5. TYPES OF ARRANGEMENTS AND OFFICE ATTENDANCE
5.1 Standard Hybrid Model
Unless otherwise specified in a formal remote working agreement, the organization's standard hybrid model requires employees to be present at the office a minimum of three (3) days per week, with up to two (2) days worked remotely.
5.2 Enhanced Remote Model
An enhanced remote arrangement (greater than two (2) remote days per week) requires a formal Remote Working Agreement, signed by the employee, their manager, and HR. This arrangement is reviewed annually.
5.3 Fully Remote Arrangement
Fully remote arrangements are approved only in exceptional circumstances, where the employee's role is wholly independent of the physical office, and are subject to VP-HR and CEO-level approval. Fully remote employees are expected to attend the office for scheduled all-hands meetings, team events, appraisals, and other key occasions as directed by management, with reasonable advance notice.
5.4 Office Attendance Requirements
Regardless of the agreed remote working arrangement, employees may be required to attend the office on specific days for:
- Team meetings, workshops, or collaboration sessions.
- Client visits or presentations.
- New employee onboarding or mentoring.
- Disciplinary, grievance, or HR meetings.
- Any other business-critical activity designated by management.
Refusing reasonable requests to attend the office when required may result in disciplinary action.

6. APPLICATION PROCESS
Step 1 — Eligibility Check: The employee confirms their eligibility by reviewing the criteria in Section 4 and completing a Remote Working Self-Assessment Form (available on the intranet).
Step 2 — Workspace Assessment: The employee submits a Home Workspace Checklist confirming that their remote location meets the health, safety, and security standards set out in Section 9.
Step 3 — Manager Discussion: The employee discusses their request with their line manager, who assesses the operational impact and individual suitability.
Step 4 — HR Review: The line manager submits the approved request to HR, who confirms eligibility and prepares the Remote Working Agreement.
Step 5 — Formal Agreement: The Remote Working Agreement is signed by the employee, manager, and HR, and a copy is retained on the employee's HR file.
Step 6 — IT Setup: IT Security provisions any required equipment, access, and VPN credentials for the remote location.
Requests may be declined where they do not meet the eligibility criteria or where the business case for the arrangement is not met. Declined requests may be appealed through the standard grievance process.

7. REMOTE WORKING AGREEMENT
All formal remote working arrangements are governed by a signed Remote Working Agreement, which includes:
- The approved type of arrangement (hybrid, enhanced remote, or fully remote).
- The scheduled office attendance days (for hybrid workers).
- The primary remote working location address.
- The applicable period (typically twelve months, subject to annual renewal).
- The conditions under which the arrangement may be modified or terminated.
- The employee's acknowledgement of their obligations under this policy, the Data Security Policy, and the Health and Safety requirements.
The organization reserves the right to modify or terminate a remote working arrangement at any time for legitimate business reasons, including (but not limited to) changes in business needs, performance concerns, security incidents, or changes to the employee's role. A minimum of four (4) weeks' written notice will be provided unless the termination of the arrangement is due to a disciplinary matter.

8. CORE HOURS AND AVAILABILITY
8.1 Core Hours
All employees — regardless of their working arrangement — must be fully available and responsive during core hours of 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM local time on all contracted working days.
8.2 Availability Standards
During working hours, remote employees must be:
- Available on corporate communication channels (email, Microsoft Teams, or the organization's designated collaboration platform) and responsive within thirty (30) minutes.
- Able to join video calls at short notice, with camera on during meetings as a default, unless technical limitations prevent this.
- Reachable by phone during core hours.
8.3 Boundaries
The organization is committed to protecting the mental health and wellbeing of its employees. Managers must not contact remote employees routinely outside of contracted working hours. The organization encourages all employees to establish and maintain clear boundaries between work and personal time when working remotely.

9. HEALTH, SAFETY & WORKSPACE REQUIREMENTS
9.1 General Obligation
The organization's duty of care for employee health and safety extends to all locations where employees perform their duties, including home offices. Employees are responsible for ensuring their remote workspace meets the following standards:
- The workspace is clean, adequately lit, well-ventilated, and free from trip hazards.
- The employee has a suitable, ergonomic chair and desk setup that minimizes the risk of repetitive strain injury and musculoskeletal issues.
- All electrical equipment used for work purposes is in good working order and properly earthed.
- The workspace is sufficiently private to prevent unauthorized individuals (including household members) from viewing, hearing, or accessing confidential business information.
- The workspace is free from distractions that would prevent the employee from maintaining professional standards during client calls and meetings.

9.2 Display Screen Equipment (DSE) Assessment
Employees with a formal remote working agreement are entitled to request a DSE (Display Screen Equipment) assessment of their home workspace. HR will arrange for this assessment upon request.
9.3 Reporting Accidents
Any accident, injury, or near-miss that occurs while the employee is working from their approved remote location must be reported to their line manager and the Health and Safety team immediately, in accordance with the organization's Accident Reporting Procedure.

10. CORPORATE ASSETS — REMOTE USE
10.1 Provision of Equipment
The organization will provide the following standard corporate equipment to employees with an approved remote working arrangement:
- Laptop or desktop computer (pre-configured with all required software and security tools).
- Corporate mobile phone or software-based mobile solution (where required for the role).
- Headset for calls and video conferencing.
- Access to the corporate VPN.
- Licensed access to all required software applications.
Additional equipment (external monitors, keyboards, docking stations) may be provided at the manager's discretion based on role requirements and within the budget approved by the department head.
10.2 Personal Equipment
Use of personal devices to access corporate systems is governed by the BYOD provisions of the Data Security Policy (HR-POL-002). Personal devices must be enrolled in the MDM platform and approved by IT Security before any corporate access is permitted.
10.3 Responsibilities for Corporate Equipment
Employees using corporate equipment at their remote location are responsible for:
- Keeping equipment secure, clean, and in good working order.
- Not allowing any unauthorized individuals to use corporate equipment.
- Reporting any damage, loss, or theft to IT Security and their line manager within one (1) hour of discovery.
- Not transporting equipment between locations without taking appropriate security precautions.
- Returning all equipment to the organization within five (5) working days of the termination of their employment or remote working arrangement, whichever is earlier.
The organization may seek to recover the cost of damaged or unreturned equipment from the employee's final salary payment where applicable law permits.
10.4 Expenses
The organization will reimburse reasonable and pre-approved expenses associated with remote working, which may include:
- A one-time home office setup allowance (up to an approved maximum), upon commencement of a formal remote working arrangement.
- A monthly internet connectivity contribution (where the employee does not already receive a data allowance as part of their package).
Expenses must be submitted through the standard expenses process with appropriate receipts and manager approval. Tax implications of home office expense reimbursements are the responsibility of the employee to manage in accordance with applicable local tax law.

11. DATA SECURITY REQUIREMENTS FOR REMOTE WORK
Employees working remotely must comply in full with the Data Security Policy (HR-POL-002). In addition, the following specific requirements apply to remote work:
- Corporate VPN must be active at all times when accessing corporate systems from any location other than a secured corporate office.
- Confidential documents or materials must not be printed at the home workspace unless a secure, cross-cut home shredder is available.
- Computer screens must not be visible to household members or visitors during confidential calls or work sessions.
- Clear desk practice applies to the home workspace. Confidential documents must be securely stored when not in use.
- Video calls conducted from home must be conducted in a room or environment where confidential business information cannot be overheard by unauthorized individuals.
- Use of smart speakers (Amazon Echo, Google Home, Apple HomePod) in the room used for corporate calls or work is prohibited due to the risk of unintended audio capture.

12. WELLBEING AND SOCIAL INCLUSION
The organization recognizes that remote working, if not managed thoughtfully, can lead to feelings of isolation, disconnection, and burnout. To support the wellbeing of remote and hybrid workers, the organization commits to:
- Maintaining regular, structured communication between managers and their remote team members.
- Including remote workers equally in team meetings, decision-making processes, and social events (whether virtual or in-person).
- Providing access to the Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) for confidential personal support.
- Conducting an annual remote worker wellbeing survey and acting on its findings.
- Ensuring remote workers have equitable access to learning, development, and career advancement opportunities.
- Encouraging all employees — remote or otherwise — to take proper breaks, disconnect outside of working hours, and prioritize their mental health.

13. MONITORING AND PERFORMANCE
The organization does not use invasive monitoring tools (such as keystroke logging, screenshot capture, or real-time screen monitoring) to monitor remote worker activity. Productivity is assessed through the same performance management framework (HR-POL-005) that applies to all employees, based on outcomes, goal achievement, and quality of work — not time spent at a keyboard.
Managers are expected to manage their remote teams through trust, clear goal-setting, and regular communication — not surveillance.

14. POLICY COMPLIANCE AND BREACH
Breach of this policy — including working from an unapproved location, unauthorized use of corporate equipment, failure to comply with security requirements, or misrepresentation of remote working compliance — may result in the revocation of the remote working arrangement and, in serious cases, disciplinary action up to and including termination.
