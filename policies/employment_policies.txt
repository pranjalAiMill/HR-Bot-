3. EMPLOYMENT POLICIES

3.1 Equal Employment Opportunity and Non-Discrimination

Objective:
To ensure a fair, inclusive, and diverse workplace free from discrimination and bias.

Scope:
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, applicants, and third parties interacting with the organization.

Policy Statement:
The Company is committed to providing equal employment opportunities to all individuals regardless of race, color, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, national origin, disability, marital status, pregnancy, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by applicable law.

Procedures:
- Recruitment and Selection: All hiring decisions will be based solely on qualifications, skills, experience, and merit.
- Promotions and Transfers: Career advancement opportunities will be provided equitably based on performance and capability.
- Compensation: Pay structures will be fair and equitable, determined by role, experience, and performance without discriminatory bias.
- Training and Development: All employees will have equal access to professional development opportunities.
- Reporting Mechanism: Employees who believe they have experienced discrimination should report concerns to Human Resources or their immediate supervisor.
- Investigation: All complaints will be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and confidentially.
- Non-Retaliation: The Company prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports discrimination or participates in an investigation.


3.2 Employment Classification and Status

Objective:
To clearly define employee categories and their associated rights and benefits.

Scope:
Applies to all individuals providing services to the Company.

Policy Statement:
Employees are classified based on their work arrangement, hours, and duration of employment. Each classification determines eligibility for benefits and protections.

Classifications:
1. Full-Time Employees
- Work a minimum of 40 hours per week (or as defined by local regulations)
- Eligible for all company benefits including health insurance, paid leave, and retirement plans
- Entitled to performance reviews and career development opportunities

2. Part-Time Employees
- Work less than 40 hours per week on a regular basis
- Eligible for prorated benefits as per company policy and legal requirements
- May include health benefits, paid leave (prorated), and other applicable benefits

3. Temporary/Contract Employees
- Hired for a specific project or defined period
- Terms of employment outlined in individual contracts
- Generally not eligible for standard employee benefits unless specified in contract
- May be converted to permanent status based on business needs and performance

4. Intern/Trainee
- Enrolled in educational programs or recent graduates gaining work experience
- Duration typically ranges from 3-12 months
- May receive stipend or salary as per agreement
- Limited benefits as outlined in internship agreement

5. Consultant/Freelancer
- Independent contractors providing specialized services
- Governed by service agreements, not employment contracts
- Responsible for their own taxes and benefits
- Not eligible for employee benefits

Procedures:
- Employment classification will be communicated in the offer letter and employment contract.
- Any change in classification must be documented and communicated in writing.
- Employees may request clarification of their status from Human Resources at any time.


3.3 Probationary Period

Objective:
To assess employee suitability and allow mutual evaluation of the employment relationship.

Scope:
Applies to all new hires and employees promoted or transferred to new roles.

Policy Statement:
All new employees and those transitioning to significantly different roles will serve a probationary period during which their performance, conduct, and cultural fit will be evaluated.

Duration:
- New Employees: 90 days (3 months) from the date of joining
- Promoted/Transferred Employees: 60 days (2 months) in the new role
- Extensions may be granted in exceptional circumstances for up to an additional 90 days

Procedures:
- Orientation: New employees will receive comprehensive onboarding during the first week, including introduction to company policies, culture, and job responsibilities.
- Performance Expectations: Clear objectives and performance standards will be communicated at the start of the probationary period.
- Regular Feedback: Supervisors will conduct informal check-ins at 30 and 60 days to provide feedback and address concerns.
- Formal Review: A formal evaluation will be conducted before the end of the probationary period.
- Outcomes:
  a. Successful Completion: Employee becomes a permanent member with full benefits and protections
  b. Extension: Probation extended with specific improvement areas identified
  c. Termination: Employment may be terminated with minimal notice (as per local law) if performance or fit is unsatisfactory

- Notice Period During Probation:
  a. Employee: 2 weeks' notice or as per employment contract
  b. Employer: 2 weeks' notice or payment in lieu, or as per local regulations

- Benefits During Probation: Probationary employees are eligible for all statutory benefits and may receive limited company benefits as specified in their offer letter.

3.4 Background Verification and Pre-Employment Screening

Objective:
To ensure the safety, security, and integrity of the workplace by verifying candidate credentials and background.

Scope:
Applies to all candidates who receive conditional offers of employment.

Policy Statement:
The Company conducts background verification and pre-employment screening to validate information provided by candidates and to ensure they meet the organization's standards for employment.

Components of Verification:
- Identity Verification: Government-issued identification documents.
- Educational Qualifications: Degrees, diplomas, and certifications from accredited institutions.
- Employment History: Verification of previous employment, job titles, and dates of service (typically last 3-5 years or 2-3 employers).
- Professional References: Contact with provided references to assess candidate suitability.
- Criminal Background Check: Screening for criminal history as permitted by law and relevant to the position.
- Credit Check: For positions involving financial responsibilities (with candidate consent).
- Drug Screening: For safety-sensitive positions or as required by law.
- Social Media Review: Professional online presence review (public information only).

Procedures:
- Conditional Offer: Employment offers are conditional upon successful completion of background verification.
- Candidate Consent: Written authorization will be obtained from candidates before conducting background checks.
- Third-Party Vendors: The Company may use reputable third-party agencies to conduct verifications.
- Confidentiality: All information obtained will be kept strictly confidential and used only for employment decisions.
- Adverse Action: If information is discovered that may result in withdrawal of the offer, candidates will be:
    a. Informed of the findings
    b. Provided with a copy of the report
    c. Given opportunity to dispute or explain discrepancies
    d. Allowed reasonable time to respond (typically 5 business days)
- Record Retention: Verification records will be maintained securely and retained as per legal requirements.
- Falsification: Providing false information or failing to disclose required information may result in offer withdrawal or termination of employment.
